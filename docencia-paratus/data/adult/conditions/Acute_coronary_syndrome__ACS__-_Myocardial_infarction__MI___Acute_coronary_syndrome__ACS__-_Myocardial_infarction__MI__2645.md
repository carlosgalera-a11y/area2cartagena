## Occlusion myocardial infarction (OMI or STEMI+)
  ### Occlusion myocardial infarction ECG patterns
    **Occlusion myocardial infarction ECG patterns**
      Classic STEMI:New ST elevation in 2 contiguous ECG leads Men: ≥2mm leads V2-V3 and ≥1mm in other leads (or ≥2.5mm if <40 years old) Women: ≥1.5mm leads V2-V3 and ≥1mm in other leads
      Ischemic ST depression max V1-V4 identifies posterior OMI with 97% specificity
      Hyperacute T waves (large relative to QRS, look inflated with large area under the curve)
      de Winter T waves (ST-segment depression rising into hyperacute T waves)
      Wellens waves (reperfusion T-wave inversion) identifies spontaneous reperfusion with risk of reocclusion
      South African flag pattern (ST elevation in noncontiguous leads V2 and aVL, with inferior reciprocal ST depression) identifies 1st diagonal occlusion
      Aslanger pattern (ST elevation in III with reciprocal ST depression I/aVL, ST in V1>V2 and ST depression V5-V6) indicates inferior OMI with concomitant critical stenosis
      Smith-Modified Sgarbossa criteria (any lead with concordant ST elevetion, concordant ST depression V1-V3, or discordant ST elevation/S >25%) identifies OMI in LBBB or ventricular paced rhythm

## Non-occlusion myocardial infarction (NSTEMI)
  ### Antiplatelet therapy
    Aspirin (ASA) loading dose: 320 mg PO
    Consider dual antiplatelet therapy with P2Y12 antagonist (e.g. ticagrelor, clopidogrel)
  ### Anticoagulation
    [DRUG] Heparin (Bolus) — Acute coronary syndrome and atrial fibrillation
    [DRUG] Heparin (Infusion) — Acute coronary syndrome and atrial fibrillation
    [DRUG] Enoxaparin (1mg/kg SC BID) — Non-occlusion acute myocardial infarction and atrial fibrillation
