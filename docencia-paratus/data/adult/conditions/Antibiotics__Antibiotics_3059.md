## Principle of antibiotic administration
  ### Order of antibiotic administration
    Draw blood cultures promptly, if indicated, before administering antibiotics
    In the treatment of severe infections, broad-spectrum antibiotics should be administered within 60 minutes of being prescribed.
    Order of antibiotic administration in cases of severe infections for which multiple antibiotics are prescribed 1- Ertapenem, meropenem, piperacillin–tazobactam, ceftolozane–tazobactam, cefepime, cefotaxime, ceftazidime, ceftobiprole, ceftriaxone 2- Moxifloxacin, levofloxacin, ciprofloxacin 3- Cefoxitin, cefuroxime, cefazolin, trimethoprim–sulfamethoxazole (TMP/SMX) 4- Amikacin, tobramycin, colistimethate, tigecycline 5- Penicillin G, ampicillin, cloxacillin, clindamycin, azithromycin 6- Gentamicin (in synergy), linezolid, vancomycin 7- Daptomycin 8- Metronidazole 9- Antivirals (acyclovir, ganciclovir) 10- Antifungals (liposomal amphotericin B, caspofungin, fluconazole, posaconazole, voriconazole)
