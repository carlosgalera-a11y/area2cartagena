## Unknown cause of bleeding or other
  ### Intermittent boluses
    [DRUG] Pantoprazole (Loading dose) — Upper GI bleeding (bolus strategy)
    [DRUG] Pantoprazole (Maintenance dose) — Upper GI bleeding (bolus strategy)
  ### Bolus - infusion
    [DRUG] Pantoprazole (Bolus) — Upper GI bleeding (bolus - infusion)
    [DRUG] Pantoprazole (Infusion) — Upper GI bleeding (bolus - infusion)
    Endoscopy should be performed within 24 hours. Consider earlier in the unstable patient
    If endoscopy fails, consider embolization or surgery, depending on local ressources CT angiography is often done prior to embolization but may be omitted in the unstable patient or when the source of bleeding is known
## Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis prevention for cirrhotics
    [DRUG] Ceftriaxone (N/A) — Severe infection
## Anticoagulation reversal
    Do not correct an elevated INR if elevation solely due to cirrhosis
    Most patients taking antiplatelet agents should NOT receive platelet transfusion in the absence of thrombocytopenia (< 50 x 10(9)/L)
    [PROCEDURE] Anticoagulation reversal — Anticoagulation reversal
