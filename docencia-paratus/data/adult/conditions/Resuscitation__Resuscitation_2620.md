## Cardiac arrest - advanced therapies
    **Treat the cause**
      Thrombosis (myocardial infarction) Thromboembolism (pulmonary embolism) Tension pneumothorax Tamponade Trauma (commotio cordis) Toxins (overdose) Hypoxia Hypovolemia Hydrogen (Acidosis) Hyper or Hypokalemia Hypothermia Hypoglycemia
    **Assessing for reversible causes**
      ABCD-Ultrasound A: Airway (supraglottic or endotracheal to ensure adequate oxygenation and ventilation) and Access (IV or IO) B : Blood gas (shock gas): pH, K, Ca, Glucose C : Core temperature (bladder or esophageal) D : Defibrillator for rhythm analysis Ultrasound : Assess for tamponade, RV dilation and strain for massive PE, Bilateral lung sliding or B lines to rule out tension pneumothorax, ultrasound pulse checks
