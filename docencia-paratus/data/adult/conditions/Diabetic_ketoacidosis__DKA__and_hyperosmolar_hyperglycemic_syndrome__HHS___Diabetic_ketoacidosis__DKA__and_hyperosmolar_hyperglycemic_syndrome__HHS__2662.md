# Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) and hyperosmolar hyperglycemic syndrome (HHS) — Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) and hyperosmolar hyperglycemic syndrome (HHS)

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Metabolic
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2662


## Tab: Default
## General measures
    Search for and treat precipitating factors: infection, myocardial infarction, pancreatitis, etc.
    Treatment needs to be adjusted in patients with advanced or terminal kidney disease
## IV fluids
  ### Initial repletion
  ### Initial maintenance when glycemia ≥ 14 mmol/L (250 mg/dL)
    Crystalloids, balanced solution preferably
    If balanced solution unavailable or incompatible and corrected Na ≤ 140 mmol/L: use NaCl 0.9 %
    If balanced solution unavailable or incompatible and corrected Na > 140 mmol/L: use NaCl 0.45 %
  ### If pH < 6.9 AND component of non-anion gap metabolic acidosis
    Consider an isotonic sodium bicarbonate infusion
## Potassium
    Measure and correct serum potassium at least every 4 hours Suggested doses are a starting point and should be tailored to patient response
  ### Serum potassium ≥ 5.3 mmol/L
    Do not administer potassium and stop all sources of potassium
  ### Serum potassium between 3.3 and 5.2 mmol/L: administer 20 mmol of potassium
    Potassium chloride (KCl) PO
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (10 mmol in 100 mL) — Hypokalemia
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (20 mmol in 100 mL) — Hypokalemia
  ### Serum potassium ≤ 3.2 mmol/L: administer 40 mmol of potassium and if started, HOLD INSULIN
    Potassium chloride (KCl) PO
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (10 mmol in 100 mL) — Hypokalemia
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (20 mmol in 100 mL) — Hypokalemia
    Consider adding potassium to maintenance IV fluids
## Insulin
  ### Start insulin only if serum potassium > 3.2mmol/L
    [DRUG] Insulin regular (Bolus (optional)) — Diabetic ketoacidosis and hyperglycemic hyperosmolar syndrome
    [DRUG] Insulin regular (Drip) — Diabetic ketoacidosis and hyperglycemic hyperosmolar syndrome
    Consider continuing patient's basal insulin
## When glycemia < 14 mmol/L (250 mg/dL)
  ### Add dextrose to IV fluids
    D5-NaCl 0.45% or D5-LR
  ### Aim for glycemia 10-14 mmol/L (180-250 mg/dL)
    Consider adding a D10% infusion titrated to target glycemia and/or decreasing insulin drip rate to 0.05 units/kg/h
## Resolution
  ### pH > 7.30, serum bicarbonate > 15-18 mmol/L, anion gap < 12-15 mmol/L, glycemia < 14 mmol/L (250 mg/dL), normal neurologic status and osmolality < 315 mOsm/kg
    Transition to subcutaneous insulin then stop fixed rate IV insulin or replace fixed dose insulin with a regular titratable IV insulin protocol
