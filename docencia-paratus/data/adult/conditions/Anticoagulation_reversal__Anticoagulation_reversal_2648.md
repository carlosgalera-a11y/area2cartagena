# Anticoagulation reversal — Anticoagulation reversal

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Trauma
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2648


## Tab: Default
## Anti-Xa agents (apixaban, edoxaban, rivaroxaban, fondaparinux)
  ### Agent taken within previous 24 hours
    [DRUG] Prothrombin complex concentrate (4-factor) (N/A) — DOAC (Xa inhibitor) reversal in patients with acute major bleeding
  ### Consider if apixaban or rivaroxaban taken < 6 hours, edoxaban taken < 2 hours
    [DRUG] Activated charcoal (aqueous) (N/A) — Toxicology
  ### If elevated INR, consider
    [DRUG] Vitamin K (Syringe driver) — Hemorrhage, patient on vitamin K antagonist
    [DRUG] Vitamin K (Volumetric pump) — Hemorrhage, patient on vitamin K antagonist
## Dabigatran
  ### If taken in previous 24 hours (2-4 days if renal failure) 
and/or Thrombin Time (TT) prolonged
    [DRUG] Idarucizumab (N/A) — Life-threatening bleeding with use of Dabigatran
  ### Consider if dabigatran taken < 2 hours
    [DRUG] Activated charcoal (aqueous) (N/A) — Toxicology
  ### Consider if idarucizumab unavailable
    [DRUG] Prothrombin complex concentrate (4-factor) (N/A) — DOAC (Xa inhibitor) reversal in patients with acute major bleeding
    Hemodialysis
## Vitamin K antagonists/Elevated INR
    [DRUG] Vitamin K (Syringe driver) — Hemorrhage, patient on vitamin K antagonist
    [DRUG] Vitamin K (Volumetric pump) — Hemorrhage, patient on vitamin K antagonist
  ### PCC: fixed dose or unknown INR
    [DRUG] Prothrombin complex concentrate (4-factor) (Fixed dose) — Vitamin K antagonist (VKA) reversal in patients with acute major bleeding
  ### PCC: INR-based dosing
    [DRUG] Prothrombin complex concentrate (4-factor) (INR 2-4) — Vitamin K antagonist (VKA) reversal in patients with acute major bleeding
    [DRUG] Prothrombin complex concentrate (4-factor) (INR 4-6) — Vitamin K antagonist (VKA) reversal in patients with acute major bleeding
    [DRUG] Prothrombin complex concentrate (4-factor) (INR > 6) — Vitamin K antagonist (VKA) reversal in patients with acute major bleeding
  ### Prothromin Complex Concentrate (PCC) contraindicated or unavailable
    [DRUG] Fresh Frozen Plasma (N/A) — Vitamin K antagonist (VKA) reversal in patients with acute major bleeding
## Unfractionated heparin
    Discontinue infusion if ongoing
    [DRUG] Protamine (N/A) — Bleeding with unfractionated heparin
## Low molecular weight heparins (LMWH)
  ### Enoxaparin
    [DRUG] Protamine (Last dose <8h) — Bleeding with enoxaparine
    [DRUG] Protamine (Last dose >8h and <12h) — Bleeding with enoxaparine
    [DRUG] Protamine (Unknown dose/time) — Bleeding with enoxaparine
    Protamine unlikely to be beneficial if > 12 hours after last dose
  ### Tinzaparin, Dalteparin, Nadroparin
    [DRUG] Protamine (Last dose <8h) — Bleeding with tinzaparin, dalteparin, nadroparin
    [DRUG] Protamine (Last dose >8h and <12h) — Bleeding with tinzaparin, dalteparin, nadroparin
    [DRUG] Protamine (Unknown dose/time) — Bleeding with tinzaparin, dalteparin, nadroparin
    Protamine unlikely to be beneficial if > 12 hours after last dose
## Antiplatelet agents
  ### May consider if life-threatening bleed or urgent neurosurgery
    Platelet transfusion
## Thrombolysis
    Stop thrombolysis if still ongoing
    Cryoprecipitate
    [DRUG] Tranexamic acid (N/A) — Hemorrhage (miscellaneous)
