# Glucagon (Glucagen® or Baqsimi®)
- Drug ID: 139
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Anaphylaxis in patient using beta-blocker
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  Administer without dilution
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation: 1 mg vial.
1) Reconstitute each vial with the included diluent.
2) Administer the dose of 5 mg IV over 5 minutes.

More rapid administration may induce vomiting.

Due to the short half-life of glucagon (approximately 6.6 minutes), an infusion is often necessary following the bolus.
      particularity: Should only be used for epinephrine refractory anaphylaxis in patients on B-blockers.

Doses of 1 to 5 mg are recommended for the bolus in the literature. Considering the use reserved for severe refractory cases, it seems appropriate to attempt the maximum dose from the outset.

  - STEP: Infusion
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation: 1 mg vial.
1) Reconstitute each vial with the included diluent.
2) Add 5mg (5 vials = 5mL) to a 50mL bag of D5%.
3) Final concentration = 0.09mg/mL.
4) Start the infusion at 5mcg/min; titrate according to clinical response.
      particularity: In poisoning, doses up to 83.3mcg/min (5mg/h) have been used.

Keep refrigerated.

Stability in 0.9% NaCl is controversial.

## Indicación: Beta-blocker toxicity
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      concentration note: ***Expert opinion recommended***
      administration: Presentation : Powder (1mg/vial)
1) Reconstitute with 1mL of the included diluent or 1mL of D5%
2) Final concentration: 1mg/mL
3) Draw the required dose
4) Administrer IV over 5min
5) Repeat q15 min as needed if effective, until initiation of other therapies
      particularity: Discuss with poison control center
Due to the side effects and the complexity of preparation and administration, glucagon should not be a first line agent for treatment of BB of CCB toxicity. Use for cases refractory to first-line treatments or while waiting for a clinical response to first line agents.

There is no evidence for the use of a glucagon infusion.

Keep refrigerated.

Stability in NaCl 0.9% is controversial.

## Indicación: Hypoglycemia (subcutaneous or IM)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [Administer subcutaneously or IM]
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Administer only in severe hypoglycemia and if IV dextrose is not available or in absence of IV access. Consider IO access to administer dextrose depending on the clinical situation, as glucagon takes at least 10-15 min to act.

Can be given IV at a dose of 1mg, but if the IV route is available, dextrose should be given instead.
      particularity: A 2nd dose can be repeated after 15 minutes PRN.

May cause nausea and vomiting. Administering PO carbohydrates might reduce this side effect.
