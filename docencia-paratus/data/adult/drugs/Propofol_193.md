# Propofol (Diprivan®)
- Drug ID: 193
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Intubation - induction agent​
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 2

  - STEP:   [IV push]
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: Administer IV push
      particularity: Monitor for hypotension. In hemodynamically unstable patients, significantly reduce the dose or choose another agent with less hypotensive effect. Consider the co-administration of vasopressors.

There is no consensus in the literature on the maximum dose of propofol. The maximum doses are an editorial choice, based on the fact that propofol is usually dosed with ideal weight.

## Indicación: Procedural sedation
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 2

  - STEP:   [IV over 1-2min]
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: An initial dose of 1 mg/kg is more effective for procedural amnesia.
Subsequent doses of 0.5 mg/kg q3-5min PRN. Adjust according to patient physiology.
      particularity: Monitor for hypotension and consider dose adjustment according to age and hemodynamic status. Elderly and/or unstable patients can be sedated at doses as low as 0.1-0.25mg/kg.

There is no consensus in the literature on the maximum dose of propofol. The maximum dose of 80mg is an editorial choice, based on the fact that propofol is usually dosed with ideal weight.

## Indicación: Refractory status epilepticus, post-intubation
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 2

  - STEP: mcg/kg/min
      concentration: 10 mg/mL (PE)
      administration: Presentation: 10mg/mL (50 or 100mL vial)
No preparation required

1) Start the infusion at a rate ranging from 50 to 80mcg/kg/min
2) Titrate until control of seizures, ideally with continuous EEG. (Expert opinion)
      particularity: Monitor for hypotension.

There is no consensus in the literature on the maximum dose of propofol. The dose of 100mcg/kg/min is an editorial choice. Some sources recommend doses up to 200mcg/kg/min in status epilepticus. Doses above 80mcg/kg/min for more than 48h increase the risk of PRIS.
