- Drug ID: 89
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Occlusion myocardial infarction
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Step 1  [IV over 1-2min]
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      concentration note: Make sure there are no contraindications
      administration: Reconstitution: 100mg vial
-100 mg vial of alteplase
-100 mL vial of Sterile Water for Injection (SWFI)
- Transfer device

Refer to the monograph for detailed reconstitution
1) Mix the alteplase vial with the diluent with the help of the transfer device provided. DO NOT shake the bottle.
2) Mix the solution with a gentle swirl to make sure the product is completely dissolved.
3) Visually inspect the solution for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration.
4) Final concentration: 1 mg/mL

Reconstitution: 50mg vial
- 50 mg vial of alteplase
- 50 mL vial of Sterile Water for Injection (SWFI)
- 60 mL syringe
- Large bore needle (18 gauge) 

Refer to the monograph for detailed reconstitution

2) Mix the solution with a gentle swirl to make sure the product is completely dissolved.
3) Visually inspect the solution for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration.

5) Withdraw 50mg (50mL) with a syringe.

Reconstitution : 50mg vial
- 50 mg vial of alteplase
- 50 mL vial of Sterile Water for Injection (SWFI)
- 60 mL syringe
- Large bore needle (18 gauge)

4) Final concentration: 1mg/mL
      particularity: A second dose of 50mg can be repeated as needed (about 15 minutes later) to complete the total dose at 100mg. There is no scientific consensus on the dosage of alteplase in cardiac arrest. The suggested dose is an editorial decision based on all the available evidence.

Reconstitution : 50mg vial
- 50 mg vial of alteplase
- 50 mL vial of Sterile Water for Injection (SWFI)
- 60 mL syringe
- Large bore needle (18 gauge) 

Refer to the monograph for detailed reconstitution
1) Using the syringe and the large needle, take 50 mL of the SWFI. Inject the SWFI into the alteplase vial.
2) Mix the solution with a gentle swirl to make sure the product is completely dissolved.
3) Visually inspect the solution for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration.
4) Final concentration: 1mg/mL
      particularity: Withhold heparin before beginning alteplase administration. Heparin can be restarted when the activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT) value is less than 1.5-2 times normal.

Consider administering a bolus of 10-20mg IV push if the patient is very unstable.
The rest of the dose can then be administered IV over 2 hours.

Some studies suggest that the 50mg dose is as effective as the 100mg dose. Consider reducing the dose in patients with lower weight (<60kg) or at higher risk of bleeding. A dose of 25mg over 6h can also be considered for patients at high risk of bleeding and/or with less instability as it might be as effective according to some studies.
