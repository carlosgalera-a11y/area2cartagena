# D10% (Dextrose 10%)
- Drug ID: 122
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Adjuvant to high dose IV insulin in toxicology
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Infusion
      concentration: 0.1 g/mL
      concentration note: D10% bag
      administration: Administer a bolus of D10% of 250mL (or 0.5g/kg of D50%) before starting the infusion.

Always administer with volumetric pump.
      particularity: Start only if blood sugar levels below 22mmol/L (400mg/dL) and aim for blood sugar levels above 5.5mmol/L (100mg/dL). Monitor blood glucose q30min up to a few hours after the end of the insulin infusion.

This concentration should be used via the peripheral route in the absence of central access. In the presence of central venous access, use an infusion of D50%. Considering the significant flow rate of the D10% solution, the transition to a more concentrated solution administered via central access should be done as quickly as possible.

In the hemodynamically unstable patient, low blood sugar should be treated by increasing the dextrose intake and not by decreasing the insulin dose.

## Indicación: Hypoglycemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 5-15 min]
      concentration: 0.1 g/mL
      concentration note: D10% bag
      administration: Administer IV direct under pressure over 5 minutes or with a volumetric pump over 15 minutes depending on the clinical situation. Follow with an infusion if clinically indicated.

For less urgent situations, consider lowering the dose to 125mL to reduce the incidence of rebound hypoglycemia.
      particularity: Equivalent dose of glucose (25g) to a 50mL ampoule of D50%.

Consider as an alternative to D50%:
- Less rebound hypoglycemia
- Less irritation at the site of injection
- Less risk in case of extravasation

Consider a dextrose infusion and frequent glycemia measurements to prevent rebound hypoglycemia.

## Indicación: Adjuvant to IV insulin in hyperkalemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 15-60 min]
      concentration: 0.1 g/mL
      concentration note: D10% bag
      administration: Equivalent dose of glucose (25g) to a 50mL ampoule of D50%.
      particularity: Administer dextrose concomitantly with IV insulin in hyperkalemia if blood glucose < 14 mmol/L (250mg/dL).

Consider a 2nd dose 30-60 minutes after insulin administration or an infusion of a dextrose-containing solution depending on blood sugar and risk of hypoglycemia (higher risk in CKD, smaller weight, older age, lower initial blood sugar).

Consider as an alternative to D50%:
- Less irritation at the injection site
- Less risk if extravasation
