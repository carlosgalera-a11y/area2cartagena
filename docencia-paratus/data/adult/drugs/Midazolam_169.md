# Midazolam (Versed®)
- Drug ID: 169
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Refractory status epilepticus, post-intubation
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 2

  - STEP: Infusion
      concentration: 5 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation: 5mg/mL (10mL vial)
1) Remove 20mL from a 100mL bag of NaCl 0.9%
2) Add 100mg (20mL) to the remaining 80mL of NaCl 0.9%
3) Final concentration : 1mg/mL
4) Start the drip at 0.1 or 0.2mg/kg/h or higher depending on the clinical picture
5) Titrate by 0.05-0.1mg/kg/hr q3-4h (Expert opinion)
6) Start at higher doses and titrate more quickly depending on the clinical situation

N.B. Increase starting dose and titrate faster depending on clinical response
      particularity: The use of continuous EEG monitoring is recommended.

Doses can be increased up to 2mg/kg/h with expert opinion.

- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   q 2 min PRN (max 5 mg total)
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Withdraw directly from vial without diluting.
Administer IV over 2min.
Repeat q2min PRN for a maximal cumulative dose of 5mg.
      particularity: The use of a cumulative dose greater than 5mg is associated with increased airway complications. However, higher doses might be required for some patients.

## Indicación: Post-intubation sedation
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 2

  - STEP: Bolus PRN  [IV over 2 min]  q 5-15 min PRN depending on level of acute agitation
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Caution: many concentrations available
Can be administered via pump with the infusion (see recipe for the infusion, final concentration = 1mg/mL) or directly with the 1mg/mL concentration.
      particularity: The SCCM recommends a non-benzodiazepine sedation option as first line for the intubated patient in the ICU.
Use the minimal required dose for adequate sedation. 
The use of a standardized tool for the evaluation of sedation depth in intubated patients is strongly recommended (ex: RASS).
Midazolam is usually dosed based on ideal body weight. Enter patient height for more precise and adequate dosing.
