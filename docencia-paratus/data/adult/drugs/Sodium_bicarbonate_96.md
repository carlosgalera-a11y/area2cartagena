# Sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO3)
- Drug ID: 96
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Urinary alkalization
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [Add 3 syringes of 50 mL of NaHCO3 to 1 L of D5%]
      concentration: 1 mL
      administration: Presentation: 1 mmol/mL (50 mL syringe)
1) Add 150 mmol (150 mL) to 1 L of D5%.
2) Do NOT remove volume during preparation.
3) Final concentration: 130 mmol/L.

Start infusion at 2-3 mL/kg/h (initial maximum 200 mL/h) and titrate according to urinary pH.
      particularity: Aim for urinary pH greater than 7.5.
Aim for serum pH 7.5-7.55.
Keep the serum potassium over 4 mmol/L.

## Indicación: Hyperkaliemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [Add 3 syringes of 50 mL of NaHCO3 to 1 L of D5%]
      concentration: 1 mL
      administration: Presentation: 1 mmol/mL (syringes of 50 mmol in 50 mL)
1) Add 150 mmol (3 syringes) to 1 L of D5%.
2) Do NOT remove volume during preparation.
3) Final concentration: 130 mmol/L.
4) Titrate the infusion acording to potassium level and volume status.
      particularity: This therapy may be effective only in the presence of metabolic acidosis and does not replace other therapies known to be effective in hyperkalemia (insulin, B2-agonists, calcium, etc.).

Some studies recommend the administration of 1 L of this solution over 4 hours (250 mL/h).

Hypertonic sodium bicarbonate therapy (non diluted ampules) does not seem to be effective in the treatment of hyperkalemia.

## Indicación: Sodium channel blocker toxicity
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 1 mmol/mL
      administration: Repeat until correction of QRS or hemodynamic stability: no maximum dose.

Maximum administration speed 1 mmol/kg/min.
      particularity: 1 meq = 1 mmol
Rinse with 0.9% NaCl before and after administration.
Repeat until correction of QRS or hemodynamic stability (no maximum dose).

Follow serum Na and avoid Na > 155 mmol/L.
