- Drug ID: 162
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Hypomagnesemia, severe and symptomatic
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 500 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation: 500mg/mL (10mL vial)
1) Add 1000mg (2mL) to 50mL of NaCl 0.9%.
2) Administer IV over 15 minutes.
3) Repeat dose if symptoms persist.
      particularity: Symptomatic hypomagnesemia definition: 
- Tetany
- Arrhythmias
- Seizures

After the initial dose of 1g or 2g, correct hypomagnesemia slowly.

## Indicación: Refractory torsade de pointes (drip)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 500 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation: 500mg/mL (10mL vial)
1) Add 25g (50mL) to 500mL of NaCl 0.9%.
2) Do NOT remove volume.
3) Final concentration: 45.5mg/mL.
4) Start at the desired rate.

Also compatible with D5% for administration.
Can be concentrated up to 20% mass/volume.
      particularity: Use only if normal renal function.

Aim for QTc less than 500msec, while keeping Mg levels less than 2-3mmol/L (5-7mg/dL).

Monitor closely for signs of magnesium toxicity: hyporeflexia, confusion, somnolence, etc. 
Antidote: calcium.

## Indicación: Hypomagnesemia, non-symptomatic
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Mg <0.70 and ≥0.55mmol/L
      concentration: 500 mg/mL
      administration: Slower administration allows a better absorption of magnesium. Recommended rate at 0.5g/h. Some sources recommend repleting at a rate of 0.25g/h.

Also compatible with D5% for administration.
Can be concentrated up to 20% mass/volume.
      particularity: If the enteral route is possible, favor PO repletion by administering 250mg of elemental magnesium PO or via NG tube QID x 4 doses.

Absolute values ​​are approximate. Rely on values ​​from your local laboratory or local protocols.
Dose for Mg <0.7mmol/L (1.7mg/dL) and ≥0.55mmol/L (1.3mg/dL).

Decrease dose and monitor closely for patients with CrCl less than 30mL/min.
