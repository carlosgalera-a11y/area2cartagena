# Insulin regular (Toronto® ou Humulin R®)
- Drug ID: 147
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Diabetic ketoacidosis and hyperglycemic hyperosmolar syndrome
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus (optional)
      concentration: 100 unités/mL
      administration: Ensure that K+ > 3.2mmol/L before initiating insulin.

Bolus dose: 0.1units/kg, maximum dose of 10 units.
      particularity: The administration of an insulin bolus is optional in the treatment of DKA and HHS as there is no evidence that it is associated with clinical benefit.

Keep refrigerated.

  - STEP: Drip
      concentration: 100 unités/mL
      administration: Ensure that K+> 3.2mmol/L before initiating insulin.

Insulin binds to IV tubing:
1) Fill the tubing with the solution and clamp for 15 minutes.
2) Rinse the tubing with the insulin solution for 1 minute before connecting it to the patient.
Step 1 can be omitted in emergency situations.
      particularity: This represents an initial regimen of insulin use for DKA and HHS. IV fluids and insulin titration should be done according to hospital policy or protocol.

If the infusion is not preceded by a bolus, some sources suggest an infusion of 0.14units/kg/hour to a maximum of 15units/h.

Consider decreasing the dose to 0,05units/kg/h when blood sugar falls below 14mmol/L (250mg/dL).

Consider continuing basal insulin to facilitate transition to SC insulin upon resolution of the acute episode.

Keep refrigerated.

## Indicación: Hyperkalemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV push]
      concentration: 100 unités/mL
      administration: Nil
      particularity: Prioritize the 5-unit dose in older patients or patients with advanced renal insufficiency: less hypoglycaemia and probably as effective as the 10-unit dose at lowering potassium.

Concomitantly administer dextrose if blood glucose lower than 14mmol/L (250mg/dL) and check blood glucose frequently after administration (at 15, 30, 60, 90, 120 minutes, than hourly). Post-insulin hypoglycaemia can occur up to 4-6 hours after administration (higher risk in CKD, smaller weight, older age, lower initial blood sugar). Consider an infusion of a dextrose-containing solution depending on the clinical situation.

Can be repeated 2-4h later if hyperkalemia persists despite other treatments.

Keep refrigerated.

## Indicación: CCB and BB intoxication (bolus)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 100 unités/mL
      administration: Administer IV over 1 minute.

Always start the infusion after the bolus.
      particularity: Contact your local toxicology center.

Administer with dextrose if blood glucose levels below 22mmol/L (400mg/dL).
Use D50 if central access available or D10 if peripheral access only.

Monitor potassium levels q2h, maintain around 3mmol/L.

## Indicación: CCB and BB intoxication (drip)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Concentration: 1 unit/mL
      concentration: 100 unités/mL
      administration: 1) Add 250units (2.5mL) of insulin to 250mL of 0.9% NaCl.
2) Rinse the tubing with the insulin solution for 1 minute before connecting it to the patient.
3) Start the infusion at 1unit/kg/h.
4) Increase by 0.5units/kg/h every 15 minutes depending on clinical response.
5) Titrate to maintain a systolic BP greater than 90mmHg and a pulse greater than 50/min.

When the flow rate exceeds 250mL/h, it is recommended to transition to the higher concentration preparation of insulin (16units/mL).

Doses up to 10units/kg h are reported in the literature.
      particularity: Contact your local toxicology center.

Administer with dextrose if blood glucose levels below 22mmol/L (400mg/dL).
Use D50 if central access available or D10 if peripheral access only.

Monitor potassium levels q2h, maintain around 3mmol/L.
