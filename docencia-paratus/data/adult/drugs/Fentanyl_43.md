2) Draw the desired dose and add 5mcg (0.1mL) to fill the dead space.
3) Connect the MAD-300 device to the syringe.
4) Divide the dose evenly between the two nostrils (possible volume of 0.2-1mL per nostril).
5) Administer over 1 to 2 seconds.
      particularity: Consider reducing the dose by 50% if:

- Pulmonary, cardiovascular or neurological comorbidities.
- Intoxication.
- Regular use of central nervous system depressants.

## Indicación: Premedication in RSI
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 2

  - STEP: N/A  [IV over 1-2min]
      concentration: 50 mcg/mL
      administration: Administer 3 to 5minutes before induction.
Administer IV undiluted over 1 to 2 minutes.
      particularity: No definitive evidence supports the use of fentanyl in rapid sequence intubation. May be used to reduce the sympathetic response to intubation in patients with increased intracranial pressure or in whom it is desired to avoid an increase in blood pressure.

May increase the risk of peri-intubation hypotension. Adjust the dose according to the clinical situation. Doses of 0.5 to 4 mcg/kg are described in the literature.

Protect ampoules from light.

  - STEP: Infusion
      concentration: 50 mcg/mL
      administration: Presentation: 50mcg/mL
1) Remove 50mL from a 250mL bag of NaCl 0.9%.
2) Add 2500mcg (50mL) of fentanyl to the remaining 200mL of NaCl 0.9%.
3) Final concentration: 10mcg/mL.
4) Start at desired rate (usually 0.35-1.5mcg/kg/h).
5) Titrate by increments of 0.25-0.5mcg/kg/h q30-60min as needed (t1/2 > 2 hours) (expert opinion).
6) IV boluses can be administered during the infusion in case of acute pain.
7) Although the maximum quoted dose is 3mcg/kg/h, doses up to 10mcg/kg/h are described in the literature.
      particularity: Use minimal required dose for adequate analgesia. The use of a standardized tool for pain evaluation in intubated patients is strongly recommended (ex: CPOT).
