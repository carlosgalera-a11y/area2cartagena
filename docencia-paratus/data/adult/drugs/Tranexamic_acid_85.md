## Indicación: Nebulization for hemoptysis
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Nebulization
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Do not dilute for faster administration.

Consider using a mouth piece rather than a mask to better direct the nebulization.
      particularity: The literature supports its use mainly for cases of hemoptysis.

Can be repeated every 8 hours as needed.

## Indicación: Post-partum hemorrhage
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Can be administered without dilution IV over 10 minutes with syringe driver.
      particularity: Consider in case of failure of medical treatment. The evidence does not demonstrate a reduction in global mortality, but rather a reduction in mortality secondary to bleeding.

Initial dose should be administered within 3 hours of birth. Initial administration beyond 3 hours does not confer any clinical benefit.

If bleeding persists after 30 minutes, or stops and restarts within 24 hours of the first dose, a second dose of 1g could be administered.

- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Can be administered without dilution IV over 10 minutes with syringe driver.
      particularity: Consider for patients with post-thrombolytic hemorrhage, perioperative bleeding, dental treatments in a patients with coagulopathy, hemoptysis or uterine bleeding, but also for patients with traumatic or postpartum bleeding (see separate entries).

Consider topical administration when possible (e.g. epistaxis, dental treatments).
Consider nebulization in patients with hemoptysis (see separate entry).

## Indicación: Angioedema, bradykinin-mediated
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Can be administered without dilution IV over 10 minutes with syringe driver.
      particularity: The efficacy of tranexamic acid remains controversial in angioedema because of the lack of definitive studies. However, tranexamic acid is safe, widely available and easy to administer which makes it a therapy to consider for bradykinin-mediated angioedema.

For hereditary angioedema cases, administer 1st line agents when possible (e.g. Berinert, icatibant).
