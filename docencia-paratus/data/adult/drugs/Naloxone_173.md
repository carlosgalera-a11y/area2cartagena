# Naloxone (Narcan®)
- Drug ID: 173
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Opioid poisoning (IV bolus)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Opioid naive  [IV push]  every 2 to 3 minutes PRN
      concentration: 0.4 mg/mL
      administration: Caution: multiple concentrations available.

Presentation 0.4 mg/mL:
Administer 0.1 mg (0.25 mL) to 0.4 mg (1 mL) IV push every 2 to 3 minutes PRN.
Doses can be increased and escalated depending on clinical response.

Presentation 1 mg/mL:
Administer 0.1 mg (0.1 mL) to 0.4 mg (0.4 mL) IV push every 2 to 3 minutes PRN.
Doses can be increased and escalated depending on clinical response.
      particularity: The primary survey (evaluation of the airway, the breathing and the circulation) should be prioritized over the administration of naloxone. 
Naloxone is not recommended for in-hospital use when patients are intubated or during cardiac arrest due to an opioid.
 
After 10 mg of naloxone without clinical response, consider an alternate diagnosis and endotracheal intubation. However, a dose higher than the maximum recommended dose may be required to reverse the effects of synthetic opioids.

## Indicación: Opioid poisoning (drip)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 0.4 mg/mL
      administration: Attention: multiple concentrations available.

Do NOT remove volume during preparation. 

1) Calculate the dose corresponding to 2/3 of the cumulative effective bolus dose.
2) Start the infusion to administer this calculated dose in 1 hour.
3) Stop the infusion according to the clinical response and the half-life of the narcotic.

Example:
The patient wakes up after 2 boluses of naloxone: 1st bolus 0.5 mg, 2nd bolus 1 mg.
1) Effective cumulative bolus dose = 0.5 + 1 = 1.5 mg.
2) 2/3 of this dose = 2/3 x 1.5 = 1 mg.
3) Start the infusion at 1 mg/h.
      particularity: An infusion can also be started empirically at 0.2-0.4 mg/h.
If respiratory depression reappears, a new loading dose of naloxone should be given and this dose repeated as needed, or continue the infusion.

The duration of the naloxone infusion should be dictated by the clinical picture, but also by the type of opioid involved, its dose and formulation (progressive release or not).
