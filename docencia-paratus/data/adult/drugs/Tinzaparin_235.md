      administration: There are few data to support the use of tinzaparin for patients over 139kg. Choose another molecule or administer a dose of 12,000 units +/- monitor anti-Xa levels or dose according to the actual body weight according to hemorrhagic risk and clinical situation.

Where possible, round to the nearest pre-prepared syringe dose:
2,500 units, 3,500 units, 4,500 units, 8,000 units, 10,000 units, 12,000 units, 14,000 units, 16,000 units, 18,000 units.
      particularity: Use with caution for patients with ClCr <30 mL/minute: the monograph recommends a dose reduction, but without defining this reduction.

The maximum dose of 12,000 units BID is an editorial choice, but supported by guidelines, since the obese population is poorly represented in the studies. Although some guidelines and organizations recommend dosing tinzaparin using total body weight and 175 units/kg daily with no upper limit, this may increase bleeding risk and potentially be of no benefit. The monograph recommends limiting the daily dose to 18,000 units. Dosing anti-Xa levels should be considered in order to ensure the effectiveness of the treatment. Alternatively, another drug may be used such as unfractionated heparin or a vitamin K antagonist or a DOAC, depending on the clinical situation.
