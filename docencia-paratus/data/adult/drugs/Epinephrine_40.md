- Type: drug

## Indicación: Cardiac arrest
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV push]  q3-5min
      concentration: 0.1 mg/mL
      concentration note: Caution: IV use in cardiac arrest
      administration: Indication: cardiac arrest
Use concentration 0.1mg/ mL
      particularity: In cardiac arrest secondary to VF/VT:
• Prioritize defibrillation over epinephrine.
• Consider waiting after the second defibrillation before giving the first dose of epinephrine.
• Consider a maximum dose of 3mg total in cases of refractory VF/VT.

## Indicación: Bradycardia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 2

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation: ampoules of 1ml (1mg/mL)
1) Add 4mg (4mL) or 8mg (8mL) to 250mL of NS
2) Do NOT remove volume
3) Final concentration : 15.7mcg/mL or 31mcg/mL
4) Start infusion at the minimal dose of 0.01mcg/kg/min
5) Titrate by increments of 0.01-0.03mcg/kg/min q3-5min as needed (t1/2 <5min) (expert opinion)

N.B. Increase starting dose and titrate faster depending on degree of instability.
      particularity: The minimal dose of 0.01mcg/kg/min was chosen to limit the chance of overdosing this medication in the obese population. Adjust according to hemodynamic response. If no response at doses greater than 0.1mcg/kg/min, evaluate the need for an alternative therapy.

Although the preferred route of administration of vasopressors is via central venous access, multiple studies demonstrate that administration via a good quality peripheral access is safe if used for a short period of time and with frequent verifications of the access site.

## Indicación: Shock
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 2

  - STEP: mcg/kg/min
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation: ampoules of 1mL (1mg/mL)
1) Add 4mg (4mL) or 8mg (8mL) or 16mg (16mL) or 32mg (32mL) to 250mL of NS
2) Do NOT remove volume
3) Final concentration: 15.7mcg/mL or 31mcg/mL or 60mcg/mL or 113mcg/mL
4) Start infusion at the minimal dose of 0.01mcg/kg/min
5) Titrate by increments of 0.01-0.03mcg/kg/min q3-5min as needed (t1/2 <5min) (expert Opinion)

N.B. Increase starting dose and titrate faster depending on degree of instability.
      particularity: The minimal dose of 0.01mcg/kg/min was chosen to limit the chance of overdosing this medication in the obese population. Adjust according to hemodynamic response.

There is no consensus in the literature on the maximum dose of epinephrine. The maximum dose is an editorial choice.

Although the preferred route of administration of vasopressors is via central venous access, multiple studies demonstrate that administration via a good quality peripheral access is safe if used for a short period of time and with frequent verifications of the access site.
For higher concentrations (16mg and 32mg in 250mL), use central venous access.

## Indicación: Push-dose pressor
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV push]  q 1-5 min PRN
      concentration: 10 mcg/mL
      administration: Presentation: cardiac epinephrine 0.1mg/mL (1mg in 10mL)
1) Withdraw 0.1mg (1mL) of epinerphrine
2) Add to 9ml of NaCl 0.9%
3) Final concentration: 0.01mg/mL = 10mcg/mL
4) Clearly identify syringes with the correct concentration
5) Administer 0.005-0.02mg = 5-20mcg (0.5-2mL) IV q1-5min PRN
      particularity: Little evidence supports the use of push-dose pressors.

Although the preferred route of administration of vasopressors is via central venous access, multiple studies demonstrate that administration via a good quality peripheral access is safe if used for a short period of time and with frequent verifications of the access site.

## Indicación: Refractory acute severe asthma
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Indication: IM refractory acute severe asthma
Use concentration 1mg / mL

IM in the thigh < 1 min
      particularity: GINA guidelines recommend the use of IM epinephrine in asthma when associated with anaphylaxis or angioedema. 
The literature tends to show that systemic epinephrine as a bronchodilator is not more effective than inhaled or nebulized bronchodilators. Use only in refractory cases.
