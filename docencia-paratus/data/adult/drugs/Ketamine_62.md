# Ketamine (Ketalar®)
- Drug ID: 62
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Analgesia (bolus as short infusion)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation : 10 or 50mg/mL
1) Add the calculated dose to 100mL of NaCl 0.9%
2) Do NOT remove volume
3) Administer IV over 15 min
      particularity: It is possible that a dose of 0.15mg/kg is non-inferior to 0.3mg/kg in terms of analgesia (Lovett 2021). 

To reduce side-effects, consider administering even more slowly (over 30 minutes).

- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: mcg/kg/min
      concentration: 50 mg/mL
      administration: Presentation: 50mg/mL (10mL vial)
1) Add 500mg (10mL) to 250mL of NaCl 0.9%
2) Do NOT remove volume
3) Final concentration 1.9mg/mL
4) Start infusion at 1mcg/kg/min
5) Titrate by 1mcg/kg/min q30min PRN until adequate pain control is achieved (expert opinion)
      particularity: Protect non-reconstituted vials from light.

Ketamine can accumulate in hepatic failure when used as a continuous infusion.

## Indicación: Severe agitation (IM)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IM in the thigh]
      concentration: 50 mg/mL
      administration: IM in the thigh.
Do not give more than 5mL per IM site.
If the dose is greater than 250mg (5mL), use two injection sites.
      particularity: Doses reported in the literature from 3 to 6mg/kg. Choose the dose according to the clinical situation, the co-administration of other molecules and the hemodynamic and respiratory status of the patient. A higher dose will result in faster and more effective sedation, but with the potential for more adverse effects.

Consider using IV or IM benzodiazepines as a first choice, depending on the clinical situation.

## Indicación: Procedural sedation
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 2-3 min]
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: Preferentially use 10mg/mL concentration.

IV push non-diluted over 2 to 3 minutes. Faster administration increases risk of apnea.
      particularity: Consider decreasing dose as low as 0.5mg/kg in the hemodynamically unstable patient and patients older than 65 years.

Subsequent doses of 0.25-0.5mg/kg can be administered in order to obtain desired level of sedation.

For delayed sequence intubation (DSI) or ketamine only breathing intubation (KOBI), administer more slowly over 30-60 seconds to prevent respiratory depression.
