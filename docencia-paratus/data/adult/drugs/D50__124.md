# D50% (Dextrose 50%)
- Drug ID: 124
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Adjuvant to high dose IV insulin in toxicology
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Infusion
      concentration: 0.5 g/mL
      administration: Although a D50% ampoule can be delivered with a syringe driver, an average patient will need an ampule approximately every 45 minutes. It is therefore preferable to use commercially available D50% bags or to have a more concentrated solution prepared by pharmacy. If this is not possible, another option is to inject several ampules of D50% into an empty bag and infuse at the same rate.
      particularity: Start only if blood sugar levels below 22mmol/L (400mg/dL) and aim for blood sugar levels above 5.5mmol/L (100mg/dL). Monitor blood glucose q30min up to a few hours after the end of the insulin infusion.

Administer a bolus of D50% of 0.5g/kg before starting the infusion.

This concentration should be used only via central access. In the absence of central venous access, use a D10% infusion. Consider obtaining central venous access as soon as possible since the administration of a D50% solution is preferable (smaller flow rate).

In the hemodynamically unstable patient, low blood sugar should be treated by increasing the dextrose intake and not by decreasing the insulin dose.

## Indicación: Hypoglycemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 2-3 min]
      concentration: 0.5 g/mL
      concentration note: D50% 50 mL vial
      administration: One ampule contains 25g of dextrose in 50mL volume.
      particularity: May be repeated as needed if hypoglycemia does not resolve.

Very high osmolarity: irritating to the veins and risk of tissue necrosis in the event of extravasation. Administer via peripheral access as proximal as possible or preferably central access. Otherwise, consider using D10%.

Consider a dextrose infusion and frequent glycemia measurements to prevent rebound hypoglycemia.

## Indicación: Adjuvant to IV insulin in hyperkalemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 2-3 min]
      concentration: 0.5 g/mL
      concentration note: D50% 50 mL vial
      administration: A commercial vial contains 25g of dextrose in 50mL of volume.
      particularity: Administer dextrose concomitantly with IV insulin in hyperkalemia if blood glucose < 14 mmol/L (250mg/dL).

Consider a 2nd dose 30-60 minutes after insulin administration or an infusion of a dextrose-containing solution depending on blood sugar and risk of hypoglycemia (higher risk in CKD, smaller weight, older age, lower initial blood sugar).

Very high osmolarity: irritating to veins and risk of tissue necrosis in the event of extravasation. Administer via peripheral access as proximal as possible or preferably central access. Otherwise, consider using D10%.
