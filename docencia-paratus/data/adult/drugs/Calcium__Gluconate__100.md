# Calcium (Gluconate) (CaGluc)
- Drug ID: 100
- Type: drug
- Entorno: adult

## Indicación: Cardiac Arrest
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 2-5 min]
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Administer undiluted IV over 2-5 minutes.
Administer preferably through a proximal IV access (hyperosmolar solution).
Rinse tubing after each injection using 2.5 - 30mL of NaCl 0.9% to reduce the risk of thrombophlebitis.
      particularity: Administer only if suspicion of a reversible cause by the administration of calcium. Empirical administration of calcium for all cases of cardiac arrest is not supported by the literature and may be associated with reduced survival.

Several sources prefer the use of calcium chloride as first-line therapy in cardiac arrest as for the same amount of elemental calcium administered, less vials are needed with calcium chloride.

## Indicación: Hypocalcemia, severe
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Administer preferably through a proximal IV access (hyperosmolar solution)

Rinse tubing after each injection using 2.5 - 30mL of NaCl 0.9% to reduce the risk of thrombophlebitis
      particularity: Severe hypocalcemia: total serum calcium < 1.9mmol/L (7.5mg/dL) and/or symptomatic patient.

In case of concomitant hypocalcemia and hyperphosphatemia, giving calcium may cause precipitation of calcium phosphate (calciphylaxis). The ideal treatment in this situation is treatment of the hyperphosphatemia.

## Indicación: Calcium channel blocker poisoning (bolus)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Administer undiluted IV over 5 minutes. Rinse the tubing after each injection with 2.5 - 30mL of NaCl 0.9% to reduce the risk of thrombophlebitis.

Repeat q10-20min PRN if favorable hemodynamic response (maximum 12g) or consider starting the infusion (discuss with your poison control center).
      particularity: The use of calcium gluconate is preferred in a stable patient with peripheral venous access.

Aim for serum calcium in the upper range of normal.

Avoid if co-intoxication with digoxin.

## Indicación: Calcium channel blocker poisoning (infusion)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 0.1 g/mL
      administration: Depending on the desired flow rate and the patient's weight, insert between 10g (100mL) and 40g (400mL) of calcium gluconate 10% solution in an empty solute bag (eg Viaflex) and administer on a volumetric pump at the indicated flow rate. If not available, insert 5g (50mL) of calcium gluconate 10% solution into an empty syringe and administer with a syringe pump at the indicated rate.

Discuss with your poison control center.
Titrate the infusion rate according to the effectiveness of the treatment on blood pressure and the serum ionized calcium concentration (do not exceed an ionized calcium of 1.5-2 times the upper limit of normal).
      particularity: Calcium chloride 10% and calcium gluconate 10% solutions are physically stable at least 48 hours at room temperature (25 ºC, 60% relative humidity) in a polypropylene syringe and in an empty intravenous solute bag that contains PVC and DEHP according to the results of an investigators lead study with the financial support of the Quebec Poison Centre and the Pharmacy department of the CIUSSSCN.

## Indicación: Massive hemorrhage
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Administer undiluted IV over 5 minutes. Administer preferably through a proximal IV access (hyperosmolar solution).
Rinse tubing after each injection using 2.5 - 30mL of NaCl 0.9% to reduce the risk of thrombophlebitis.
Do not administer in the same IV access as blood products.
      particularity: Most patients with massive hemorrhage develop hypocalcemia. Blood product administration exacerbates this hypocalcemia by dilution and chelation because of citrate. It is recommended to administer calcium empirically for these patients, ideally at every step of a major hemorrhage protocol.

Calcium chloride is preferred in hemorrhagic shock. 3g of calcium gluconate contains as much elemental calcium as 1g calcium chloride.
