# Non-invasive Ventilation (BiPAP - CPAP) — Non-invasive Ventilation (BiPAP - CPAP)

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2757


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Acute respiratory distress (pulmonary edema, ARDS, exacerbated COPD, atelectasis, etc.) Hypoxemic respiratory failure Hypercapnic respiratory failure Obstructive Sleep Apnea Syndrome
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Respiratory or cardiac arrest Severely alteratedlevel of consciousness (inability to protect the airway) Obstruction or severe trauma of the airways Active vomiting
  ### Relative
    Persistent vomiting or active upper gastrointestinal bleeding Significant facial trauma or recent airway surgery Pneumothorax or other barotrauma without chest tube Severe pulmonary hypertension or RV failure Agitated and uncooperative patient
## Equipment Preparation
    NIV device with adapted circuits (BiPAP / CPAP) Interface (oronasal or nasal mask) Support harness Antibacterial filter Oxygen Pulse oxymetry Capnograph (optional but recommended)
## Select the interface based on the type of circuit used
  ### Single-limb circuit WITH expiratory port
    
    Use a non-vented mask (without intentional leaks) + anti-asphyxiation valve
  ### Single-limb circuit WITHOUT expiratory port
    
    Use a ventilated mask (with intentional leaks) + anti-asphyxiation valve
  ### Double-lumen (including coaxial) circuit
    
    Use a non-vented mask (without intentional leaks) Masks with blue elbows are typically used for this type of circuit

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Select the interface suitable for the patient's face (appropriate size) Detach the clips of the two lower straps of the mask Connect and turn on the device Perform the required verification tests according to the device
## 2
    Comfortably position the patient in a semi-seated or seated position (according to tolerance) Place a pulse oximeter for continuous monitoring of oxygenation
## 3
  ### BiPAP Mode (e.g. COPD, pulmonary edema)
    Initial IPAP: 12 cmH₂O (10-15 cmH₂O) Initial EPAP: 5 cmH₂O (5-8 cmH₂O) Initial FiO₂: 100 %, adjust quickly according to target SpO₂ > 92 %
  ### CPAP mode (e.g. pulmonary edema)
    EPAP (PEEP): 8 cmH₂O (5-10 cmH₂O) Initial FiO₂: 100 %, adjust quickly according to target SpO₂ > 92 %
## 4
    
    Gently bring the mask towards the patient’s face, avoiding any sudden movements, and place the harness over the patient’s head Press the mask onto the face and secure the clips of the lower straps
## 5
    
    First, adjust the upper straps by gently tightening them (both sides simultaneously) Adjust the lower straps accordingly Avoid excessive pressure that causes discomfort and leaks
## 6
    Regularly monitor:  Check the tightness of the mask frequently and adjust the straps as needed. Gradually adjust IPAP/EPAP or CPAP pressure according to clinical effectiveness, patient comfort, and gasometry results if available
