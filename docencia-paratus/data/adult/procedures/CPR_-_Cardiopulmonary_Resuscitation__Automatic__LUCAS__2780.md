# CPR - Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation — Automatic (LUCAS®)

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Essentials
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2780


## Tab: Execution
## Indications
    Cardiac arrest, especially if: Likely prolonged need for CPR Need for transport Need to liberate team members or space for other tasks To facilitate interventions (e.g. ECMO cannulation, cath lab)
## Contraindications
    Impossible to put the LUCAS® safely on the patient (Too small, too large)
    Pregnancy Extreme obesity Thoracic deformity
## 1
    Install the defibrillation pads and check the rhythm BEFORE installing the LUCAS® Never delay the start of manual CPR
## 2
    Take out the device and the backplate
## 3
    Press and hold the "ON/OFF" button for 1 second to power on the LUCAS® device and initiate the self-test The device is ready for use when the green LED next to the ADJUST key will illuminate
## 4
    Brief pause in compressions (< 10 seconds) Place under the patient, aligned under the armpits Resume CPR

## 6
    Quickly attach the top part to the back plate ("click" to confirm) Minimize CPR interruptions If transport anticipated: prepare/secure neck/back straps
## 7
    
    Make sure that the LUCAS® is in the ADJUST mode Press the Suction Cup with two fingers until the pad touches the chest without compressing it

## 9

## Tab: Aftercare
## Continuous monitoring
    With prolonged CPR, monitor frequently for downward migration toward abdomen (serious injury risk) Re‑check after every rhythm check, shock, movement/transfer, or pad adjustment If continuous monitoring is difficult (e.g., transport): use/tighten straps to mitigate migration
## Post-ROSC
    Systematically assess injuries related to mechanical compressions
