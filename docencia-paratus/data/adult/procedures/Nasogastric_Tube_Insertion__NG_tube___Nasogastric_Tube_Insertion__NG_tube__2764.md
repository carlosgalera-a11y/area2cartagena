# Nasogastric Tube Insertion (NG tube) — Nasogastric Tube Insertion (NG tube)

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Essentials
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2764


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Gastric decompression (obstruction, ileus, gastric distension) Administration of medication or enteral nutrition Aspiration of gastric contents for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Suspected or confirmed skull base fracture Severe facial trauma Complete obstruction of the nasal passages Esophageal strictures (risk of perforation)
  ### Relative
    Severe coagulation disorders Esophageal varices History of recent ENT or esophageal surgery
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment Preparation
    Nasogastric tube (standard adult size 14–18 Fr)
    Tubing for connection to suction
    Cap (to close the tube when needed)
    Lubricant
    Non-sterile gloves
    60 mL syringe
    Transpore Medical tape
    Glass of water with a straw (if patient is conscious)
    Container for aspiration or drainage
    Towel

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Choose the right size of TNG (14-18 Fr for adults) Check the integrity of the tube Lubricate the tube distal end
## 2
    Install in a sitting or semi-sitting position if possible (minimum 30 degrees) Keep the head straight, slight bend of the neck forward
## 3
    Place the end of the tube at the end of the nose, then towards the earlobe, and then up to the xiphoid process (sternum) Mark the measurement obtained with adhesive tape or visually identify
## 4
    The floor of the nose is horizontal Gently insert the tube through one nostril, horizontally along the nasal floor Advance until reaching the oropharynx
## 5
    Encourage the patient to swallow (give small sips of water with a straw if conscious) Gradually advance with each swallow until reaching the mark
## 6
    Gently aspirate with the syringe to obtain gastric fluid If available, use pH test strips/paper to check aspirated fluid by dipping directly Consider an X-ray in case of doubt
## 7
    Securely attach the tube to the nostril with adhesive tape or specific fastening Avoid any excessive pulling on the tube

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Repeat checks every 8 hours if not in use, every 4 hours during enteral feeding, and before each medication or irrigation Monitor drainage and/or continuous administration (nutrition or medication) Maintain good nasal and oral hygiene
## Removal
    Disconnect any attached system (suction, feeding) Clamp the tube or place a cap Gently and continuously remove it, asking the patient to hold their breath or exhale slowly during removal
## Complications
    Poor placement (lung risk) Nasal bleeding or irritation Esophageal perforation (rare but serious)
