# Intravenous (IV) Catheter Insertion — Standard

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2768


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    IV fluid administration Intravenous drug administration Emergency venous access
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Obvious infection or severe burn at the insertion point Arteriovenous fistula or shunt (dialysis)
  ### Relative
    Trauma or proximal fracture at the insertion site Limb presenting with significant edema or compromised circulation Significant coagulation disorders (increased risk of hematoma) History of mastectomy and/or upper limb lymphedema

## Equipment Preparation
    IV catheter (appropriate size: Adult 18–20 G) Tourniquet Alcohol swabs or chlorhexidine Sterile dry gauze/compress Syringe with 0.9 % NaCl (for patency check / flush) Adhesive bandage or transparent dressing (± additional tape if needed) Tubing or needleless stopper plug IV solution and tubing (if indicated) Non-sterile gloves (± mask/eye protection if splash risk) Safe sharps disposal container

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Prepare all the necessary equipment within reach Wear non-sterile gloves
## 2
    
    Place the tourniquet 10-15 cm above the chosen site Identify an appropriate vein (visible, palpable, straight) Disinfect the chosen site extensively by making circular movements from the center outwards (let dry)
## 3
    
    Immobilize the vein by gently stretching the skin downwards with the free hand Insert the catheter, bevel up, at an angle of 10 to 30 degrees Observe the return of blood (flashback) in the catheter room Slightly reduce the angle once the blood appears, to avoid perforating the opposite wall of the vein
## 4
    
    Slightly advance the entire set (needle + catheter) by 1 to 2 mm after the blood return Hold the needle firmly in place, then gently slide the catheter alone into the vein all the way to its base Release the tourniquet immediately after successful catheter insertion
## 5
    Slightly compress the vein just above the catheter to prevent bleeding at the needle exit Remove the needle gently and immediately dispose of it in the secure container Quickly connect the tubing or place the stopper cap to prevent any bleeding or air entry
## 6
    
    Check the permeability by slowly injecting a small volume of liquid (e.g. NaCl 0.9 %) while monitoring the absence of infiltration (swelling, pain)

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Check regularly: Change the insertion site at 72-96 hours (or according to local protocols) Replace immediately in case of signs of infection or malfunction
## Removal
    Place a sterile compress directly on the insertion point without compressing it for now Slowly and gently remove the catheter while keeping the compress on the site Apply moderate pressure to the compress placed on the site for about 2 to 3 minutes or until the bleeding stops completely Visually check that the removed catheter is intact and complete (no fragments should be missing) Dispose of the catheter in a suitable secure container Apply a small adhesive bandage or a compress fixed with adhesive tape to the site
## Complications
    Hematoma or bleeding at the site Infiltration or extravasation (swelling, pain, local cold) Phlebitis (redness, warmth, local pain) Local infection (redness, warmth, purulent discharge) Gas embolism (rare) Fragmentation of the catheter during removal (very rare)
