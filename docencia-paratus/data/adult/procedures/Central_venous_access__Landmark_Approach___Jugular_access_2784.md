# Central venous access (Landmark Approach) — Jugular access

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2784


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Central venous access for administration of vasoactive medications, parenteral nutrition, chemotherapy, multiple transfusions, etc. Central venous pressure monitoring Peripheral venous access impossible or insufficient
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Local infection at insertion site Known or suspected jugular vein or superior vena cava thrombosis or stenosis
  ### Relative
    Major coagulation disorder (uncorrected) Distorted anatomy (e.g. trauma, radiation, surgery, malformation): ultrasound use recommended Uncooperative patient (consider procedural sedation)
## Anatomy
    
## Necessary equipment
    Central venous catheter kit (guidewire, dilator, scalpel, central venous catheter, puncture needle) Gauze Antiseptic solution (alcoholic chlorhexidine) Sterile fields, sterile gown, mask, sterile gloves, cap 10 mL syringe 21 G and 25 G needles Lidocaine 2 or 1 % (local anesthesia) Suture material and sterile transparent dressing Sterile saline flushes Needleless connectors/Caps (one per lumen) Appropriate patient monitoring (cardiac trace, saturometer, blood pressure)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
  ### Patient's position
    Supine position Head turned slightly away (45 degrees) from the chosen side Trendelenburg position (head of bed inclined downwards) unless contraindicated
## 2
    Position yourself at the head of the bed Place equipment tray to your dominant side for easy access without body rotation
## 3
    
    Hand washing, putting on a gown, gloves, mask, and hair net
## 4
    Large disinfection (neck and clavicle) with chlorexidine x 3 Wait at least 1 minute before applying sterile drapes Place wide sterile fields around the site
## 5
    
    Open central venous catherization kit Place the guidewire near you (on the patient's chest if they're calm) Flush each lumen to confirm patency Apply needleless connectors to all ports except the distal (brown) port to reduce air embolism risk
## 6
    
    Locate the two heads of the SCM muscle, aim for the apex of the triangle they form The puncture will be directed towards the ipsilateral nipple, at a 30-45 degree angle to the skin
## 7
    Draw up at least 5 mL of Lidocaine without epinephrine in the local anesthesia syringe Inject subcutaneously at the entry point
## 8
    
    Slowly insert the introducer needle, continuously aspirate Look for continuous dark venous blood flow as a sign of jugular entry, but always confirm you are not in the carotid artery (see Danger)
## 9
    
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index Detach the syringe with a gentle twist, keeping the needle steady
## 10
    
    Gently insert the guidewire through the needle (without resistance) to a distance of about 20 cm Always keep one hand on the guidewire to avoid accidental migration Remove the needle while keeping the guidewire in place For left IJ lines, turning the bevel to the patient's right and aiming the J tip curve to the right (towards the heart) will reduce the likelihood of misdirection into the arm
## 11
    
    Make a small skin nick at the guidewire entry:
## 12
    
    Thread the dilator onto the guidewire and advance to the skin entry Stabilize the wire with your non-dominant hand The guidewire should not move by more than 1-2cm Hold the dilator with a overhand grip, close to the skin entry Advance the dilator through skin and subcutaneous tissue using a twisting motion until loss of resistance (vessel entry)
## 13
    Remove the dilator with your dominant hand Make sure the guidewire doesn't move with your non dominant hand
## 14
    
    Advance the catheter over the guidewire until 1–2 cm before skin entry Ensure the guidewire exits the distal catheter tip; if not, withdraw it until visible Pinch the guidewire at the distal tip to prevent movement Slide the catheter to the desired depth, keeping the guidewire from advancing
## 15
    
    Remove the guidewire gently Attach needleless connector to the distal (brown) port Confirm venous return and aspirate air with a saline syringe Flush each lumen with 10 mL of saline
## 16
    
    Suture the catheter to the skin or use a sutureless securement device Apply sterile transparent dressing
## 17
    Chest X-ray to check position and rule out complications (pneumothorax, incorrect positioning)

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Daily monitoring of the site (redness, discharge, pain) Change the sterile transparent dressing every 7 days or as soon as it is soiled/wet Monitoring infectious signs or mechanical complications (chest pain, dyspnea)
## Complications
  ### Immediate and short term
    Neck hematoma Pneumothorax Carotid puncture Arrythmias Oozing from entry site
  ### Rare
    Air embolism Carotid cannulation which requires surgical removal Major vessel or right atrial laceration and cardiac tamponade Lost or entrapped guidewire
