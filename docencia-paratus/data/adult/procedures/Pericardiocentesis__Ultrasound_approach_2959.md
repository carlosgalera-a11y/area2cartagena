# Pericardiocentesis — Ultrasound approach

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2959


## Tab: Preparation
## Acute indications
    Cardiac tamponade with hemodynamic instability
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None in case of immediate life-threatening danger
  ### Relative
    Severe coagulopathy Skin infection at the puncture site Surgical treatment preferred:

## Equipment
    Ultrasound machine with cardiac probe Sterile sheath Sterile ultrasound gel Pericardiocentesis kit or: Long Needle (minimum 8 cm, 16-18 G, such as a spinal needle) Syringe 20-50 mL Gloves Consider time permitting: Antiseptic (chlorhexidine or iodopovidone) Sterile fields, sterile gown, mask, sterile gloves, cap Continuous cardiac monitoring (ECG, BP, SpO₂) Local anesthetic (e.g. lidocaine 1-2 %, without epinephrine)
## Localizing preferred approach
    Perform left parasternal, apical and subxiphoid views to assess the largest pocket and safest approach

## Tab: Execution
## 1
  ### Patient position
    Recumbent on back, head of bed slightly elevated to optimize fluid pocket size
## 2
    Position the ultrasound machine in the same axis as the anticipated trajectory of your needle Apply non-sterile gel to the cardiac probe Ensure the ultrasound is in cardiac mode Adjust ultrasound settings for optimal visualization (Mode, Gain, Depth)
## 3
    
    If time permits: Hand washing, putting on a sterile gown, sterile gloves, mask, and hair net
## 4
    Open pericardiocentesis kit Connect needle to syringe Insert the sterile sheath onto the ultrasound probe Stabilize the sheath by applying an elastic band Ensure there is no air bubble between the sheath and the probe Apply sterile gel to the puncture site
## 5
    This step may be omitted in the unconscious or arrested patient Anesthetize with 1 % lidocaine subcutaneously and deeper towards the pericardium
## 6
  ### Subxiphoid approach
    Locate a needle path to the pericardial fluid (ideally that does not traverse the liver)  Visualize the needle using a long axis view Insert the needle just distally to the probe at a shallow angle and locate it on the screen Needle inclined steeply (greater than 45 degrees relative to the skin)  Slowly advance the needle while exerting continuous negative pressure on the syringe while maintaining visualization on ultrasound Sudden appearance of fluid in the syringe suggests pericardial puncture Stop advancing immediately upon return of fluid
  ### Apical approach
    Locate a needle path to the pericardial fluid that does not traverse the lung  Visualize the needle using a long axis view Insert the needle over the rib, just distally to the probe at a shallow angle and locate it on the screen Needle inclined 30 to 45 degrees relative to the skin and on top of rib to avoid intercostal vessels Slowly advance the needle while exerting continuous negative pressure on the syringe while maintaining visualization on ultrasound Sudden appearance of fluid in the syringe suggests pericardial puncture Stop advancing immediately upon return of fluid
  ### Left parasternal approach
    Locate a needle path to the pericardial fluid that does not traverse the lung and is at least 3 cm from the sternum to avoid the internal mammary artery Visualize the needle using a long axis view Insert the needle over the rib, just distally to the probe at a shallow angle and locate it on the screen Needle inclined steeply (almost perpendicular  to the skin)  Slowly advance the needle while exerting continuous negative pressure on the syringe while maintaining visualization on ultrasound Sudden appearance of fluid in the syringe suggests pericardial puncture Stop advancing immediately upon return of fluid
## 7
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index
## 8
    Aspirate fluid, while monitoring hemodynamic stability
    Once patient has reached hemodynamic stability, slowly withdraw the needle

## Tab: Aftercare
## Complications
    Failure to relieve tamponade (e.g. in acute hemopericardium, loculated effusion, etc.) Recurrent cardiac tamponade Pericardial decompression syndrome (right ventricular or left ventricular dysfunction following drainage) Vasovagal reaction Arrythmia
 Injury to adjacent tissues including myocardium and liver
 Vascular injury (coronary, intraadbominal, etc.) Pneumothorax
