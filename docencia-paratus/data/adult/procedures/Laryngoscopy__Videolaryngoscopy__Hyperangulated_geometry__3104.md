# Laryngoscopy — Videolaryngoscopy (Hyperangulated geometry)

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 3104


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Cardiorespiratory arrest Aspiration protection Severe respiratory distress requiring mechanical ventilation Inability to maintain airway patency Current obstruction or at high risk of progressing to obstruction Procedural or transport facilitation
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Major traumas preventing access to the upper airways Anatomical impossibility of intubation
  ### Relative
    Significant anatomical anomalies (tumors, severe burns, severe edema, etc.)
    Shock likely to deteriorate from intubation and positive pressure ventilation:
    Significant pneumothorax or pulmonary air leak without chest tube

## Tab: Execution
## Mouth-screen-mouth-screen technique
    Mouth
 Screen
 Mouth
 Screen
## 1
    
    Head at the level of the intubator's sternum Ear-to sternal notch alignment If cervical collar is in place, remove it and ask an assistant to ensure stabilization
## 2
    Non-rebreather mask aiming 100 % FiO₂ (Flow rate ≥ 15 L/min) Consider apneic oxygenation (nasal cannula under mask)
## 3
    Look into the mouth Take the handle of the laryngoscope with the left hand Open the mouth using the scissoring technique of the index and middle fingers Rotate the blade into the center of the mouth
## 4
    
    Look at the screen Advance along the tongue in small increments until visualization of the epiglottis
## 5
    
    Visualization of the vocal cords on the entire screen = TOO DEEP
## 6
    Mildly increase upward traction in the direction of the laryngoscope handle Consider increasing the elevation of the head with your free hand Try external laryngeal manipulations to improve visualization (see tips)
## 7
    If there is partial visualization of structures despite optimization maneuvers, consider using a bougie (See Tips)
## 8
    
    Look at the mouth Insert tube on the right side of the mouth and then posteriorly Look at the screen Ensure a view of the vocal cords in the upper 1/2 - 1/3 of the screen Insert the tube in the laryngeal inlet
## 9
    Withdraw the stylet by 2 cm (pop it from the top with your thumb) Advance the tube through the vocal cords Continue advancing the tube to the desired depth Grip the tube against the laryngoscope handle with your left hand  Remove the stylet towards the foot of the bed
    ETT Depth
## 10
    Inflate the balloon with about 5-10 mL of air (just enough to ensure a tight seal)
## 11
    
    Connect the ventilation device and the capnograph to the endotracheal tube Initiate ventilation
## 12
    Use waveform EtCO₂ (gold standard), ultrasound or esophageal detector device to confirm tube placement Auscultation or lung ultrasound at the costophrenic angle
## 13
    Secure the endotracheal tube with tape or a commercial holder Order a chest X-ray

## Tab: Aftercare
## Post-intubation checklist
    Secure the tube Provide pain control and additional sedation  Evaluate pCO₂ using venous or arterial blood gases Chest X-ray to assess tube depth and exclude complications Place oro/nasogastric tube to decompress stomach Elevate the head 15-30 degrees Set a lung-protective ventilation strategy to maintain a plateau pressure < 30 cmH₂0 Humidify and assess the need for bronchodilators Establish endotracheal cuff pressure of 20-30cmH₂0
## Complications
    Hypoxemia / Hypoxia Aspiration Suspect endobronchial intubation if: Suspect supraglottic intubation if: Hypotension / Shock Hypertension Arrhythmias Dental trauma or injury to oral, buccal, or pharyngeal tissues
