# Oxygen (O₂) administration — Nasal cannula

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2755


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Administration of oxygen to a stable patient with spontaneous breathing Maintenance of oxygen saturation (SpO₂ ≥ 92-94 % or target according to local protocol)
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Complete blockage of the nasal passages
  ### Relative
    Severe facial trauma involving the nose Significant rhinorrhea (abundant discharge) Patient requiring high oxygen flow rates (> 6 L/min)
## Equipment Preparation
    Nasal cannula adapted to the size of the patient (adult or pediatric) Oxygen source with flowmeter Oxygen humidifier (optional, recommended for flow rates ≥ 4 L/min)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Check gauge before use; ensure > 500 PSI
## 2
    Connect the distal end of the nasal cannula directly to the flowmeter If a humidifier is used (flow rate ≥ 4 L/min), connect the nasal cannula to the distilled water reservoir connected to the flowmeter
## 3
    Slowly open the oxygen source Adjust the flow rate: 1 to 6 L/min (typically 2-4 L/min) Confirm the flow by feeling the oxygen stream on your hand
## 4
    
    Position the two curved tips downward in the patient's nostrils Pass the tubes on each side behind the patient's ears Gently adjust the slider under the chin to stabilize the goggles without tightening too much
## 5
    Immediately check the patient's comfort Monitor oxygen saturation (SpO₂) after 1 to 2 minutes to confirm the effectiveness of the selected flow rate Slightly adjust the flow if necessary to reach the target saturation
