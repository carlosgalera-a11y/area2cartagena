# Epistaxis — Manual compression

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Essentials
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2798


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Active nosebleed requiring immediate intervention
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
  ### Relative
    Severe facial trauma (risk of fracture or bone displacement) Presence of a foreign body in the nostril Patient on anticoagulants (special attention required)
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment Preparation
    Medical gloves Sterile compresses or clean tissues Topical nasal vasoconstrictor (Oxymetazoline - Otrivin®, Dristan®, if available) Nasal clip or nasal compression device (optional) Ice bag (optional)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Sit the patient upright, head slightly tilted forward
## 2
    
    Ask the patient to blow their nose to clear out the clots Apply firm, constant pressure on the soft part (wings) of the nose between the thumb and index finger Maintain the compression without releasing for at least 10 minutes
## 3
    Soak a compress or cotton ball with the nasal vasoconstrictor (Oxymetazoline) Gently insert the soaked compress into the bleeding nostril Keep in place during the duration of the compression (10 minutes)
## 4
    Place an ice pack or cold compress on the root of the nose or forehead
## 5
    Gently release the pressure after the initial compression period Check if the bleeding has stopped If bleeding persists, repeat the compression a second time (for an additional 10-15 minutes)
## 6
    Advise the patient not to blow their nose for 4 hours after bleeding has stopped
