# Cardioversion — Electrical

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2815


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Unstable arrhythmia with compromised vital signs: For stable patients, cardioversion is considered when medications are ineffective or as a chosen rhythm control strategy
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
  ### Relative
    Conscious patient without adequate sedation Inadequate anticoagulation in prolonged atrial fibrillation (> 24 hours), high embolic risk Arrhythmia due to digitalis toxicity (High risk of refractory Vfib/Vtach)
## Equipment Preparation
    Defibrillator / Cardiac monitor Self-adhesive electrodes or paddles Personal protective equipment (gloves) Sedation / Analgesia (if patient conscious) Oxygen and ventilatory assistance equipment (BVM, airway equipment) Suction equipment

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Install the patient in a supine position Remove jewelry and uncomfortable clothing Shave the hairy areas under the electrodes if necessary
## 2
    Upper right sternum under the right clavicle Left lateral, under the left breast, mid-axillary line
## 3
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (IV) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Ketamine (N/A) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Propofol (N/A) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Etomidate (N/A) — Procedural sedation
## 4
    
    Press on the "Sync" button
## 4
    Check that the monitor clearly displays the synchronization markers on the R wave
## 5
    Biphasic cardioversion preferred (higher success rate with lower energy) Flutter / SVT: 50 to 100 J Atrial Fibrillation: 120 to 200 J Ventricular Tachycardia with pulse: 100 J then increase if unsuccessful
## 6
    
    Clearly announce: "I'm clear, you're clear, everybody's clear" Make sure that no one actually touches the patient and that oxygen is removed Press the "CHARGE" button
## 7
    
    Visually check: "Everyone is ready, shock in 3, 2, 1..." Press firmly on the "Shock" button and hold until the shock is delivered
## 9
    Immediately check the ECG and vital signs If ineffective, repeat with higher energy according to protocol

## Tab: Aftercare
## Monitoring
    Continuously monitor ECG, SpO₂, blood pressure, and level of consciousness Maintain oxygen if necessary Monitor side effects related to sedation (respiratory distress)
## Complications
    Rhythm disorders (e.g.: VF if non-synchronized shock) Minor skin burns at the electrode sites Embolism (in prolonged non-adequately anticoagulated AF)
