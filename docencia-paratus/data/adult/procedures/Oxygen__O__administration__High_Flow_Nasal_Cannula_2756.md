# Oxygen (O₂) administration — High Flow Nasal Cannula

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2756


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Hypoxemia requiring a high oxygen supply Prevention of intubation in certain patients (e.g. COVID-19, exacerbated COPD)
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Complete obstruction of the upper airways Apnea or respiratory arrest (indication for assisted ventilation or intubation)
  ### Relative
    Severe nasal trauma or fracture of the facial skeleton Partial or significant obstruction of the nasal passages
## Equipment Preparation
    High-flow nasal cannula (adjusted to the patient's size) High flow generator device (e.g. AirvoTM, OptiflowTM) Heated circuit with sterile humidification chamber and sterile water bag Adequate source of oxygen Saturometer (continuous monitoring) Suction equipment nearby (in case of heavy secretions)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    The tips must fill ≤ 50 % of the nostrils without complete obstruction
## 2
    Connect the sterile water bag and secure it to the holder Check the water level in the reservoir and fill as needed (prevents airway drying) Turn on the device Set the initial temperature to 37 °C (May decrease to 34 °C based on patient comfort)
## 3
    Start with a flow rate typically between 30 and 40 L/min Adjust the FiO₂ according to the clinical condition or start with a high FiO₂ (e.g. 100 %) and gradually decrease based on the SpO₂ evolution
## 4
    
    Comfortably position the patient (semi-upright or upright position) Gently insert the nasal prongs into the nostrils Adjust the strap behind the head without excessive tightening Connect the nasal cannula to the device tubing
## 5
    Titrate based on clinical status and target SpO₂ (typically 92-94 %) If the patient is dyspneic or if their condition does not improve, gradually increase the flow rate up to 60 L/min

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    To prevent the risk of burns, do not place the tubing directly on the patient's skin Ensure that the tubing is not placed under a blanket or pillow Avoid sources of air directed towards the device (e.g. air conditioning, fan) to prevent condensation issues in the tubing Regularly check the water level in the humidification chamber
