# Central venous access (Ultrasound Approach) — Subclavian (axillary) access

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2941


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Central venous access for administration of vasoactive medications, parenteral nutrition, chemotherapy, multiple transfusions, etc. Central venous pressure monitoring Peripheral venous access impossible or insufficient
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Local infection at insertion site Known or suspected subclavian vein thrombosis
  ### Relative
    Major coagulation disorder (uncorrected) Fracture of the clavicle or proximal ribs Inability to tolerate pneumothorax or hemothorax ipsilateral to line placement Distorted anatomy (e.g. trauma, radiation, surgery, malformation): ultrasound use recommended Uncooperative patient (consider procedural sedation)
## Anatomy
    
## Necessary equipment
    Central venous catheter kit (guidewire, dilator, scalpel, central venous catheter, puncture needle) Gauze Antiseptic solution (alcoholic chlorhexidine) Sterile fields, sterile gown, mask, sterile gloves, cap 10 mL syringe 21 G and 25 G needles Lidocaine 2 or 1 % (local anesthesia) Sterile transparent suture material and dressing Sterile saline flushes Needleless connectors/Caps (one per lumen) Ultrasound machine with linear probe Sterile ultrasound sheath Sterile ultrasound jelly Appropriate patient monitoring (cardiac trace, saturometer, blood pressure)
## Preliminary ultrasound exam
    Perform a non sterile preliminary assessment of the relevant anatomy in a short axis view, taking care to distinguish the vein from the artery Locate the pleura (lung sliding) Ensure the vein is free of thrombosis by compressing it Identify the site of vessel entry as well as the site of skin entry The site of skin entry is distal to the site of vessel entry, at the same distance as the vein depth when using a 45 degree needle insertion angle

## Tab: Execution
## 1
  ### Patient's position
    Lying position, slightly inclined (Trendelenburg) unless contraindicated Head neutral Shoulder shrugged
## 2
    Position to the side of the patient at shoulder level Place equipment tray to your dominant side for easy access without body rotation
## 3
    Position the ultrasound machine in the same axis as the anticipated trajectory of your needle Apply non-sterile gel to the linear probe Ensure the ultrasound is in vascular mode Adjust ultrasound settings for optimal visualization (Mode, Gain, Depth)
## 4
    
    Hand washing, putting on a gown, gloves, mask, and hair net
## 5
    Large disinfection (neck + clavicle + chest) Wait at least 1 minute before applying sterile drapes Place wide sterile fields around the site
## 6
    
    Open central venous catheterization kit Place the guidewire near you (on the patient's chest if they're calm) Flush each lumen to confirm patency Apply needleless connectors to all ports except the distal (brown) port to reduce air embolism risk
## 7
    
    Insert the sterile sheath onto the ultrasound probe Stabilize the sheath by applying an elastic band Ensure there is no air bubble between the sheath and the probe Apply sterile gel to the puncture site
## 8
    Locate the subclavian/axillary vein below the clavicle, a few centimeters distal to the midclavicular or nipple line in short axis Glide towards the shoulder, sweeping through the vein Ensure the vein is free of thrombus by compressing it Distinguish vein from artery which has thicker walls, is usually more cranially located and is less compressible Identify the optimal vein entry point and the skin entry point Turn the probe 90 degrees for a long axis view
## 9
    Draw up at least 5 mL of Lidocaine without epinephrine in the local anesthesia syringe Inject subcutaneously at the entry point
## 10
    Short axis approach Sweep your finger on the left of the ultrasound probe to ensure it corresponds to the left side of the screen by seeing movement Slowly insert the needle  at an angle of 45 degrees with the bevel facing patient's feet Localize the needle tip just below the skin as a white dot on the ultrasound screen that disappears when the probe is advanced slightly Alternate advancing the ultrasound probe until the needle tip disappears and then the needle until the needle tip reappears until you reach the centre of the vein lumen Maintain the needle tip and the vein in the middle of the screen
    Long axis approach Rotate the probe 90 degrees from the short axis view Adjust the view to obtain the largest view possible of the vein Insert the needle with it's bevel towards patient's feet, just distally to the probe at a shallow angle and locate it on the screen Slowly advance the needle towards the vein, adjusting needle direction so it is clearly visible on the screen Increase the needle angle as needed to reach the vein
    
## 11
    A slight resistance may be felt when the vein wall is reached and a pop felt upon piercing through the anterior vein wall followed by a flash of dark blood in the syringe barrel
## 12
    
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index Detach the syringe with a gentle twist, keeping the needle steady
## 13
    
    Gently insert the guidewire through the needle (without resistance) to a distance of about 20 cm Aim the J tip towards the heart (facing down) Consider the Ambesh maneuvre (having an assistant occlude the jugular vein) to avoid misplacement Always keep one hand on the guidewire to avoid accidental migration Remove the needle while keeping the guidewire in place
## 14
    
    Make a small skin nick at the guidewire entry:
## 15
    
    Thread the dilator onto the guidewire and advance to the skin entry Stabilize the wire with your non-dominant hand The guidewire should not move by more than 1-2 cm Hold the dilator with a overhand grip, close to the skin entry Advance the dilator through skin and subcutaneous tissue using a twisting motion until loss of resistance (vessel entry)
## 16
    
    Remove the dilator with your dominant hand Make sure the guidewire doesn't move with your non dominant hand
## 17
    
    Advance the catheter over the guidewire until 1–2 cm before skin entry Ensure the guidewire exits the distal catheter tip; if not, withdraw it until visible Pinch the guidewire at the distal tip to prevent movement Slide the catheter to the desired depth, keeping the guidewire from advancing
## 18
    
    Remove the guidewire gently Attach needleless connector to the distal (brown) port Confirm venous return and aspirate air with a saline syringe Flush each lumen with 10 mL of saline
## 19
    Suture the catheter to the skin or use a sutureless securement device Apply sterile transparent dressing
## 20
    Chest X-ray to check position and rule out complications (pneumothorax, incorrect positioning)

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Daily monitoring of the site (redness, discharge, pain) Change the sterile transparent dressing every 7 days or as soon as it is soiled/wet Monitoring infectious signs or mechanical complications (chest pain, dyspnea)
## Complications
  ### Immediate and short term
    Hematoma Pneumothorax Hemothorax Arterial puncture Arrythmias Oozing from entry site
  ### Rare
    Air embolism Arterial cannulation which requires surgical removal Major vessel or right atrial laceration and cardiac tampnnade Lost or entrapped guidewire
## Removal
    [PROCEDURE] Central venous access removal — Central venous access removal
