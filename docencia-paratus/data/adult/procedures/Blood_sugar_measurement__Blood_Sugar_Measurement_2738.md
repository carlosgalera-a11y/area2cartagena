# Blood sugar measurement — Blood Sugar Measurement

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Essentials
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2738


## Tab: Preparation
## Indication
    Suspected hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia Altered mental status (unconsciousness, confusion, seizure) Routine monitoring
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
## Equipment preparation
    Glucometer
    Glucose testing strips
    Lancet
    Alcohol skin prep and gauzes
    Sharps containers
    Non-sterile gloves

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Wash your hands Put on non-sterile gloves
## 2
    
## 3
    Insert test strip in the glucometer The device should open automatically
## 4
    Choose lateral side of fingertip (ideally 3rd or 4th finger) Clean selected area with alcohol swab Allow alcohol to dry completely (prevents inaccurate readings)
## 5
    Remove protective cover from single-use lancet Place firmly against cleaned site Activate lancet to puncture skin quickly and firmly
## 6
    Gently squeeze fingertip, avoiding excessive pressure, until a small blood drop appears Wipe away first blood drop using gauze (recommended practice for accuracy) Gently squeeze again for a second blood drop
## 7
    Touch the test strip to the second drop of blood, allowing it to fill the test strip window Apply a gauze to the fingertip and press gently to stop the bleeding
## 8
    Wait for glucometer reading (usually < 10 seconds) Record displayed result immediately
## Interpretation
    Normal fasting: 4.0 - 5.6 mmol/L (72 - 100 mg/dL) Suspected hypoglycemia: < 4.0 mmol/L (< 72 mg/dL) Suspected hyperglycemia: > 11.1 mmol/L (> 200 mg/dL)
    [PROCEDURE] Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) and hyperosmolar hyperglycemic syndrome (HHS) — Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) and hyperosmolar hyperglycemic syndrome (HHS)
    [PROCEDURE] Hypoglycemia — Hypoglycemia
