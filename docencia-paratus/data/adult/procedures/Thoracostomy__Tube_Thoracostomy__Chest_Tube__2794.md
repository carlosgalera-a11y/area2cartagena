# Thoracostomy — Tube Thoracostomy (Chest Tube)

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Breathing
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2794


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Pneumothorax Hemothorax Empyema Thoracic trauma with severe respiratory distress or hemodynamic compromise Following needle decompression or finger thoracostomy
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
  ### Relative
    Significant local skin infection Severe uncorrected coagulopathy Multiple pleural adhesions (e.g. prior surgery) Emphysematous blebs Significant scarring over insertion site Uncooperative patient (consider procedural sedation)
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment Preparation
    Sterile chest tube (See tips) Thoracic drainage set with anti-reflux valve or vacuum suction system Sterile field or drapes Antiseptic Local anesthesia (Lidocaine 1 %) 10-20 mL syringe Size 21 G and 25 G needles Scalpel with 10 or 15 blade Hemostatic forceps (Kelly clamp) Suture thread and needle (usually size 0 or 1) Needle driver Straight (suture) scissors Petroleum jelly or petrolatum impregnated dressing Occlusive dressing Sterile gauze Adhesive tape

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Patient lying flat on their back (if possible slightly inclined with the opposite side down) Arm ideally elevated overhead to increase intercostal space size
## 2
    Position yourself at the side of the bed Place equipment tray to your dominant side for easy access without body rotation
## 3
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (IV) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Ketamine (N/A) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Propofol (N/A) — Procedural sedation
## 4
    Put on a mask, gloves, and sterile gown Large disinfection of the skin (lateral thoracic region) Apply sterile sheet or drapes
## 5
    
    Open the chest tube insertion kit and prepare the equipment Mount the chest tube onto the Kelly clamp through the Murphy eye
## 5
    
    Identify the 4th or 5th intercostal space at the anterior axillary line
## 6
    Draw up at least 10 mL of lidocaine Inject a skin wheal of lidocaine Aim towards superior margin of rib and anesthetize periosteum well Withdraw the needle slightly and aim just over the rib
## 7
    Advance until air or fluid is aspirated → confirm pleural entry Withdraw slightly until aspiration stops → needle tip just outside pleura Infiltrate lidocaine to anesthetize the pleura Continue withdrawing toward the skin, injecting lidocaine along the tract to cover pleura, muscle, and subcutaneous tissue
## 8
    Make an horizontal incision of about 2 to 4 cm through the full thickness of the skin  Insert your finger to feel the top of the rib, validating the correct tract
## 9
    
    
    Insert hemostat firmly over the rib with steady pressure Advance until a “pop” is felt → parietal pleura entered (expect air/fluid release) Open the clamp and withdraw to the skin to dilate the tract
## 10
    
    Quickly explore with your finger to confirm the release and permeability of the pleural space Check for the absence of adhesions or blood clots obstructing the opening
## 11
    
    Using a hemostatic clamp, gently insert the chest tube into the pleural cavity Advance 10–15 cm, ensuring all side holes are intrathoracic, then insert an additional 4–5 cm
## 12
    
    Connect the chest tube to a sealed drainage system or a Heimlich valve Immediately confirm the flow of air or liquid
    [PROCEDURE] Thoracostomy — Chest Drainage System (Pleur-evac™)
## 13
    
    Secure the drain to the skin with stitches Apply a petrolatum impregnated dressing or simply petroleum jelly and gauze Apply precut gauze to each side of the tube Secure with an adhesive tape (e.g. Hypafix™)
## 14
    Chest X-ray Observe the respiratory variations on the drainage system indicating an intrapleural position

## Tab: Aftercare
## Acute (< 24 hours) complications
    Poor positioning of the drain (e.g extrapleural, insufficient insertion) Hemorrhage in trauma Laceration of internal thoracic structures (e.g. blood vessels, lung, heart if left sided) Subcutaneous emphysema Re-expansion pulmonary edema
## Removal
    Cut the suture fixing the drain Ask the patient to hold their breath during a deep exhalation (Valsalva) Gently remove the drain while immediately covering the insertion site with a sterile occlusive dressing Post-removal chest Xray
