# Central venous access (Landmark Approach) — Femoral access

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2935


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Central venous access for administration of vasoactive medications, parenteral nutrition, chemotherapy, multiple transfusions, etc. Central venous pressure monitoring Peripheral venous access impossible or insufficient
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Local infection at insertion site Known or suspected femoral vein thrombosis Major pelvic trauma or abdominal hemorrhage
  ### Relative
    Major coagulation disorder (uncorrected) Distorted anatomy (e.g. trauma, radiation, surgery, malformation): ultrasound use recommended Uncooperative patient (consider procedural sedation)

## Necessary equipment
    Central venous catheter kit (guidewire, dilator, scalpel, central venous catheter, puncture needle) Gauze Antiseptic solution (alcoholic chlorhexidine) Sterile fields, sterile gown, mask, sterile gloves, cap 10 mL syringe 21 G and 25 G needles Lidocaine 2 or 1 % (local anesthesia) Suture material and sterile transparent dressing Sterile saline flushes Needleless connectors/Caps (one per lumen) Appropriate patient monitoring (cardiac trace, saturometer, blood pressure)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
  ### Patient's position
    Supine position Abduct and externally rotate leg Reverse Trendelenburg Have a colleague apply traction to retract pannus or use adhesive retention tape if needed
## 2
    Position yourself to the patient's right if right handed, left if left handed, at the level of the groin Place equipment tray to your dominant side for easy access without body rotation
## 3
    
    Hand washing, putting on a gown, gloves, mask, and hair net
## 4
    Large disinfection (entire ipsilateral groin area to midline and 15 cm below inguinal ligament) Wait at least 1 minute before applying sterile drapes Place wide sterile fields around the site
## 5
    
    Open central venous catheterization kit Place the guidewire near you (on the patient if they're calm) Flush each lumen to confirm patency Apply needleless connectors to all ports except the distal (brown) port to reduce air embolism risk
## 6
    
    Locate the inguinal ligament which stretches from the anterior-superior iliac spine to the pubic tubercle (lift adipose pannus if present) Locate the femoral artery near the middle of the inguinal ligament Palpate with 2 fingers and light pressure Skin Insertion site is 1 cm medial to the femoral artery and 3-4 cm below the inguinal ligament (or more if a lot of subcutaneous tissue) Vessel entry site is 1-2cm below the inguinal ligament and 1 cm medial to the artery Vessel entry MUST be below inguinal ligament
## 7
    Draw up at least 5 mL of Lidocaine without epinephrine in the local anesthesia syringe Inject subcutaneously at the entry point
## 8
    
    Slowly insert the needle with the bevel facing upward at an angle of 45-60 degrees aiming towards the umbilicus, continuously aspirate Aim to hit the femoral vein 1-2cm below the inguinal ligament, about 1cm medial to your fingers on the arterial pulse Look for continuous dark venous blood flow as a sign of femoral vein entry, but always confirm you are not in the artery (see Danger)
## 9
    
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index Detach the syringe with a gentle twist, keeping the needle steady
## 10
    
    Gently insert the guidewire through the needle (without resistance) to a distance of about 25 cm Always keep one hand on the guidewire to avoid accidental migration Remove the needle while keeping the guidewire in place
## 11
    
    Make a small skin nick at the guidewire entry:
## 12
    
    Thread the dilator onto the guidewire and advance to the skin entry Stabilize the wire with your non-dominant hand The guidewire should not move by more than 1-2 cm Hold the dilator with a overhand grip, close to the skin entry Advance the dilator through skin and subcutaneous tissue using a twisting motion until loss of resistance (vessel entry)
## 13
    
    Remove the dilator with your dominant hand Make sure the guidewire doesn't move with your non dominant hand
## 14
    
    Advance the catheter over the guidewire until 1–2 cm before skin entry Ensure the guidewire exits the distal catheter tip; if not, withdraw it until visible Pinch the guidewire at the distal tip to prevent movement Slide the catheter to the desired depth, keeping the guidewire from advancing
## 15
    
    Remove the guidewire gently Attach needleless connector to the distal (brown) port Confirm venous return and aspirate air with a saline syringe Flush each lumen with 10 mL of saline
## 16
    
    Suture the catheter to the skin or use a sutureless securement device Apply sterile transparent dressing
## 17
    Consider abdominal X-ray to check position

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Daily monitoring of the site (redness, discharge, pain) Change the sterile transparent dressing every 7 days or as soon as it is soiled/wet Monitoring infectious signs or mechanical complications (vascular thrill or bruit, leg swelling)
## Complications
  ### Immediate and short term
    Local hematoma Retroperitoneal hemorrhage Arterial puncture Arrythmias Oozing from entry site
  ### Rare
    Air embolism Arterial cannulation which requires surgical removal Arteriovenous fistula Major vessel or right atrial laceration and cardiac tamponade Lost or entrapped guidewire
