# Pericardiocentesis — Landmark approach

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2958


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Cardiac tamponade with hemodynamic instability
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None in case of immediate life-threatening danger
  ### Relative
    Severe coagulopathy Skin infection at the puncture site Surgical treatment preferred:
## Equipment
    Pericardiocentesis kit or: Long Needle (minimum 8 cm, 16-18 G, such as a spinal needle) Syringe(s) 20-50 mL Gloves Consider time permitting: Antiseptic (chlorhexidine or iodopovidone) Sterile fields, sterile gown, mask, sterile gloves, cap Continuous cardiac monitoring (ECG, BP, SpO₂) Local anesthetic (e.g. lidocaine 1-2 %, without epinephrine)
## Preprocedural stabilization
    Bolus fluids may be used as a temporizing measure Avoid:

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Recumbent on back, head of bed slightly elevated to 30-45 degrees if possible
## 2
    
    If time permits: Hand washing, putting on a sterile gown, sterile gloves, mask, and hair net
## 3
    
    Locate the xiphoid process Puncture just below and slightly to the left of the xiphoid process Needle inclined 15-30 degrees relative to the skin, directed towards the patient's left shoulder/clavicle
## 4
    This step may be omitted in the unconscious or arrested patient Anesthetize with 1 % lidocaine subcutaneously and deeper towards the pericardium
## 4
    
    Slowly advance the needle while exerting continuous negative pressure on the syringe Sudden appearance of fluid in the syringe suggests pericardial puncture Stop advancing immediately upon return of fluid
## 5
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient
## 6
    Aspirate fluid, while monitoring hemodynamic stability
    Once patient has reached hemodynamic stability, slowly withdraw the needle

## Tab: Aftercare
## Complications
    Recurrent cardiac tamponade Pericardial decompression syndrome (right ventricular or left ventricular dysfunction following drainage) Vasovagal reaction Arrythmia
 Injury to adjacent tissues including myocardium and liver
 Pneumothorax
