## Equipment

## Tab: Execution
## 1
  ### Patient position
    Recumbent on back, head of bed slightly elevated to optimize fluid pocket size
## 2
    Position the ultrasound machine in the same axis as the anticipated trajectory of your needle Apply non-sterile gel to the cardiac probe Ensure the ultrasound is in cardiac mode Adjust ultrasound settings for optimal visualization (Mode, Gain, Depth)
## 3
    
    Hand washing, putting on a gown, gloves, mask, and hair net
## 4
    Large disinfection with chlorhexidine x 3 Wait at least 1 minute before applying sterile drapes Place wide sterile fields around the site
## 5
    
    Open pericardial drain kit and other equipment Connect needle to syringe Place the guidewire near you Insert the sterile sheath onto the ultrasound probe Stabilize the sheath by applying an elastic band Ensure there is no air bubble between the sheath and the probe Apply sterile gel to the puncture site
## 6
  ### Subxiphoid puncture
    Locate a needle path to the pericardial fluid (ideally that does not traverse the liver)  Visualize the needle using a long axis view Insert the needle just distally to the probe at a shallow angle and locate it on the screen Needle inclined steeply (greater than 45 degrees relative to the skin)  Slowly advance the needle while exerting continuous negative pressure on the syringe while maintaining visualization on ultrasound Sudden appearance of fluid in the syringe suggests pericardial puncture Stop advancing immediately upon return of fluid
  ### Apical approach
    Locate a needle path to the pericardial fluid that does not traverse the lung  Visualize the needle using a long axis view Insert the needle over the rib, just distally to the probe at a shallow angle and locate it on the screen Needle inclined 30 to 45 degrees relative to the skin and on top of rib to avoid intercostal vessels Slowly advance the needle while exerting continuous negative pressure on the syringe while maintaining visualization on ultrasound Sudden appearance of fluid in the syringe suggests pericardial puncture Stop advancing immediately upon return of fluid
  ### Left parasternal approach
    Locate a needle path to the pericardial fluid that does not traverse the lung and is at least 3 cm from the sternum to avoid the internal mammary artery Visualize the needle using a long axis view Insert the needle over the rib, just distally to the probe at a shallow angle and locate it on the screen Needle inclined steeply (almost perpendicular  to the skin)  Slowly advance the needle while exerting continuous negative pressure on the syringe while maintaining visualization on ultrasound Sudden appearance of fluid in the syringe suggests pericardial puncture Stop advancing immediately upon return of fluid
## 7
    
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index Detach the syringe with a gentle twist, keeping the needle steady
## 8
    
    Gently insert the guidewire through the needle (without resistance) to a distance of about 15 cm Always keep one hand on the guidewire to avoid accidental migration Remove the needle while keeping the guidewire in place
## 9
    Clinical judgment is crucial, as no single method is entirely definitive
    Locate the guidewire inside the pericardium on ultrasound
    Agitated saline test: Injecting agitated saline through the catheter and observing for bubbles via echocardiography confirms catheter placement within the pericardial space
    Coagulation behavior: The blood in a cardiac chamber usually forms a clot, while pericardial fluid does not
    Hematocrit levels: The pericardial fluid generally has a lower hematocrit or hemoglobin level than intracardiac blood
## 10
    
    Make a small skin nick at the guidewire entry:
## 11
    
    Thread the dilator onto the guidewire and advance to the skin entry Stabilize the wire with your non-dominant hand The guidewire should not move by more than 1-2 cm Hold the dilator with a overhand grip, close to the skin entry Advance the dilator through skin and subcutaneous tissue using a twisting motion until loss of resistance (vessel entry)
## 12
    
    Remove the dilator with your dominant hand Make sure the guidewire doesn't move with your non dominant hand
## 13
    
    Advance the catheter over the guidewire until 1–2 cm before skin entry Ensure the guidewire exits the distal catheter tip; if not, withdraw it until visible Pinch the guidewire at the distal tip to prevent movement Slide the catheter to the desired depth, keeping the guidewire from advancing
## 14
    
    Remove the guidewire gently Attach a 3 way stopcock to the catheter
## 15
    
    Aspirate fluid, while monitoring hemodynamic stability
## 16
    Suture the catheter to the skin or use a sutureless securement device Apply sterile transparent dressing
## 17
    
    Once patient has reached hemodynamic stability, connect to Pleur-evac® with suction to gravity or collection bag

## Tab: Aftercare
## Complications
    Failure to relieve tamponade (e.g. in acute hemopericardium, loculated effusion, etc.) Recurrent cardiac tamponade Pericardial decompression syndrome (right ventricular or left ventricular dysfunction following drainage) Vasovagal reaction Arrythmia
 Injury to adjacent tissues including myocardium and liver
 Vascular injury (coronary, intraadbominal, etc.) Pneumothorax
