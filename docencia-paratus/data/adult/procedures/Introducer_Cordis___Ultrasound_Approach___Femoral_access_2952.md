# Introducer Cordis™ (Ultrasound Approach) — Femoral access

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2952


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Large bore central venous access for administration of large volumes of fluid (e.g. massive transfusion) Introducer for endovascular equipment such as transvenous pacemaker, pulmonary artery catheter, etc.
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Local infection at insertion site Known or suspected femoral vein thrombosis Major pelvic trauma or abdominal hemorrhage
  ### Relative
    Major coagulation disorder (uncorrected) Distorted anatomy (e.g. trauma, radiation, surgery, malformation): ultrasound use recommended Uncooperative patient (consider procedural sedation)
## Anatomy
    
## Necessary equipment
    Central venous introducer kit (guidewire, dilator, scalpel, central venous introducer, puncture needle) Gauze Antiseptic solution (alcoholic chlorhexidine) Sterile fields, sterile gown, mask, sterile gloves, cap 10 mL syringe 21 G and 25 G needles Lidocaine 2 or 1 % (local anesthesia)
 Suture material and sterile transparent dressing Sterile saline flushes Needleless connector/Cap Appropriate patient monitoring (cardiac trace, saturometer, blood pressure) Ultrasound machine with linear probe Sterile ultrasound sheath Sterile ultrasound jelly
## Preliminary ultrasound exam
    
    Perform a non sterile preliminary assessment of the relevant anatomy in a short axis view Ensure the vein is free of thrombosis by compressing it Identify the site of vessel entry as well as the site of skin entry The site of skin entry is distal to the site of vessel entry, at the same distance as the vein depth when using a 45 degree needle insertion angle

## Tab: Execution
## 1
  ### Patient's position
    Supine position Abduct and externally rotate leg Reverse Trendelenburg Have a colleague apply traction to retract pannus or use adhesive retention tape if needed
## 2
    Position yourself to the patient's right if right handed, left if left handed, at the level of the groin Place equipment tray to your dominant side for easy access without body rotation
## 3
    Position the ultrasound machine in the same axis as the anticipated trajectory of your needle Apply non-sterile gel to the linear probe Ensure the ultrasound is in vascular mode Adjust ultrasound settings for optimal visualization (Mode, Gain, Depth)
## 4
    
    Hand washing, putting on a gown, gloves, mask, and hair net
## 5
    Large disinfection (entire ipsilateral groin area to midline and 15cm below inguinal ligament) Wait at least 1 minute before applying sterile drapes Place wide sterile fields around the site
## 6
    
    Open central venous introducer kit Place the guidewire near you (on the patient's chest if they're calm) Flush the side port to confirm patency Apply needleless connector to the side port to reduce air embolism risk Insert dilator into introducer
    
## 7
    
    Insert the sterile sheath onto the ultrasound probe Stabilize the sheath by applying an elastic band Ensure there is no air bubble between the sheath and the probe Apply sterile gel to the puncture site
## 8
    
    Locate the femoral artery just inferior to the midpoint of the inguinal ligament The femoral vein is just medial to the femoral artery and should be easily compressible The site of venous entry is just proximal to the saphenous vein and 1-2 cm below the inguinal ligament The site of skin entry is distal to the site of vessel entry, at the same distance as the vein depth when using a 45 degree needle insertion angle
## 9
    Draw up at least 5 mL of Lidocaine without epinephrine in the local anesthesia syringe Inject subcutaneously at the entry point
## 10
    
    Sweep your finger on the left of the ultrasound probe to ensure it corresponds to the left side of the screen by seeing movement Slowly insert the needle with the bevel facing upward at an angle of 45 degrees Localize the needle tip just below the skin as a white dot on the ultrasound screen that disappears when the probe is advanced slightly Alternate advancing the ultrasound probe until the needle tip disappears and then the needle until the needle tip reappears until you reach the centre of the vein lumen Maintain the needle tip and the vein in the middle of the screen
## 11
    A slight resistance may be felt when the vein wall is reached and a pop felt upon piercing through the anterior vein wall followed by a flash of dark blood in the syringe barrel
## 12
    
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index Detach the syringe with a gentle twist, keeping the needle steady
## 13
    
    Gently insert the guidewire through the needle (without resistance) to a distance of about 20 cm Always keep one hand on the guidewire to avoid accidental migration Remove the needle while keeping the guidewire in place
## 14
    
    Make a small skin nick at the guidewire entry:
## 15
    
    Thread the dilator onto the guidewire and advance to the skin entry Stabilize the wire with your non-dominant hand The guidewire should not move by more than 1-2 cm Hold the dilator and sheath together as a unit with a overhand grip Advance the dilator and introducer through skin and subcutaneous tissue using a twisting motion until the introducer is fully inserted
## 16
    
    Gently remove the guidewire and the dilator Check for venous return in the sideport by withdrawing blood with the sterile saline seringe Flush the sideport with 20 mL of saline
## 17
    
    Suture the catheter to the skin or use a sutureless securement device Apply sterile transparent dressing
## 18
    Consider abdominal X-ray to check position

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Daily monitoring of the site (redness, discharge, pain) Change the sterile transparent dressing every 7 days or as soon as it is soiled/wet Monitoring infectious signs or mechanical complications (vascular thrill or bruit, leg swelling)
## Complications
  ### Immediate and short term
    Local hematoma Retroperitoneal hemorrhage Arterial puncture Arrythmias Oozing from entry site
  ### Rare
    Air embolism Arterial cannulation which requires surgical removal Arteriovenous fistula Major vessel or right atrial laceration and cardiac tamponade Lost or entrapped guidewire
## Removal
    [PROCEDURE] Central venous access removal — Central venous access removal
