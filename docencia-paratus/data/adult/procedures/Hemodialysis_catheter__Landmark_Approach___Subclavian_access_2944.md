# Hemodialysis catheter (Landmark Approach) — Subclavian access

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2944


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Need for urgent or short term hemodialysis (otherwise tunneled catheter preferred) Need for rapid administration of fluid (i.e. massive transfusion)
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Local infection at insertion site Known or suspected subclavian vein thrombosis
  ### Relative
    Major coagulation disorder (uncorrected) Fracture of the clavicle or proximal ribs Inability to tolerate pneumothorax or hemothorax ipsilateral to line placement Distorted anatomy (e.g. trauma, radiation, surgery, malformation): ultrasound use recommended Uncooperative patient (consider procedural sedation)
## Anatomy
    
## Necessary equipment
    Hemodialysis venous catheter kit (guidewire, dilator, scalpel, hemodialysis catheter, puncture needle) Gauze Antiseptic solution (alcoholic chlorhexidine) Sterile fields, sterile gown, mask, sterile gloves, cap 10 mL syringe 21 G and 25 G needles Lidocaine 2 or 1 % (local anesthesia) Suture material and sterile transparent dressing Sterile saline flushes Needleless connectors/Caps (one per lumen) Appropriate patient monitoring (cardiac trace, saturometer, blood pressure)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
  ### Patient's position
    Lying position, slightly inclined (Trendelenburg) unless contraindicated Head neutral Arm adducted, along torso Shoulder neutral or lowered
## 2
    Position to the side of the patient at shoulder level Place equipment tray to your dominant side for easy access without body rotation
## 3
    
    Hand washing, putting on a gown, gloves, mask, and hair net
## 4
    Large disinfection (neck + clavicle + chest) Wait at least 1 minute before applying sterile drapes Place wide sterile fields around the site
## 5
    
    Open hemodialysis catherization kit Place the guidewire near you (on the patient's chest if they're calm) Flush each lumen to confirm patency Apply needleless connector to the red (arterial) port to reduce air embolism risk
## 6
    
    Using non dominant hand, place either your index or thumb on the sternal notch and your other finger at the midpoint of the clavicle Insertion site is 2 cm lateral to and at the level of to the clavicle's bend
## 7
    Draw up at least 5 mL of Lidocaine without epinephrine in the local anesthesia syringe Inject subcutaneously at the entry point
## 8
    
    Slowly insert the needle parallel to the clavicle in the coronal plane, with the bevel facing patient's feet, continuously aspirate Skirt the lower part of the clavicle, aiming for the sternal notch Push the skin above the needle downward to maintain a horizontal angle of the needle Look for continuous dark venous blood flow as a sign of subclavian vein entry, but always confirm you are not in the artery (see Danger)
## 9
    
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index Detach the syringe with a gentle twist, keeping the needle steady
## 10
    
    Gently insert the guidewire through the needle (without resistance) to a distance of about 20 cm Aim the J tip towards the heart (facing down) Consider the Ambesh maneuvre (having an assistant occlude the jugular vein) to avoid misplacement Always keep one hand on the guidewire to avoid accidental migration Remove the needle while keeping the guidewire in place
## 11
    
    Make a skin nick at the guidewire entry:
## Rule out arterial placement prior to dilating
## 12
    
    If multiple dilators present, start with smallest Thread the dilator onto the guidewire and advance to the skin entry Stabilize the wire with your non-dominant hand The guidewire should not move by more than 1-2 cm Hold the dilator with a overhand grip, close to the skin entry Advance the dilator through skin and subcutaneous tissue using a twisting motion until loss of resistance (vessel entry) If multiple dilators present, remove smaller dilator and repeat with larger
## 13
    
    Remove the dilator with your dominant hand Make sure the guidewire doesn't move with your non dominant hand
## 14
    
    Advance the catheter over the guidewire until 1–2 cm before skin entry Ensure the guidewire exits the distal catheter tip; if not, withdraw it until visible Pinch the guidewire at the distal tip to prevent movement Slide the catheter to the desired depth, keeping the guidewire from advancing
## 15
    
    Remove the guidewire gently Attach needleless connector to the blue (venous) port Confirm venous return and aspirate air with a saline syringe Flush each lumen with 20 mL of saline Rinse each lumen with the amount of citrate specified on the catheter
## 16
    Suture the catheter to the skin or use a sutureless securement device Apply sterile transparent dressing
## 17
    Chest X-ray to check position and rule out complications (pneumothorax, incorrect positioning)

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Remove citrate prior to use by aspirating the amount specified on each lumen of the catheter Daily monitoring of the site (redness, discharge, pain) Change the sterile transparent dressing every 7 days or as soon as it is soiled/wet Monitoring infectious signs or mechanical complications (chest pain, dyspnea)
## Complications
  ### Immediate and short term
    Hematoma Pneumothorax Hemothorax Arterial puncture Arrythmias Oozing from entry site
  ### Rare
    Air embolism Arterial cannulation which requires surgical removal Major vessel or right atrial laceration and cardiac tamponade Lost or entrapped guidewire
## Removal
    [PROCEDURE] Central venous access removal — Central venous access removal
