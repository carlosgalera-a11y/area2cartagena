# Transcutaneous Pacing — Transcutaneous Pacing

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2816


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Unstable symptomatic bradycardia (HR < 50 bpm) Severe atrioventricular block (2nd degree type II or 3rd degree) Refractory bradycardia to medications
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
  ### Relative
    Severe hypothermia (Risk of inducing ventricular arrhythmias)
## Equipment Preparation
    Monitor / defibrillator (with pacing function) Adhesive pacing electrodes Razor (if necessary) Alcohol swabs (for skin cleaning)

## 2
    Expose the patient's chest Quickly dry any moisture on the skin Shave the chest if necessary to ensure proper electrode adhesion
## 3
    Anterior Left of sternum over the precordium Posterior Left infrascapular region (medial to scapula)
## 4
    
    Check that the pacemaker is sensing the patient's intrinsic rhythm
## 5
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (IV) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Ketamine (N/A) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Propofol (N/A) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Etomidate (N/A) — Procedural sedation
## 6
    
    Initially set à 70 bpm (adjust according to the patient's condition)
## 7
    
    Gradually increase the intensity (mA) until obtaining a wide QRS complex after every pacer spike arrow
## 8
    Check the patient's femoral pulse Use bedside ultrasound to confirm mechanical capture Check patient's clinical improvement (increase in blood pressure, improvement in level of consciousness)
## 9
    Once mechanical capture is achieved and the patient is improving, increase the current mA by 10 % for safety

## Tab: Aftercare
## Monitoring
    Continuously monitor ECG, SpO₂, blood pressure, and level of consciousness Maintain oxygen if necessary Monitor side effects related to sedation (respiratory distress)
## Transition to permanent solution
    Provide quick transfer to definitive care Consider internal pacing according to evolution
## Complications
    Chest pain secondary to muscle contraction (unpleasant but necessary for pacing) Mild skin burns (frequent to high intensity) Lack of capture (rare if good technique)
