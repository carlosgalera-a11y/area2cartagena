# Vagal maneuvers — Carotid massage

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Essentials
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2765


## Tab: Default
## Indications
    Stable supraventricular tachycardia Evaluation of an unexplained fainting episode Clarify the type and origin of a narrow-complex tachycardia
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Recent history of stroke or TIA Known carotid stenosis or bruit Recent myocardial infarction (less than 3 months)
  ### Relative
    Elderly patient (> 65 years old) Severe atherosclerosis
## 1
    [PROCEDURE] Vagal maneuvers — Valsalva maneuver (REVERT method)
    Carotid Sinus Massage Complications
## 2
    Place the patient in a supine position Confirm the absence of carotid bruit Install continuous ECG monitoring
## 3
    Slightly turn the patient's head to the opposite side Identify the carotid pulse at the neck level
## 4
    With the pulp of the fingers (index and middle), apply moderate pressure to the carotid artery, just below the mandibular angle Perform a smooth circular movement for 5 to 10 seconds
## 5
    Immediately check the effect on the heart rate via the ECG monitor Stop the massage as soon as the heart rate slows down or after a maximum of 10 seconds If the first attempt fails, consider repeating the maneuver on the opposite side
