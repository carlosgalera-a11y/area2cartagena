# Emergency Cricothyroidotomy — Scalpel-Finger-Bougie

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2782


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
  ### Complete obstruction or severe trauma of the upper airways.
    Failure or inability to intubate AND inability to ventilate by other means (BVM, LMA, etc.) Complete airway obstruction Severe upper airway trauma
## Contraindications
    Airway trauma or obstruction that makes access via cricothyrotomy futile, e.g.: Tracheal transection Tracheal obstruction In these cases, if it is not possible to secure the airway from above (ideally with fiberoptic assistance), tracheostomy should be the initial surgical airway

## Equipment Preparation
    Scalpel
    Bougie
    Endotracheal tube
    Syringe 10 mL
    BVM mask (Adult)
    Antiseptic Swabstick
## Mental and team preparation
    Perform one step of the procedure at a time Though this is a high pressure situation, you have likely performed more difficult procedures

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Immobilize the larynx with the non-dominant hand Quickly locate the cricothyroid membrane with the index finger
## 2
    
    As the scalpel approaches, raise your index finger to avoid injuring yourself Make a vertical incision of about 3 to 4 cm, starting at the thyroid cartilage and ending at the cricoid cartilage
## 3
    Palpation of the cricothyroid membrane with the index finger through the vertical incision
## 4
    
    Lift your index Puncture the membrane with the scalpel (blade facing you) Make an horizontal incision towards you until you reach cartilage
## 5
    
    Turn the scalpel 180 degrees without removing it from the patient Complete the horizontal incision across the entire width of the cricothyroid membrane (until you meet cartilage again)
## 6
    
    Scalpel comes out Insert your index in the incision Feel all the way in until the posterior wall of the cricoid cartilage
## 7
    
    Slide bougie along the finger into trachea Feel the bougie go underneath the pad of your finger Get your finger out as you confirm the bougie is in
## 8
    
    Gently slide the endotracheal tube (6.0 mm ideally) along the bougie until the cuff has just passed through the opening (approximately 2 to 4 cm beyond the incision)
## 9
    
    Remove the bougie while holding the tube in place. Inflate the cuff of the endotracheal tube with the syringe (approximately 5-10 mL of air)
## 10
    Quickly connect the ventilation system (BVM) Confirm the positioning with capnography (EtCO₂) Secure the tube to the patient, ideally using an anchor suture to the skin and multiple wraps around the tube (similar to a chest tube)

## Tab: Aftercare
## Arrange for definitive airway management
    Definitive airway management should be undertaken within 72 hours
## Tube stabilization
    A drain stitch is the most secure method of securing the tube Consider deep sedation +/- paralysis to minimize chances of losing the airway Consider using flexible ventilator tubing and ensuring the tubing doesn't apply weight to the endotracheal tube
## Acute (< 24 hours) complications
    Pneumothorax False passage/subcutaneous emphysema Endobronchial intubation Bleeding Local tissue laceration Tracheoesophageal fistula Mediastinal bleeding or leak
