# Bag-Valve-Mask (BVM) Ventilation — One-handed technique

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2751


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Ineffective breathing Respiratory arrest Cardiac arrest
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Conscious patient with intact gag reflex (risk of vomiting)
  ### Relative
    Major facial trauma compromising the seal of the mask
    Severely obstructed airway (unlikely to be effective)
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment Preparation
    Bag-valve-mask (BVM) with oxygen reservoir (preferred) Mask adapted to the size of the patient Source of oxygen (> 10-15 L/min) Oropharyngeal (OPA) or nasopharyngeal (NPA) airway if necessary to maintain patency
    [PROCEDURE] Oropharyngeal Airway (OPA) insertion — Oropharyngeal Airway (OPA)
    [PROCEDURE] Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA) insertion — Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA) insertion

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Connect the BVM to the oxygen source (> 10-15 L/min) Check the integrity of the valve and the expansion of the oxygen reservoir Choose the correct mask size (covers the nose and chin without overlapping the eyes)
## 2
    Position the patient supine, in a sniffing position Gentle extension of the head (unless cervical trauma is suspected, in which case maintain a neutral position with mandibular elevation only) Open the mouth and clear any secretions if present (suction)
## 3
    Place the mask on the face: upper edge on the bridge of the nose, lower edge under the chin Make sure the mask is centered and tightly covers the mouth and nose
## 4
    
    With the thumb and index finger, form a "C" around the mask to maintain the seal The three other fingers form an "E" under the jaw to perform a mandibular elevation
## 5
    Lift the jaw to open the airway and improve the mask-to-patient seal
## 6
    Gently squeeze the bag for 1 second just enough to observe chest rise  Confirm adequate seal (minimal air leaks) The respiratory rate should generally be about once every 4 to 6 seconds, adjusted depending on the clinical situation Use waveform EtCO₂ to confirm effective ventilation
