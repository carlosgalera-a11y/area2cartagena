# Resuscitative hysterotomy (perimortem C-section) — Resuscitative hysterotomy (perimortem C-section)

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Rare & Extreme
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2785


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Maternal cardiac arrest without ROSC at 4 minutes AND Pregnancy ≥ 20 weeks (palpable uterus ≥ umbilicus)
## Contraindications
    Pregnancy < 20 weeks (non-viable fetus and little hemodynamic benefit) ROSC before 4 minutes → no indication

## 5
    Midline vertical incision from xiphoid to symphysis pubis
## 6
    Retract the abdominal wall laterally Pull down to expose the bladder
## 7
    Vertical incision starting 5 cm inferior to the midline of uterus
## 8
    Insert your finger into the incision and lift the uterine wall away from the fetus Insert the scissors into the opening and make sure to shield the fetus from the posterior blade with your finger Extend the incison up to the fundus
## 9
    Insert your hand into the uterine cavity to grasp the baby's head Have assistant perform uterine pressure on the fundus
## 10
    Deliver the head while supporting the neck, the body will follow Use external pressure at the fundus (top of the uterus) if necessary
## 11
    Clamp the cord in two places Cut between the clamps Transfer the newborn to the neonatal team Baby will likely require significant resuscitation, make sure team is prepared
## 12
    Use cord traction and manual removal to deliver placenta

## Tab: Aftercare
## Immediate next steps
    Continue maternal and fetal resuscitation  Left lateral uterine displacement no longer required
    Administer synthetic Oxytocin
    [DRUG] Oxytocin (IV) — Post-partum hemorrhage
## In case of maternal ROSC
    Be prepared for marked bleeding
    [PROCEDURE] Postpartum hemorrhage — Postpartum hemorrhage
    Consider prompt transfer to operating room
