# Thoracostomy — Needle Thoracostomy

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Breathing
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2792


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Clinical signs of a tension pneumothorax Thoracic trauma with severe respiratory distress or hemodynamic compromise
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
  ### Relative
    Suspicion of massive bullous emphysema (rare) Penetrating trauma with risk of cardiac injury (attention to the site)
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment Preparation
    Large gauge needle 14 G (5 or 8 cm) Gloves (ideally sterile) Skin disinfectant

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Patient position Patient lying on their back or sitting upright if can't lie down If possible arm abduction > 90 degrees
    [PROCEDURE] Oxygen (O₂) administration — Non-rebreather mask ("100 % O₂")
## 2
  ### 4th/5th intercostal space (Recommended)
    
    Identify the insertion point 4th/5th intercostal space at anterior axillary line  Feel and mark the point just above the upper edge of the lower rib
  ### 2nd intercostal space, mid-clavicular line
    
    Identify the insertion point 2nd intercostal space, mid-clavicular line Locate Louis' angle → go down to the 2nd space Feel and mark the point just above the upper edge of the lower rib
## 3
    
    Don personal protective equipment  Perform a quick desinfection
## 4
    
    Keep the needle at a 90 degree angle to the chest wall Insert until you feel a "pop" (pleural perforation) and/or hear a hissing sound then stop Advance the catheter and then remove the needle
## 5
    Secure with occlusive dressing or adhesive tape

## Tab: Aftercare
## Transition to chest tube
    As soon as possible, set up a formal chest tube with a drainage system
    [PROCEDURE] Thoracostomy — Tube Thoracostomy (Chest Tube)
    [PROCEDURE] Thoracostomy — Percutaneous chest tube (Pigtail)
## Acute (< 24 hours) complications
    Poor positioning/failure (e.g extrapleural, below diaphragm) Thoracostomy closure with recurrent tension physiology Laceration of internal thoracic structures (e.g. blood vessels, lung, heart if left sided) Pneumothorax
