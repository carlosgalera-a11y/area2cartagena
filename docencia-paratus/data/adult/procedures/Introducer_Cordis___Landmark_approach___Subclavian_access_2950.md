# Introducer Cordis™ (Landmark approach) — Subclavian access

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2950


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Large bore central venous access for administration of large volumes of fluid (e.g. massive transfusion) Introducer for endovascular equipment such as transvenous pacemaker, pulmonary artery catheter, etc.
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Local infection at insertion site Known or suspected subclavian vein thrombosis
  ### Relative
    Major coagulation disorder (uncorrected) Fracture of the clavicle or proximal ribs Inability to tolerate pneumothorax or hemothorax ipsilateral to line placement Distorted anatomy (e.g. trauma, radiation, surgery, malformation): ultrasound use recommended Uncooperative patient (consider procedural sedation)

## Necessary equipment
    Central venous introducer kit (guidewire, dilator, scalpel, central venous introducer, puncture needle) Gauze Antiseptic solution (alcoholic chlorhexidine) Sterile fields, sterile gown, mask, sterile gloves, cap 10 mL syringe 21 G and 25 G needles Lidocaine 2 or 1 % (local anesthesia) Suture material and sterile transparent dressing Sterile saline flushes Needleless connector/Cap Appropriate patient monitoring (cardiac trace, saturometer, blood pressure)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
  ### Patient's position
    Lying position, slightly inclined (Trendelenburg) unless contraindicated Head neutral Arm adducted, along torso Shoulder neutral or lowered
## 2
    Position to the side of the patient at shoulder level Place equipment tray to your dominant side for easy access without body rotation
## 3
    
    Hand washing, putting on a gown, gloves, mask, and hair net
## 4
    Large disinfection (neck + clavicle + chest) Wait at least 1 minute before applying sterile drapes Place wide sterile fields around the site
## 5
    
    Open central venous introducer kit Place the guidewire near you (on the patient's chest if they're calm) Flush the side port to confirm patency Apply needleless connector to the side port to reduce air embolism risk Insert dilator into introducer
## 12
    
## 6
    
    Using non dominant hand, place either your index or thumb on the sternal notch and your other finger at the midpoint of the clavicle Insertion site is 2 cm lateral to and at the level of to the clavicle's bend
## 7
    Draw up at least 5 mL of Lidocaine without epinephrine in the local anesthesia syringe Inject subcutaneously at the entry point
## 8
    
    Slowly insert the needle parallel to the clavicle in the coronal plane, with the bevel facing patient's feet, continuously aspirate Skirt the lower part of the clavicle, aiming for the sternal notch Push the skin above the needle downward to maintain a horizontal angle of the needle Look for continuous dark venous blood flow as a sign of subclavian vein entry, but always confirm you are not in the artery (see Danger)
## 9
    
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index Detach the syringe with a gentle twist, keeping the needle steady
## 10
    
    Gently insert the guidewire through the needle (without resistance) to a distance of about 20 cm Aim the J tip towards the heart (facing down) Consider the Ambesh maneuvre (having an assistant occlude the jugular vein) to avoid misplacement Always keep one hand on the guidewire to avoid accidental migration Remove the needle while keeping the guidewire in place
## 11
    
    Make a small skin nick at the guidewire entry:
## 12
    
    Thread the dilator onto the guidewire and advance to the skin entry Stabilize the wire with your non-dominant hand The guidewire should not move by more than 1-2 cm Hold the dilator and sheath together as a unit with a overhand grip Advance the dilator and introducer through skin and subcutaneous tissue using a twisting motion until the introducer is fully inserted
## 13
    
    Gently remove the guidewire and the dilator Check for venous return in the sideport by withdrawing blood with the sterile saline seringe Flush the sideport with 20 mL of saline
## 14
    Suture the catheter to the skin or use a sutureless securement device Apply sterile transparent dressing
## 15
    Chest X-ray to check position and rule out complications (pneumothorax, incorrect positioning)

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Daily monitoring of the site (redness, discharge, pain) Change the sterile transparent dressing every 7 days or as soon as it is soiled/wet Monitoring infectious signs or mechanical complications (chest pain, dyspnea)
## Complications
  ### Immediate and short term
    Hematoma Pneumothorax Hemothorax Arterial puncture Arrythmias Oozing from entry site
  ### Rare
    Air embolism Arterial cannulation which requires surgical removal Major vessel or right atrial laceration and cardiac tamponade Lost or entrapped guidewire
