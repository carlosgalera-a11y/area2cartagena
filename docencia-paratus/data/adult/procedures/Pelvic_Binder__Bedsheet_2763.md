# Pelvic Binder — Bedsheet

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2763


## Tab: Default
## Indications
  ### Indications
    Presumed or confirmed pelvic fracture Pelvic instability (Open-book) Polytraumatized with suspected hemorrhagic shock of pelvic origin
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
  ### Relative
    Low-energy osteoporotic fractures  Lateral compression fractures:
## 1
    Lay the patient on their back (supine position) If possible, remove thick clothing or carefully cut to clearly expose the pelvis Perform a quick inspection to identify injuries, deformations, or signs of open fracture
## 2
    Choose a large blanket or a sturdy sheet Carefully fold in the lengthwise direction to obtain a strip of 20 to 30 cm (8 to 12 inches) in width Avoid wrinkles to minimize pressure on the skin
## 3
    
    Locate the greater trochanters of the femur (bony protrusions on the sides of the hips) Position the bedsheet so that it covers this area symmetrically
## 4
    Slide the bedsheet under the patient's legs Pull the bedsheet up to the level of the greater trochanters
## 5
    
    Pass the sheet to the partner on the opposite side
## 6
    Apply a constant, horizontal and progressive force to stabilize the pelvis
## 7
    
    With tension maintained, create a single 180 degree twist at the midline where the tails cross A second twist can be added as needed
## 8
    
    Place two clamps laterally on each side of the sheet Consider securing the clamps with tape to prevent loosening
## 9
    
    Internal rotation of the patient’s legs improves pelvic reduction Use tape to maintain the rotation in place
## 10
    Check the symmetry and alignment of the binder on the hips Regularly check neurovascular signs (peripheral pulses, color, sensitivity, and motor function of the lower limbs) Obtain control X-ray as soon as possible
