# Central venous access removal — Central venous access removal

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2915


## Tab: Preparation
## Indication
    Central venous access is no longer required for intravenous products Catheter installed in non-aseptic emergency conditions Damaged or non-functional catheter Presence of signs of catheter related complication or infection
## Contraindications
  ### Relative
    Uncorrected coagulopathy Uncooperative patient
## Equipment
    Non-sterile gloves Surgical mask for the clinician and for the patient Disposable pad Equipment to remove catheter stabilization system or sutures (e.g. sterile tweezers, sterile scissors) Antiseptic tampon or antiseptic stick Sterile gauze Occlusive dressing

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Stop all ongoing perfusion and close the clamps of each central venous access route Place the disposable pad under the catheter insertion site
## 2
    Place the patient in a supine position (head flat) or Trendelenburg position if there is no contraindication Ask the patient to turn their head opposite the catheter insertion site
## 3
    Don a surgical mask and then put a mask on the patient Proceed with hand hygiene Put on non-sterile gloves
## 4
    
    Gently remove the dressing in place Remove the sutures or sutureless fixation system
## 5
    Disinfect the catheter insertion site as well as the area where the dressing will be applied Let dry, without blowing, ventilating by hand, or touching for about 2 minutes
## 6
    Apply a sterile compress to the catheter insertion site Cooperative patient Perform the Valsalva maneuver: Non-cooperative patient
## 7
    
    Remove the catheter gently and steadily Apply immediate pressure to the site with the compress while removing Maintain pressure for 5 to 10 minutes (or even more if the patient has a coagulopathy)
## 8
    Confirm the integrity of the catheter If tip culture ordered, hold catheter over sterile container, cut off distal 5 cm so that it falls into container then send for culture
## 9
    Hold compression until the bleeding has stopped completely  Apply the occlusive dressing according to the establishment's protocol
## 10
    Leave the patient in a supine or Trendelenburg position for at least 30 minutes Watch the site for any signs of bleeding, respiratory symptoms or other complications
