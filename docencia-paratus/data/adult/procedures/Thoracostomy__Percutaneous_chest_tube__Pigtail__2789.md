# Thoracostomy — Percutaneous chest tube (Pigtail)

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Breathing
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2789


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Pneumothorax Pleural effusion (hemothorax, empyema, etc.)
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Skin infection at the insertion site (insert at alternate site)
  ### Relative
    Significant pleural adhesions Major coagulation disorder (uncorrected) Distorted anatomy (e.g. trauma, radiation, surgery, malformation) Uncooperative patient (consider procedural sedation)
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment Preparation
    Percutaneous chest tube insertion kit (guidewire, dilator, scalpel, pigtail pleural catheter usually 14 Fr, puncture needle, chest tube connector tubing, 3 way stopcock) Sterile insertion tray (sterile fields, compresses) Antiseptic solution (Chlorhexidine or Proviodine) Local anesthetic (Lidocaine 1 % or 2 %) 21 G and 25 G needles Syringes (10-20 mL for anesthesia, 20-50 mL for aspiration) Suture thread (silk 2-0 or 3-0 with curved needle) and kit Drainage system (Heimlich valve or water seal system) Occlusive dressing (Opsite or Tegaderm type) Appropriate patient monitoring (cardiac trace, saturometer, blood pressure)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Dorsal decubitus (ideally with insertion side mildly elevated), semi-seated or seated position  Arm on the affected side raised behind the head or in a comfortable position exposing the lateral region of the thorax
## 2
    
    Consider using ultrasound localization Pneumothorax: Effusion:
## 3
    Put on a mask, gloves, and sterile gown Large disinfection of the skin  Apply sterile sheet or drapes
## 4
    
    Open pigtail drain kit and other equipment Connect needle to syringe Place the guidewire near you
## 4
    
    Attach the 3 way stopcock to the catheter Insert the straightening dilator (dark grey) into the pigtail catheter and secure it with the luer lock connection
## 5
    Draw up at least 10 mL of lidocaine with 21 G needle Inject a skin wheal of lidocaine with 25 G needle
## 6
    
    Attach the lidocaine syringe to the inserting needle Aim towards superior margin of rib and anesthetize the subcutaneous tissues and intercostal muscles Once the pleura is breached, air or fluid will be aspirated Stop advancing
## 7
    
    Stabilize your hand with the hypothenar eminence and last 2–3 fingers on the patient Hold the needle port between thumb and index Detach the syringe with a gentle twist, keeping the needle steady
## 8
    
    Gently insert the guidewire through the needle (without resistance) to a distance of about 15 cm Always keep one hand on the guidewire to avoid accidental migration Remove the needle while keeping the guidewire in place
## 9
    
    Make a small skin nick at the guidewire entry:
## 10
    
    Thread the dilator onto the guidewire and advance to the skin entry Stabilize the wire with your non-dominant hand The guidewire should not move by more than 1-2 cm Hold the dilator with a overhand grip, close to the skin entry Advance the dilator through skin and subcutaneous tissue using a twisting motion until loss of resistance (pleural entry). This will require more force than used for a central line
## 11
    
    Remove the dilator with your dominant hand Make sure the guidewire doesn't move with your non dominant hand
## 12
    
    Thread the guidewire into the straightening dilator (which you've placed inside the pigtail catheter) Advance the catheter over the guidewire until 1–2 cm before skin entry Ensure the guidewire exits the distal catheter tip; if not, withdraw it until visible Pinch the guidewire at the distal tip to prevent movement Slide the catheter to the desired depth, keeping the guidewire from advancing Aim the curvature of the pigtail apically for a pneumothorax and postero-inferiorly for an effusion
## 13
    
    Remove the guidewire and the straightening dilator Close the 3 way stopcock to the patient
## 14
    
    Attach the catheter to the connecting tubing with the stopcock Attach the connecting tubing to a chest drainage system with water seal or a Heimlich valve
    [PROCEDURE] Thoracostomy — Chest Drainage System (Pleur-evac™)
## 15
    
    Secure the drain to the skin with a stitch or apply the dedicated percutaneous chest tube dressing or other securing dressing Apply a petrolatum impregnated dressing or simply petroleum jelly and gauze to the entry site Secure with an adhesive tape (e.g. Hypafix™)
## 16
    Chest X-ray Observe the respiratory variations on the drainage system indicating an intrapleural position

## Tab: Aftercare
## Acute (< 24 hours) complications
    Poor positioning of the drain (e.g extrapleural, insufficient insertion) Hemorrhage in trauma Laceration of internal thoracic structures (e.g. blood vessels, lung, heart if left sided) Pneumothorax Intra-abdominal insertion with liver, spleen or GI laceration Subcutaneous emphysema Re-expansion pulmonary edema
