# Rhinopharyngolaryngoscopy (RPL) — Rhinopharyngolaryngoscopy (RPL)

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Essentials
- Entorno: adult
- Indication ID: 2842


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Examine the supraglottic region to determine the presence of foreign bodies, edema, burns, or infection
## Contraindications
    None

## Anatomy seen through RPL
    
## Equipment Preparation
    Adult endoscope (diameter ≈ 3.5 mm) Screen to connect the endoscope or direct visualization through the eyepiece with a light source Lidocaine spray or lidocaine in syringe via nasal atomizer MAD300 Nasal decongestant (e.g., oxymetazoline, xylometazoline) (optional) Anti-fog solution (optional)

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Choose the nostril that allows better permeability by looking at each one
## 2
    Administer topical anesthesia
    Optionnal
## 3
    Seat the patient with the head well supported to prevent backward movement during the procedure Use an ENT chair if available, otherwise a stretcher Favour the Sniffing position
    
## 4
    
    Hold the distal tip of the endoscope between the thumb and index finger of the non-dominant hand Keep the dominant hand on the proximal section, controlling the adjustments Place the thumb on the control lever for the flexible tip Rest the 4th and 5th fingers on the patient’s face for better stability
## 4
    
    Follow the nasal floor in a straight line, distal tip in neutral (non-angled) position Advance the endoscope between the nasal floor and the inferior turbinate
## 5
    
    Ask the patient to breathe through the nose to open the nasopharynx further Direct the endoscope inferiorly
## 6
    
    Stop advancing as soon as the larynx is visible (avoid touching it) If needed:
## Removal
    Make sure the endoscope is in neutral position and remove it
