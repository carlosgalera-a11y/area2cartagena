# Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA) insertion — Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA) insertion

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2979


## Tab: Preparation
## Indication
    Unconscious or semi-conscious patient with an intact gag reflex Patient with difficulty maintaining airway patency Inability to insert oropharyngeal airway (OPA)
## Contraindication
  ### Absolute
    Severe facial trauma involving the nose or disruption of the midface or roof of mouth Known or suspecter basilar skull fracture (signs include Battle’s sign, raccoon eyes, cerebrospinal fluid leaking from nose or ears)
  ### Relative
    Severe nasal obstruction or deformity History of recent nasal surgery Significant coagulopathy Epiglottitis

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Measure the airway from the tip of the patient's nose to the earlobe or angle of the jaw
## 2
    Generously apply water-based lubricant to the NPA
## 3
    Position the patient's head in a neutral or "sniffing" position if possible
## 4
    
    Keep the bevel (pointed end) away from the nasal septum from the point of entry
## 5
    
    Lift the tip of the nose and insert the cannula perpendicular to the face (90 degree angle)
## 6
    Advance the NPA gently along the floor of the nasal passage until the flange rests against the patient's nostril
## 7
    Inspect the oral cavity after insertion If the cannula descends past the uvula, it is too long and should be replaced with a shorter one
