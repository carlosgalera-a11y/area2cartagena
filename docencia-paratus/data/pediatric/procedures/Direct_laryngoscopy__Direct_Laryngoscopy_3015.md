# Direct laryngoscopy — Direct Laryngoscopy

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 3015


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Airway protection Oxygenation failure Ventilation failure Anticipated Clinical Course (expected deterioration or transport) Definitive airway management during resuscitation
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Major trauma preventing access to the upper airways Anatomical impossibility of intubation
  ### Relative
    Unstable cervical lesion (importance of cervical precautions) Significant anatomical anomalies (tumors, severe burns, severe edema, etc.)
    Shock likely to deteriorate from intubation and positive pressure ventilation:
    Significant pneumothorax or pulmonary air leak without chest tube

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Hemodynamic stabilization Head at the level of the intubator's sternum Ear-to sternal notch alignment
## 2
    Non-rebreather mask aiming 100 % FiO₂ (Flow rate ≥ 15 L/min) Consider apneic oxygenation (nasal cannula under mask)
## 3
    
    Take the handle of the laryngoscope with the left hand Open the mouth using the scissoring technique of the index and middle fingers Insert the blade into the center of the mouth
## 4
    Advance along the tongue in small increments until visualization of the epiglottis Insert the blade tip between the base of the tongue and the epiglottis (vallecula)
## 5
    
    Engage the hyoepiglottic ligament by moving forward and upward Avoid the backward tipping movement
## 6
    Attempt external laryngeal manipulation to improve visualization (see tips) Consider lifting the epiglottis directly with the tip of the blade if the view is unsatisfactory
## 7
    
    Insert tube on the right side of the mouth and then posteriorly Advance the tube and insert it between the vocal cords Gently remove the stylet
## 8
    In direct visualization, advance the tube to the suggested depth (see below) Remove the laryngoscope
    ETT Depth
## 9
    Inflate the balloon with about 5-10 mL of air (just enough to ensure a tight seal)
## 10
    
    Connect the ventilation device and the pediatric capnograph to the endotracheal tube Initiate ventilation
## 11
    Use waveform EtC0₂ (gold standard), ultrasound or esophageal detector device to confirm tube placement Auscultation or lung ultrasound at the costophrenic angle
## 12
    Secure the endotracheal tube with tape or a commercial holder Order a chest X-ray
## 13
    Nasogastric catheter
    Gastric distension can impair ventilation effectiveness Consider placing a gastric tube after intubation
