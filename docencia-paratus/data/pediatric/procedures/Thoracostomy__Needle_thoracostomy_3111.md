# Thoracostomy — Needle thoracostomy

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Breathing
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 3111


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Clinical signs of a tension pneumothorax
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment Preparation
    Chlorhexidine swabs (antiseptic solution) 18-20 G catheter Sterile gloves 10 mL syringe

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Connect the catheter to the 10 mL syringe (free of any air)
## 2
    Patient position Patient lying on their back or sitting upright if can't lie down If possible arm abduction > 90 degrees
## 3
    
    Two possible sites: Palpate and mark the point just above the upper edge of the lower rib
## 4
    
    Don personal protective equipment  Perform a quick desinfection
## 5
    
    Insert the catheter slowly, perpendicular to the kid's chest wall, with the bevel facing upward, while maintaining continuous aspiration with the syringe Advance along the upper edge of the lower rib to avoid injury to the neurovascular bundle that runs beneath the rib Stop advancing the needle as soon as air appears in the syringe
## 6
    
    Slide cannula over needle into pleural space Withdraw needle and syringe
## 7
    Secure with occlusive dressing or adhesive tape

## Tab: Aftercare
## Transition to chest tube
    As soon as possible, set up a formal chest tube with a drainage system
## Acute (< 24 hours) complications
    Poor positioning/failure (e.g extrapleural, below diaphragm) Thoracostomy closure with recurrent tension physiology Laceration of internal thoracic structures (e.g. blood vessels, lung, heart if left sided) Pneumothorax
