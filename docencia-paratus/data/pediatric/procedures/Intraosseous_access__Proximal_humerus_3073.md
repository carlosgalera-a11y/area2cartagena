# Intraosseous access — Proximal humerus

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 3073


## Tab: Preparation
## Indication
    Intravenous access is difficult, impossible or insufficient in the context of a critical condition
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Fracture of the targeted bone Extremity with signs of neurovascular compromise Osteogenesis imperfecta
  ### Relative
    Infection at area of insertion site IO access in targeted bone within 48 hours (Extravasation risk) Previous significant orthopedic procedure at the site, prosthetic limb or joint
## Anatomy
    Gently rotate the arm inward to better expose the humeral head Locate the humeral head by placing the hand on the patient's shoulder, thumb pointing forward, palm facing down The insertion site is located at the junction between the humeral head and the greater tubercle, approximately 1-2 cm above the surgical neck
    Anatomical landmark
## Prepare your equipment
    Recommended needle
    EZ-IO® Drill
    EZ-Stabilizer® Dressing
    EZ-Connect® Extension Set
    Normal Saline Syringe 10 mL
    IV Pressure bag
    Antiseptic Swabstick
    Syringe 10 mL
    Non-sterile gloves

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Clean, no touch technique with antiseptic solution according to local protocols
## 2
    Position the arm to expose the humeral head and move critical structures away from the insertion path Option 1 Option 2
## 3
    
    1. Identify the humeral head Palpate the patient’s shoulder with the palm of your hand to locate the humeral head (it feels like a round ball under your hand) 2. Locate the surgical neck Place your hands on each side of the humeral head and use your thumbs to palpate the junction between the head and the shaft (it should feel like a ball sitting on a golf tee) This junction is the surgical neck of the humerus 3. Determine the insertion site The insertion site is located 1–2 cm above the surgical neck, on the anterolateral surface of the humeral head
## 4
    
    Flush the EZ-Connect® extension set with 1 mL of sterile saline
## 5
    Keep the patient's arm against their body to ensure stability during insertion
## 6
    
    Insertion angle: Apply light pressure without activating the driver to pierce the skin and advance until bone contact.
## 7
    Check if you are using the appropriate needle length You should see at least one 5 mm black mark; if not, your needle is too short
## 8
    Press the driver trigger and apply gentle pressure Release the trigger as soon as you feel a "pop" or a loss of resistance. Avoid recoil and do not pull back on the driver when releasing the trigger
## 9
    Stabilize the needle with your thumb and index already sitting on the patient A stable needle within the bone is your first confirmation of placement
## 10
    Disconnect the driver and remove the stylet by twisting counterclockwise
## 11
    Are labs needed? If yes, carefully connect an empty syringe to the hub of the needle and aspirate bone marrow
## 12
    Apply the EZ-Stabilizer® Dressing
## 13
    
    Connect the primed EZ-Connect® Extension Set to the hub, secure by twisting clockwise Pull the tab off the dressing and apply the adhesive to the skin Confirm placement by aspirating bone marrow if not already done
## 14
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (1st dose) — Intraosseous analgesia
      Lidocaine 2% WITHOUT preservative and WITHOUT epinephrine (20 mg/mL "cardiac" lidocaine)
    A second dose may be repeated after the flush
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (2nd dose) — Intraosseous analgesia
      Lidocaine 2% WITHOUT preservative and WITHOUT epinephrine (20 mg/mL "cardiac" lidocaine)
## 15
    Flush NaCl 0.9% and look for any evidence of extravasation
## 16
    
    Administer Drugs/Fluids/Blood under 300 mmHg of pressure via infusion pump or pressure bag

## Tab: Aftercare
## Confirmation of placement
    1) Needle stands on its own within the bone 2) Bone marrow is obtained The incapacity to draw bone marrow doesn't necessarily mean that the IO is not at the right place. Consider aspirating after the flush 3) Flushing of the needle occurs without any evidence of extravasation Resistance to flushing is expected
## Maintenance
    Frequently check for signs of extravasation Infusion rate with infusion pump has been shown to be sufficient to maintain IO line patency Remove the IO after 24 hours of use
## Removal
    1) Attach a luer lock syringe to the hub of the needle 2) Maintain axial alignment 3) Twist the syringe clockwise, while pulling straight out Do not rock or bend the catheter during removal
## Complications
    Extravasation or infiltration of fluids Compartment syndrome Osteomyelitis
## Theoretical complications without evidence
    Injury to the growth plate in pediatric patients No growth plate damage has been documented in known medical literature Fat embolism There are no reports of clinically significant fat embolism in the literature
