# King LTSD™ insertion — King LTSD™ insertion

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2975


## Tab: Preparation
## Indication
    Failed or difficult endotracheal intubation attempts (Plan B)
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Limited mouth opening Inadequate paralysis or sedation (intact gag reflex) Complete upper airway obstruction
  ### Relative
    Trauma or disease of the oropharynx/esophagus (Risk of perforation or hemorrhage) High airway pressure Airway obstruction by a foreign body
## Anatomy
    A single valve inflates both cuffs Proximal cuff: At base of tongue Distal cuff: In proximal esophagus
    
## Equipment preparation
    King LTS-D
    Endotracheal tube holder

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    King LTS-D™
## 2
    Inflate the cuffs with a 60 mL syringe to check for leaks, then completely deflate both cuffs prior to insertion
## 3
    Apply a water-based lubricant to the posterior aspect of the tube Do NOT put lubricant on the ventilation port
## 4
    Position the patient's head in a neutral or "sniffing" position if possible
## 5
    Hold the King LTS-D™ with your dominant hand like a pencil
## 6
    Grasp the patient's mandibule & tongue with your non dominant hand Pull upward to open the mouth and create space
## 7
    Insert the tip into the mouth at a 45-90 degree angle so the blue orientation line touch the corner of the mouth
## 8
    As the tube tip passes under the tongue, rotate the tube back to the midline (blue orientation line facing the patient's chin)
## 9
    Without exerting excessive force, advance the tube until the base of the connector is aligned with the patient's teeth or significant resistance is felt
## 10
    Do not exceed the recommended maximum cuff volume
    Recommended Cuff Volume
## 11
    Connect the BVM to the 15 mm connector of the King LTS-D™
## 12
    First attempt at ventilation often result in no air movement and high ventilation pressure Slowly withdraw the King LTS-D™ until ventilation is easy and free-flowing
## 13
    Confirm proper positioning using a capnograph (EtCO₂) and bilateral lung auscultation
## 14
    Use tape or a commercial tube holder to secure the device to the patient's face
## 15
    If gastric decompression is needed, a suction tube can be inserted through the gastric access lumen of the KING LTS-D™. Lubricate the gastric tube before insertion

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    The King LTSD™ is a temporary measure Plan for a prompt transition to a definitive airway (e.g.: Endotracheal intubation)
    Monitor cuff pressure Periodically check and adjust cuff pressure to ensure an adequate seal without causing excessive pressure on the surrounding tissues If available, use a manometer and aim for a cuff pressure < 60 cmH₂O
    Monitor patient's ventilation & oxygenation Continuously monitor the patient's oxygen saturation (SpO₂) & waveform capnography (EtCO₂)
## Removal
    1) Deflate Cuffs Completely deflate both the proximal and distal cuffs using the syringe. The pilot balloon will appear completely collapsed, confirming deflation 2) Gentle Removal Gently and smoothly withdraw the King LTS-D™ from the patient's mouth. Anticipate possible gagging or vomiting during removal
## Complications
    Device malposition can lead to inadequate ventilation Tissue trauma Aspiration (rare)
