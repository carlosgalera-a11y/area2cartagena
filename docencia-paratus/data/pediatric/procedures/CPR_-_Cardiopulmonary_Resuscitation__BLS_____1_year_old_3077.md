# CPR - Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (BLS) — < 1 year old

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 3077


## Tab: Execution
## Indications
    Cardiac arrest (unresponsive, no normal breathing, no pulse) Heart rate < 60 /min with signs of poor perfusion
## 1
    Immediately call for help Obtain an AED or send someone to get it if available
## 2
    Pad size
    Pad position
    Turn on AED and follow voice instructions Place electrode pads on bare chest as indicated Ensure no one touches the patient during analysis and before shock delivery Immediately resume CPR after shock, starting with compressions
## 3
    
    Place thumbs at the level of the sternum below the inter-nipple line The remaining fingers are under the back as support
## 4
    
    Compress to a depth of approximately 1/3 of the anteroposterior diameter of the chest at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute Keep hands in constant contact with the chest at all time:
## 5
    
    Open airway (Chin lift or Jaw thrust) Provide 2 breaths after every 15 compressions Each breath should last approximately 1 second, just enough to make the chest rise
## 6
    Continue with cycles of 15 compressions and 2 breaths Check cardiac rhythm every 2 minutes Switch rescuers performing compressions every 2 minutes or once too fatigued to maintain CPR quality Continue until advanced medical help arrives or the patient shows signs of life
