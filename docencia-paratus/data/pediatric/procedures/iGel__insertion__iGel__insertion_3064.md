# iGel® insertion — iGel® insertion

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 3064


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Failed or difficult endotracheal intubation attempts (Plan B)
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Inadequate paralysis or sedation (intact gag reflex) Complete upper airway obstruction
  ### Relative
    Limited mouth opening Trauma or disease of the oropharynx/esophagus (Risk of perforation or hemorrhage) Airway obstruction by a foreign body

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    iGel®
## 2
    
## 3
    Apply a water-based lubricant to the posterior aspect of the tube Do NOT put lubricant on the ventilation port
## 4
    Position the patient's head in a neutral or "sniffing" position if possible
## 5
    Hold iGel® with your dominant hand like a pencil
## 6
    The chin should be gently pressed down before proceeding Introduce the leading soft tip into the mouth of the patient in a direction towards thehard palate
## 7
    
    Insert the device gently, following the natural curvature of the palate, pushing it downward and backward until clear resistance is felt
## 8
    Connect the ventilation device and the pediatric capnograph to the iGel® Initiate ventilation
## 9
    Confirm proper positioning using a pediatric-appropriate capnograph (EtCO₂) Bilateral lung auscultation Observe chest rise
## 10
    Use tape to secure the device to the patient's face
## 11
    If gastric decompression is needed, a gastric tube can be inserted through the gastric access lumen of the iGel® Lubricate the gastric tube before insertion

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Monitor patient's ventilation & oxygenation Continuously monitor the patient's oxygen saturation (SpO₂) and waveform capnography (EtCO₂)
## Removal
    Gently and smoothly withdraw the iGel® from the patient's mouth Anticipate possible gagging or vomiting during removal.
## Complications
    Device malposition can lead to inadequate ventilation Tissue trauma Laryngospasm Aspiration (rare)
