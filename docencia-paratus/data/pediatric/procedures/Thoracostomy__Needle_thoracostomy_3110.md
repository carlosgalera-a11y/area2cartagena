# Thoracostomy — Needle thoracostomy

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Breathing
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 3110


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Clinical signs of a tension pneumothorax
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    None
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment preparation
    Fenestrated sterile drape Chlorhexidine swabs (antiseptic solution) Sterile 2x2 woven gauze pads JELCO® 20 G catheter (pink) Sterile IV extension tubing with T-connector Three-way stopcock 10 mL syringe 20 mL syringe Sterile gloves Transparent dressing, OPSITE® type

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Connect the catheter to the 10 mL syringe (free of any air) Connect the tubing to the 3-Way Stopcock Connect the 3-Way Stopcock to the 20 mL syringe
## 2
    Place the infant with the affected arm raised above the shoulder and maintain immobilization throughout the procedure Whenever feasible, position the side of the pneumothorax slightly upward to allow air to rise and collect in the superior aspect of the thoracic cavity
## 3
    
    Two possible sites: Palpate and mark the point just above the upper edge of the lower rib
## 4
    
    Put on personal protective equipment Perform a rapid disinfection Place a fenestrated sterile drape over the puncture site
## 5
    
    Insert the catheter slowly, perpendicular to the infant’s chest wall, with the bevel facing upward, while maintaining continuous aspiration with the syringe Advance along the upper edge of the lower rib to avoid injury to the neurovascular bundle that runs beneath the rib Stop advancing the needle as soon as air appears in the syringe
## 6
    The aspiration of air confirms entry into the pleural cavity Gently withdraw the needle and syringe while firmly holding the catheter in place with your non-dominant hand
## 7
    
## 8
    
    Turn the stopcock to close the external port, allowing a connection between the newborn and the syringe Gently aspirate air with the syringe Stop if resistance is felt, do not force aspiration
## 9
    
    If the 20 mL syringe is completely filled with air and no resistance is felt, evacuate the air and continue aspiration Evacuate the aspirated air: Continue aspiration Repeat the cycle

## Tab: Aftercare
## Monitoring
    Use continuous cardiorespiratory and pulse oximetry monitoring to assess response to treatment
## Catheter removal
    The catheter may be secured with a dressing and left in place temporarily while awaiting chest drain insertion, if indicated Removal procedure:
## Complications
    Pulmonary penetration and air leak Needle track trauma (bleeding) Reaccumulation of pneumothorax Infection
