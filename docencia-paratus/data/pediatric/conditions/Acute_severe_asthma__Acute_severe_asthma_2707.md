## Refractory acute severe asthma
  ### In collaboration with pediatric intensive care
    [DRUG] Salbutamol (Infusion (Pump)) — Acute severe asthma (IV infusion)
      IV infusion
    Consider High-flow nasal cannula (HFNC)
    Consider initiating CPAP or BiPAP with bedside titration: Start PEP at 5 cm H2O ± Inspiratory pressure between 6-10 cm H2O
    [DRUG] Ketamine (Bolus) — Refractory Acute Severe Asthma
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Ketamine (Infusion (Pump)) — Refractory Acute Severe Asthma
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Dexmedetomidine (N/A) — Sedation
      IV infusion
    Do not use IV aminophylline unless serum dosages are readily available. Little advantage over beta-agonists.
## Hemodynamic support
  ### Fluid bolus
    Assess volume status and consider cautious correction according to insensible losses, intakes and hemodynamic effects of PEP
    Crystalloid bolus, balanced solution preferably
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (Infusion (Pump)) — Acute severe asthma
      IV infusion
## Altered state of consciousness or respiratory failure secondary to bronchosopasm
  ### Intubation
    Make sure all other therapies are maximized
    Consider ketamine as an induction agent
    Intubation by the best intubator in the room
    Carefully adjust the respirator with PEEP considering patient's auto-PEEP with short inspiratory time with respiratory rate adjustment
  ### Peri-cardiac or respiratory arrest
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (N/A) — Push-Dose pressor
      Push-dose pressor IV
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (N/A) — Cardiac arrest
    **Consider intubation - First and only attempt at larngoscopy**
      Neuromuscular blocker
      Best intubator in the room
      Temporarily stop CPR during the intubation attempt to maximize success.
    Consider emergency surgical airway
  ### Deep sedation (RASS -3 or less) ± Neuromuscular blocker
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (Bolus) — Post-intubation analgesia
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (Infusion (Pump)) — Post-intubation analgesia
      IV infusion
    Avoid morphine, as it may cause histamine release.
    [DRUG] Propofol (mcg/kg/min) — Post-intubation sedation
    [DRUG] Propofol (mg/kg/h) — Post-intubation sedation
    [DRUG] Midazolam (Infusion (Premixed bag)) — Post-intubation sedation
    [DRUG] Midazolam (Infusion (Pump)) — Post-intubation sedation
    [DRUG] Rocuronium (N/A) — Neuromuscular Blocker (miscellaneous/non-intubation)
      Bolus
    Consider an urgent anesthesia consultation for the administration of volatile anesthetics ideally sevoflurane
    Consider ECMO as a last resort if available locally
