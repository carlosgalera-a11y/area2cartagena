# Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) — Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Metabolic
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2705

## Initial investigations
    Biochemical criteria of DKA  Blood sugar > 11 mmol/L with ketonemia > 3 mmol/L or moderate/high ketonuria + pH < 7.3 with bicarbonates < 15 mmol/L
    Risk factors for cerebral edema <5 years of age, severe acidosis, hypotension, low pCO2, high urea
  ### If serum K+ > 5.5 mmol/L
    12-lead ECG and administer maintenance without K+
    [PROCEDURE] Hyperkalemia — Hyperkalemia
  ### If serum K+ < 2.5 mmol/L
    12-lead ECG, replete in K+ IV and WAIT until K+ > 2.5 mmol/L before starting insulin
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (Peripheral or central line) — Severe hypokalemia
## Initial fluid management
  ### Fluid bolus
    [DRUG] Lactate Ringer's (N/A) — Diabetic Ketoacidosis
    [DRUG] Plasmalyte (N/A) — Diabetic Ketoacidosis
    [DRUG] NaCl 0.9% (N/A) — Diabetic Ketoacidosis
  ### Maintenance fluid
    Balanced solution preferably unless neuroprotection
    [DRUG] Maintenance fluids (Hyperhydration (150%)) — Maintenance fluids
    If blood glucose > 16 mmol/L: LR-KCl 40 mmol/L or NaCl 0.9%-KCl 40 mmol/L
    If blood glucose between 11-16 mmol/L: D5%-LR-KCl 40 mmol/L or D5%-NaCl 0.9%-KCl 40 mmol/L
    If blood glucose < 11 mmol/L or fall > 5 mmol/L/h: D10%-LR-KCl 40 mmol/L or D10%-NaCl 0.9%-KCl 40 mmol/L
## Insulin infusion
  ### Start 60 min after starting the maintenance fluids
    Avoid IV insulin bolus, as it carries a risk of cerebral edema.
    [DRUG] Insulin regular (Infusion (Pump)) — Diabetic ketoacidosis XX
    [DRUG] Insulin regular (Infusion (Premixed bag)) — Diabetic ketoacidosis XX
    The dose of 0.1 units/Kg/h is possible for certain special cases or cases refractory to 0.05 units/Kg/h. Contact a pediatric endocrinologist or pediatric intensivist if in doubt.
## Specific treatment
  ### Cerebral oedema
    In cerebral oedema secondary to DKA, mannitol could be use as a first line agent in absence of hypovolemia or hyponatremia.
    [DRUG] Mannitol 20% (N/A) — Intracranial Hypertension
    [DRUG] Hypertonic saline 3% (N/A) — Intracranial Hypertension
    Avoid hyperosmolar Bicarbonates IV bolus, because of risk of cerebral edema except in life-threatening HyperK or severe cardiac dysfunction with pH < 6.9
## Subsequent management
  ### Maintenance
    Adjust maintenance type and rate according to local DKA protocols
    Balanced solution preferably unless if neuroprotection
    If blood glucose > 16 mmol/L: LR-KCl 40 mmol/L or NaCl 0.9%-KCl 40 mmol/L
    If blood glucose between 11-16 mmol/L: D5%-LR-KCl 40 mmol/L or D5%-NaCl 0.9%-KCl 40 mmol/L
    When blood sugar < 11 mmol/L: D10%-LR-KCl 40 mmol/L or D10%-NaCl 0.9%-KCl 40 mmol/L
