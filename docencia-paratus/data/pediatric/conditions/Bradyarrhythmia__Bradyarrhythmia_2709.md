# Bradyarrhythmia — Bradyarrhythmia

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Cardiovascular
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2709


## Tab: Default
## Initial medical management
    **ABC and rule out hypoxia/hypercapnia as a cause of bradyarrhythmia**
    Assessment for signs of hypoperfusion (altered mental status, hypotension, peripheral hypoperfusion)
    If no signs of hypoperfusion, search the etiology (12-lead ECG ± cardiology consultation)
## Heart rate < 60/min with hypoperfusion despite oxygenation and ventilation
  ### Follow PALS algorithm for bradycardia
    Start CPR
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (N/A) — Cardiac arrest
    [PROCEDURE] Resuscitation — Resuscitation
  ### If increased vagal tone or primary AV block
    [DRUG] Atropine (N/A) — Bradycardia
## 1st line chronotropy
    Consult pediatric cardiology and pediatric intensive care
  ### Normal or high blood pressure
    [DRUG] Isoproterenol (Infusion (Pump)) — Bradyarrhythmia
      IV infusion
  ### Low blood pressure or isoproterenol not available
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (Infusion (Pump)) — Cardiogenic and distributive shock
      Bradycardia with cardiogenic shock
    Echocardiography for evaluation of cardiac structures and function
## Refractory
    Consider transcutaneous, transvenous (if bigger patient) or transesophageal pacing
    Plan transfer to a specialized pediatric cardiac center with VA ECMO and electrophysiology
## Specific conditions
    [PROCEDURE] Hyperkalemia — Hyperkalemia
    Consider hypocalcemia or hypermagnesemia
    [DRUG] Calcium (Gluconate) (N/A) — Hypocalcemia, severe
    [DRUG] Calcium (Chloride) (N/A) — Hypocalcemia, severe
      Central line
    Consider other causes such as hypothermia, acidosis and hypothyroidism
    Consider toxicologic causes (BB, CCB, antiarrhythmics, clonidine, organophosphates, anticonvulsants)
