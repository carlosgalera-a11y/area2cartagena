## Airway and breathing (PO)
  ### Laryngitis
    [DRUG] Dexamethasone PO (N/A) — Laryngitis
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (N/A) — Nebulization
      Nebulization
  ### Asthma
    [DRUG] Salbutamol (Inhalation) — Acute severe asthma
    [DRUG] Ipratropium (Inhalation) — Acute Severe Asthma
    [DRUG] Dexamethasone PO (N/A) — Mild to severe acute asthma
    Favor Dexamethasone in asthma (shorter treatment and better tolerated)
    [DRUG] Prednisone (Mild to severe acute asthma) — Usual dosage
    [DRUG] Prednisolone (Mild to severe acute asthma) — Usual dosage
## Gastrointestinal
  ### Constipation
    [DRUG] PEG 3350 (Maintenance dose) — Constipation
    [DRUG] PEG 3350 (Bowel emptying) — Constipation
  ### Gastroesophageal reflux disease
    [DRUG] Lansoprazole (N/A) — Gastroesophageal reflux
    Oral solutions of ranitidine or famotidine not commercially available for pediatrics
    Prokinetics (metoclopramide or domperidone) not recommended as first-line treatment for GERD
  ### Gastroenteritis
    [DRUG] Ondansetron PO (N/A) — Nausea and vomiting
    Dimenhydrinate shown to be ineffective for gastroenteritis
    Antidiarrheal medications like loperamide should not be used
  ### Gastroenteritis (Mild to moderate dehydration)
    PO rehydration
  ### Gastroenteritis (Severe dehydration)
    IV rehydration: balanced solution preferably (LR or Plasmalyte)
    [DRUG] Lactate Ringer's (Non-cardiogenic shock) — Shock
    [DRUG] Plasmalyte (Non-cardiogenic shock) — Shock
    [DRUG] NaCl 0.9% (Non-cardiogenic shock) — Shock
    [DRUG] Maintenance fluids (Hyperhydration (150%)) — Maintenance fluids
## Pruritus
    Use cetirizine rather than diphenhydramine PO to avoid drowsiness and paradoxical reaction
    [DRUG] Cetirizine PO (6 months to < 2 years) — Pruritus secondary to anaphylaxis
    [DRUG] Cetirizine PO (2 to 5 years) — Pruritus secondary to anaphylaxis
    [DRUG] Cetirizine PO (> 5 years) — Pruritus secondary to anaphylaxis
    [DRUG] Hydroxyzine PO (> 6 months old) — Pruritus secondary to urticaria
