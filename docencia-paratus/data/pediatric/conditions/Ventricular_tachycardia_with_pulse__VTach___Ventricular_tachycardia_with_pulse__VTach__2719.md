- Categoría: Cardiovascular

## General measures
    ABC + 12-lead ECG
    Assessment for signs of hypoperfusion (altered mental status, hypotension, peripheral hypoperfusion)

## Stable
  ### If QRS is monomorphic and regular, consider a trial of adenosine before an antiarrhythmic (lidocaine, amiodarone or procainamide)
    [DRUG] Adenosine (1st dose) — Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)
    [DRUG] Adenosine (2nd dose) — Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)
    Consult pediatric cardiology and pediatric intensive care
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (Bolus) — VT with pulse/Torsade de pointes
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (Drip) — VT with pulse/Torsade de pointes
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (Drip) — VT with pulse/Torsade de pointes
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Amiodarone (Bolus) — VT with pulse/SVT (Pump)
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Amiodarone (Drip) — VT with pulse/SVT (Pump)
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Procainamide (Bolus) — VT with pulse/SVT (Pump)
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Procainamide (Drip) — VT with pulse/SVT (Pump)
      IV infusion
    Echocardiography for evaluation of cardiac structures and function
## Refractory
    Plan transfer to a specialized pediatric cardiac center with VA ECMO and electrophysiology
  ### Hypokalemia
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (Peripheral or central line) — Severe hypokalemia
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (Central line) — Severe hypokalemia
      Central line
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (Central line) — Severe hypokalemia
      Central line
  ### Hypomagnesemia
    [DRUG] Magnesium (Sulfate) (N/A) — Hypomagnesemia, severe and symptomatic
  ### Hypocalcemia
    [DRUG] Calcium (Gluconate) (N/A) — Hypocalcemia, severe
    [DRUG] Calcium (Chloride) (N/A) — Hypocalcemia, severe
      Central line
  ### Sympatholysis and temperature control
    [DRUG] Dexmedetomidine (N/A) — Sedation
      IV infusion
    Consider intubation and deep sedation/analgesia
    If cardiac function allows, minimize inotropes
  ### Unstable or cardiomyopathy
    Consider venoarterial ECMO if available
    Children, particularly those with cardiomyopathy, do not tolerate beta-blockers well. Use esmolol only under recommendations from pediatric intensive care or pediatric cardiology.
