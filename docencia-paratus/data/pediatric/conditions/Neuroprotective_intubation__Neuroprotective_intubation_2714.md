# Neuroprotective intubation — Neuroprotective intubation

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Respiratory
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2714


## Tab: Default
    Consider semi-elective neuroprotective intubation only if patient condition and time permits
    Do not tolerate hypoxia or hypotension If the patient is too unstable or the clinician is less comfortable, favor standard rapid sequence intubation instead
## Blood pressure control
  ### High blood pressure
    Systolic blood pressure treatment threshold > 15% of the 99th percentile
    Decrease the initial systolic blood pressure by a maximum of 15%
    [DRUG] Nicardipine (Infusion (Premixed bag)) — Targeted BP control
    [DRUG] Nicardipine (Infusion (Pump)) — Targeted BP control
    [DRUG] Clevidipine (N/A) — Targeted BP control
      IV infusion
    Avoid nitroglycerin and nitroprussiate, as they increase intracranial pressure (ICP)
  ### Low blood pressure
    Hypotension threshold (MAP)
    Hypotension threshold (systolic)
    Titrate clevidipine or nicardipine in anticipation of hypotension secondary to induction
    [DRUG] Norepinephrine (Infusion (Premixed bag)) — Shock
    [DRUG] Norepinephrine (Infusion (Pump)) — Shock
      Premixed bag not available (IV infusion)
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (N/A) — Push-Dose pressor
      Push-dose pressor
    Avoid atropine Loss of pupillary exam by mydriasis x 30-45 min
## Prevention of increase of intracranial pressure (ICP)
    [DRUG] Hypertonic saline 3% (N/A) — Intracranial Hypertension
## Prepare patient
    Pre-oxygenation with O2 100% x 3 min or more Ideally with high-flow nasal canula
    Consider apneic oxygenation with nasal cannula
    Minimize the duration where head of bed < 30°
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (N/A) — Premedication in rapid sequence intubation
      Premedication (wait for 3 min for effect)
    Consider topical airway anesthesia rather than IV lidocaine as premedication: Lidocaine 2% (<35 Kg) or Lidocaine 4% or 5% (>35 Kg)
## Induction
    [DRUG] Etomidate (N/A) — Intubation - Induction Agent
    [DRUG] Ketamine (N/A) — Intubation - Induction Agent
    [DRUG] Propofol (N/A) — Intubation - Induction Agent
## Neuromuscular blockade
    [DRUG] Rocuronium (Rapide sequence intubation) — Intubation- Neuromuscular Blocker
    [DRUG] Succinylcholine (Rapid sequence intubation (< 12 years)) — Intubation- Neuromuscular Blocker
    [DRUG] Succinylcholine (Rapid sequence intubation (> 12 years)) — Intubation- Neuromuscular Blocker
  ### Urgent reversal of rocuronium
    [DRUG] Sugammadex (N/A) — Urgent reversal of rocuronium
## Intubation
    Intubation with gentle placement and minimal glottic stimulation Ideally via videolaryngoscope by best intubator in the room

## Post-intubation analgesia
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (Bolus) — Post-intubation analgesia
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (Infusion (Pump)) — Post-intubation analgesia
      IV infusion
## Post-intubation sedation
    [DRUG] Propofol (mcg/kg/min) — Post-intubation sedation
    [DRUG] Propofol (mg/kg/h) — Post-intubation sedation
    Avoid analgosedation with agents with risk of accumulation or long t1/2 (e.g. morphine, midazolam) to allow neurological assessment
