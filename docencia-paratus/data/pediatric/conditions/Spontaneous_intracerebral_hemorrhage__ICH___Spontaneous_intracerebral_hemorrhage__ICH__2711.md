## Blood pressure control
    Systolic blood pressure treatment threshold > 15% of the 99th percentile
    Decrease the initial systolic blood pressure by a maximum of 15%
    [DRUG] Nicardipine (Infusion (Premixed bag)) — Targeted BP control
    [DRUG] Nicardipine (Infusion (Pump)) — Targeted BP control
    [DRUG] Clevidipine (N/A) — Targeted BP control
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Labetalol (N/A) — Targeted BP control (bolus)
      Bolus
    Avoid nitroglycerin and nitroprussiate, as they increase intracranial pressure (ICP)
  ### As a last resort if nicardipine/clevidipine not available and bradycardia with labetalol
    [DRUG] Phentolamine (Infusion (Pump)) — Targeted BP control
      IV infusion
## Analgesia
    [DRUG] Acetaminophen PO (15 mg/Kg q6h) — Pain or fever
    [DRUG] Acetaminophen IV (N/A) — Analgesia (IV)
    Avoid NSAID (e.g.: ibuprofen)
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (IV) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (Intranasal) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Morphine IV (Bolus) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Hydromorphone (N/A) — Analgesia
      IV
    Avoid ketamine to allow neurological examination without confounding factors
## Correction of coagulation
  ### Anticoagulation reversal
    [PROCEDURE] Anticoagulation reversal (major bleed) — Anticoagulation reversal (major bleed)
  ### On antiplatelet
    Avoid platelet transfusions EXCEPT in the perioperative context of emergency neurosurgery for hematoma evacuation
    [DRUG] Desmopressin (N/A) — Intracranial hemorrhage associated with antiplatelet agents
      IV bolus
## General measures
    Start neuroprotection (including BP control): Aim for glycemia 5-10 mmol/L, Sat O2 >94%, EtCO2/pCO2 35-45 mmHg (if intubated), T° 36-37.5°C, Na >140 mmol/L, avoid hyponatremia, treat fever, head of bed at 30° if possible
    No routine antiseizure prophylaxis Close clinical and EEG monitoring for seizure detection
## Intracranial hypertension
    [DRUG] Hypertonic saline 3% (N/A) — Intracranial Hypertension
    [DRUG] Mannitol 20% (N/A) — Intracranial Hypertension
  ### Consider neuroprotective intubation (semi-elective)
    Do not tolerate hypoxia or hypotension If the patient is too unstable or the clinician is less comfortable, favor standard rapid sequence intubation instead
    [PROCEDURE] Neuroprotective intubation — Neuroprotective intubation
