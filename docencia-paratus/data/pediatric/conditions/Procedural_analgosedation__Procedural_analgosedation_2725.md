# Procedural analgosedation — Procedural analgosedation

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Basics
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2725


## Tab: Default
## General measures
    Consider the presence of parents to reassure the child
    If available, evaluate and adjust the level of analgosedation with validated tools in pediatrics (e.g.: numeric rating scale/FACES, FLACC, COMFORT-B, RASS)
    Consider dose adjustment according to hemodynamic status
    [PROCEDURE] Local anesthesia — Local anesthesia
    [DRUG] Acetaminophen PO (15 mg/Kg q6h) — Pain or fever
      At least 30 min pre-painful procedure
  ### Validate the absence of contraindications to NSAIDs
    [DRUG] Ibuprofen PO (N/A) — Pain or fever
      At least 30 min pre-painful procedure
    [DRUG] Ketorolac (N/A) — Analgesia
## Procedural sedation
  ### No IV access
    [DRUG] Ketamine (Intranasal) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Midazolam (Intranasal) — Procedural sedation
    Intranasal dexmedetomidine is not recommended for routine procedural sedation because the onset of action is at least 20 minutes
  ### IV access
    [DRUG] Ketamine (IV) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Midazolam (IV) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Propofol (N/A) — Procedural sedation
    IV dexmedetomidine is not recommended as a bolus, because of the risk of severe bradycardia and the onset of action of the continuous IV infusion is too long to allow procedural sedation within 1 hour of initiation
## Analgesia
  ### No IV access
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (Intranasal) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Ketamine (Intranasal) — Procedural sedation
  ### IV access
    [DRUG] Fentanyl (IV) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Ketamine (IV) — Procedural sedation
    [DRUG] Morphine IV (Bolus) — Analgesia
    [DRUG] Hydromorphone (N/A) — Analgesia
      IV
## Hemodynamic support
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (N/A) — Push-Dose pressor
      Push-dose pressor
## Specific antidotes

      IV infusion
