# Torsade de pointes — Torsade de pointes

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Cardiovascular
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2721


## Tab: Default
## General measures
    ABC + 12-lead ECG
    Assessment for signs of hypoperfusion (altered mental status, hypotension, peripheral hypoperfusion)
## Without pulse
  ### Start CPR
    [PROCEDURE] Resuscitation — Resuscitation
    [DRUG] Magnesium (Sulfate) (N/A) — Pulseless VT/VF associated with torsade de pointes
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (N/A) — Pulseless VT/VF
    Avoid Amiodarone or Procainamide
## With pulse
    [DRUG] Magnesium (Sulfate) (Bolus) — Torsade de pointes (Polymorphic VT with pulse and prolonged QT)
    Stop all QT-prolonging medications
    Consult pediatric cardiology and pediatric intensive care
  ### Correct hypocalcemia
    [DRUG] Calcium (Gluconate) (N/A) — Hypocalcemia, severe
    [DRUG] Calcium (Chloride) (N/A) — Hypocalcemia, severe
      Central line
  ### Correct hypokalemia
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (Peripheral or central line) — Severe hypokalemia
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (Central line) — Severe hypokalemia
      Central line
    [DRUG] Potassium chloride (Central line) — Severe hypokalemia
      Central line
  ### Consider intoxication with sodium blockers (tricyclics, antiarrhythmics, lacosamide, phenytoin, etc.)
    [DRUG] Sodium bicarbonate (N/A) — Sodium channel blocker toxicity
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Sodium bicarbonate (N/A) — Sodium channel blocker toxicity
      Bolus
## Refractory
    [DRUG] Magnesium (Sulfate) (Bolus) — Torsade de pointes (Polymorphic VT with pulse and prolonged QT)
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Magnesium (Sulfate) (Drip 25 mg/kg/h) — Torsade de pointes (Polymorphic VT with pulse and prolonged QT)
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Magnesium (Sulfate) (Drip 25 mg/kg/h) — Torsade de pointes (Polymorphic VT with pulse and prolonged QT)
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (Bolus) — VT with pulse/Torsade de pointes
      Bolus
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (Drip) — VT with pulse/Torsade de pointes
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Lidocaine IV (Drip) — VT with pulse/Torsade de pointes
      IV infusion
## Long QTc (congenital or acquired) under recommendation of the pediatric electrophysiologist
  ### Positive chronotropy
    [DRUG] Isoproterenol (Infusion (Pump)) — Bradyarrhythmia
      Medical chronotropy
    [DRUG] Epinephrine (Infusion (Pump)) — Cardiogenic and distributive shock
      Medical chronotropy
    Electrical chronotropy (> 15-25 bpm over normal heart rate)
  ### Negative chronotropy
    [DRUG] Esmolol (N/A) — SVT
      Refractory VTach
