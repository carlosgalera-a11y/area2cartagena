# Bronchiolitis (Infant) — Bronchiolitis (Infant)

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Respiratory
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2710


## Tab: Default
## 1st line
    Nasal suctioning regularly
    **Standard oxygen therapy for Sat O2 >92%**
      Reserve high-flow nasal canula for patients with oxygenation problems and not to treat work of breathing
    No high-flow nasal canula to prevent PICU admission
  ### Nutrition
    Enteral nutrition with target of 120 mL/Kg/day (feeds or via discontinuous or continuous NG tube)
    If severe respiratory distress, D5%-NaCl 0.9% or D5%-LR maintenance
    [DRUG] Maintenance fluids (All critically ill children unless dehydrated (75%)) — Maintenance fluids
  ### Investigations for differential diagnosis or if critical patient
    Rapid viral test or viral PCR Identification to avoid antibiotic therapy
    Bacterial cultures, CBC, CRP or Procalcitonin If septic or fever in < 3 months old
    Chest X-ray If auscultatory asymmetry, heart murmur, atypical presentation or unexpected evolution
    Blood gas Initial contribution uncertain, instead adjust ventilation according to clinical examination
## 2nd line
    Routine intubation for transport is not recommend
    High-flow nasal cannula (HFNC): Lack of indication in bronchiolitis
    CPAP: Start PEEP at 7 cm H2O
    Consider BiPAP: Start Pressure Support at 5-10 cm H2O then titrate at the bedside
    [DRUG] Dexmedetomidine (N/A) — Sedation
      Premixed commercial vial at 4 mcg/mL
## Other considerations
    Supine position Consider prone positioning only for intubated patients with indications
    No routine antibiotics Reserve for suspected pulmonary superinfection or bacterial co-infection
    NaCl 3% nebulized and respiratory physiotherapy Not routine, only if atelectasia
    Therapies that have not demonstrated benefit:  Systemic or inhaled corticosteroids, salbutamol, nebulized epinephrine, Heliox, ribavirin, caffeine (even if apnea), IV magnesium
