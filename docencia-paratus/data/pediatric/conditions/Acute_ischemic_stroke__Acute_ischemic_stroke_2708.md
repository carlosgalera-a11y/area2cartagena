# Acute ischemic stroke — Acute ischemic stroke

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Neurological
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2708


## Tab: Default
## Blood pressure control
    Systolic blood pressure treatment threshold > 15% of the 99th percentile
    Decrease the initial systolic blood pressure by a maximum of 15%
    [DRUG] Nicardipine (Infusion (Premixed bag)) — Targeted BP control
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Nicardipine (Infusion (Pump)) — Targeted BP control
    [DRUG] Clevidipine (N/A) — Targeted BP control
      IV infusion
    [DRUG] Labetalol (N/A) — Targeted BP control (bolus)
      Bolus
    Avoid nitroglycerin and nitroprussiate, as they increase intracranial pressure (ICP)
  ### As a last resort if nicardipine/clevidipine not available and bradycardia with labetalol
    [DRUG] Phentolamine (Infusion (Pump)) — Targeted BP control
      IV infusion
## Reperfusion therapies
  ### Thrombolysis
    Consult pediatric neurology
    Validate the absence of contraindications to thrombolysis
    [DRUG] Alteplase (Step 1) — Acute ischemic stroke
      Step 1
    [DRUG] Alteplase (Step 2) — Acute ischemic stroke
      Step 2
    [DRUG] Tenecteplase (N/A) — Acute ischemic stroke
  ### Mechanical thrombectomy
    Consider thrombectomy if ≥ 5 years old with occlusion of a large vessel or if contraindication to thrombolysis
    Consult pediatric neurology and evaluate the feasibility in a referral center for interventional neuroradiology
## Other considerations
    Continue neuroprotection: Aim for blood glucose 5-10 mmol/L, Sat O2 >94%, pCO2 35-45 mmHg (if intubated), avoid hyponatremia, treat fever
## Post thrombolysis hemorrhage
    Stop thrombolysis if still ongoing
    [DRUG] Cryoprecipitate (N/A) — Massive hemorrhage
    [DRUG] Tranexamic acid (Bolus) — Hemorrhage (miscellaneous/non traumatic)
      Bolus
## Post-thrombolysis angioedema
  ### Treat according to severity
    [PROCEDURE] Angioedema — Angioedema
    Avoid tranexamic acid
    If IM epinephrine necessary, anticipate and treat hypertension to avoid secondary hemorrhages
