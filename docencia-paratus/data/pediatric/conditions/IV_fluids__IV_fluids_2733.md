# IV fluids — IV fluids

- Tipo: condition
- Categoría: Basics
- Entorno: pediatric
- Indication ID: 2733


## Tab: Default
    Balanced solution preferably unless contraindicated (ex.: Neuroprotection)
## Bolus
    Isotonic crystalloid bolus
  ### Fluid choice
    If metabolic acidosis, hyperchloremia or hyperkalemia, use Lactate Ringer's or Plasmalyte
    If intracranial hypertension, hypercalcemia or metabolic alkalosis, use NaCl 0.9% or Plasmalyte
## Maintenance
    Favor commercial IV solutions available locally. Risk of error with the compounding of IV solutions.
    [DRUG] Maintenance fluids (All critically ill children unless dehydrated (75%)) — Maintenance fluids
    [DRUG] Maintenance fluids (All healthy or dehydrated children (100%)) — Maintenance fluids
  ### Fluid choice
    **Potassium (KCl) is usually not necessary in initial solute**
      If in doubt, measure serum potassium before changing to a solution with KCl
    Avoid routine hypotonic solutions (ex.: NaCl 0.45%) unless Na > 145 mmol/L in the newborn
  ### < 1 month of life at term
    If newborns ≤ 72 hrs of life and NPO, use D10%-LR, D10%-NaCl 0.9% or D10%-NaCl 0.45% (if Na > 145)
    **To obtain D10% with electrolytes (ex.: D10%-NaCl 0.9%)**
      Add 50 mL of D50% to a 500 mL of D5% with electrolytes (ex.: D5%-NaCl 0.9%)
    If patient with PO intake, maintenance to keep the vein open or newborns > 72 hrs of life, use D5%-LR or D5%-NaCl 0.9%
    Closer monitoring of the ionogram and glycemia (maintain > 3.3 mmol/L) is recommended, particularly if glycemic intake is limited
  ### 1 month of life up to puberty or pubescent patient with prolonged fasting
    Use D5%-Lactated Ringer's or D5%-NaCl 0.9%
  ### Pubescent patient (excluding hepatic impairment and prolonged fasting)
    Use Lactated Ringer's, Plasmalyte or NaCl 0.9%
