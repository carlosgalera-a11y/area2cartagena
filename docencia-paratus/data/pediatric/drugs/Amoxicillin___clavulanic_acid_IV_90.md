# Amoxicillin / clavulanic acid IV (Clavulin®)
- Drug ID: 90
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Usual dosage
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      concentration note: Dose expressed in mg of amoxicillin
      administration: The Amox/Clav 5:1 formulation is not indicated for community acquired pneumonia or other suspected pneumococcal infections. A single dose of 50 mg/Kg, max 2 g with the 5:1 formulation is safe for the patient, but to limit side effects related to the clavulanic acid dose, favor the use of the Amoxicillin 2000 mg/Clavulanic Acid 200 mg formulation (Amox/Clav ratio 10:1).

Reconstitution:
1) Presentation: Vial of Amoxicillin 2000 mg/Clavulanic Acid 200 mg
2) Add 20 mL of sterile water
3) Concentration after reconstitution = 100 mg/mL in Amoxicillin. 
4) Draw the indicated dose and add to the indicated volume of NaCl 0.9%

Additional information:
• If different presentation, reconstitute according to the manufacturer's recommendations. (The concentration after reconstitution of the 5:1 formulation is 50 mg/mL.) 
• The primary dilution is stable 15 minutes after dilution.
• The primary dilution may be slightly pink, this coloration should disappear after shaking.
• The maximum concentration for administration, after further dilution, is 20 mg/mL.
      particularity: Incompatible with D5W. Y-site compatible with NaCl, LR.

The first dose of an anti-infective medication (mg) should not be influenced by the presence or absence of renal insufficiency in order to achieve an effective concentration quickly. If adjustments are necessary, these should be applied at subsequent doses.

Usual dose including pneumococcus (10:1 formulation):
> 3 months of life: 50 mg/Kg/dose IV q8h up to a maximum of 2g IV q8h
< 3 months of life: 50 mg/Kg/dose IV q12h
excluding premature patients and patients with reduced renal function.

Usual dosage (e.g.: non-severe ENT infections, cystitis) (Formulation 10:1 or 5:1)
> 3 months of life: 25 mg/Kg/dose IV q8h up to a maximum of 1g IV q8h
< 3 months of life: 25 mg/Kg/dose IV q12h
excluding premature patients and patients with reduced renal function.

Dose adjustment in renal failure. The 10:1 formulation has not been studied in renal impairment. In renal impairment, the clearance of amoxicillin is more affected than that of clavulanic acid. The ratio may possibly increase, so it seems safe to use the 5: 1 formulation.
