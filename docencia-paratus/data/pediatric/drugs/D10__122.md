## Indicación: Adjuvant to high dose IV insulin in toxicology
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  Administer on volume/time pump
      concentration: 0.1 g/mL
      administration: Start only if blood sugar levels below 22 mmol/L and aim for blood sugar levels above 5.5 mmol/L.

In the hemodynamically unstable patient, low blood sugar should be treated by increasing the dextrose intake and not by decreasing the insulin dose.

If D10% is unavailable:
Presentation : Vial of D50%
1) Withdraw 63 mL of D50%
2) Add to 500 mL bag of D5% 
3) Do NOT remove volume during preparation
4) Final concentration : D10%
5) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration 
6) Enter the required volume on the pump and administer over 5 minutes.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5%, NaCl, LR and KCl.

  - STEP: Drip
      concentration: 0.1 g/mL
      administration: • Start only if blood sugar levels below 22 mmol/L and aim for blood sugar levels above 5.5 mmol/L.
• D10% should only be used via the peripheral route in the absence of central access. In the presence of central venous access, use an infusion of D25%. Considering the significant flow rate of D10%, D25% via central access should be used as quickly as possible. 
• In the hemodynamically unstable patient, low blood sugar should be treated by increasing the dextrose intake and not by decreasing the insulin dose.
• The 2 active infusions (insulin and D10%) are usually at high flow rates and do not contain sodium. Consider close monitoring of serum sodium or the addition of a solute with sodium in Y.

## Indicación: Adjuvant to IV insulin in hyperkalemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

      concentration: 0.1 g/mL

• Ratio of 0.1 unit of regular insulin to 0.5 g of dextrose to avoid hypoglycemia
