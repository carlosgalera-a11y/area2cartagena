# Milrinone (Primacor®)
- Drug ID: 170
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Pulmonary hypertension (nebulization)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [Nebulization every 4 hours as needed]  Administer by nebulizer diluted up to 2-5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: • Study demonstrates ineffectiveness if administered with standard (jet) nebulizer vs mesh nebulizer.
• In a pulmonary hypertension crisis in patients intubated before cardiac arrest, milrinone can be administered directly into the endotracheal tube followed by a flush of 1-2 mL (< 10 Kg) or 2-5 mL (≥ 10 Kg) of NaCl 0.9% and by manual ventilations with a bag.
• Administrer at a minimal O2 flow rate of 1 L/min
      particularity: • NO remains the treatment of choice for pulmonary hypertension in pediatrics; prioritize this treatment if available.
• Despite significant systemic absorption, inhaled milrinone causes less vasodilator effect than continuous IV infusion, according to studies combining pharmacokinetics and cardiac ultrasound measurements.
• In addition to pulmonary vasodilator effects, inhaled milrinone has positive inotropic effects.
• Inhaled milrinone may be combined with inhaled epoprostenol treatment; alternating administration every 2 hours is recommended in this situation.

## Indicación: Cardiogenic Shock
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Infusion (Pump)
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Suggested titration:
Milrinone has a half-life of 1.9-3.2 hours; unlike beta-agonists such as epinephrine, its titration q5-15min is not recommended. In the absence of hypotension, start at 0.5 mcg/Kg/min and adjust upwards in 4-6 hours. (Expert Opinion)

Additional information:
• Do NOT remove volume during preparation
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration
• The loading dose of milrinone is rarely indicated outside the operating room.
• Adjust the dose according to renal function
• If significant fluid restriction, possibility of concentrating the infusion to 500 mcg/mL by adding 50 mg (50 mL of milrinone 1 mg/mL) to 50 mL of NaCl 0.9%.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR. 
If patient with reduced dextrose intake, very at risk of hypoglycaemia or with a history of hypoglycaemia, dilute in D5% rather than NaCl 0.9%.

Monitor for hypotension.
