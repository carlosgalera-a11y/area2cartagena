# Glucagon (Glucagen® or Baqsimi®)
- Drug ID: 139
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Mild beta-blocker toxicity
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9% after reconstitution
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: The efficacy of a glucagon infusion is not supported by evidence. Contact your local poison center.

Reconstitution :
Presentation: Vial of Glucagon 1 mg
1) Add 1 mL of included diluent or D5W
2) Concentration after reconstitution = 1 mg/mL
3) Draw the required dose
      particularity: Keep refrigerated.
Stability in NaCl 0.9% is controversial; if a drip is indicated by your local poison center, prepare in D5W.

## Indicación: Hypoglycemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: IM or subcutaneous  [Administer IM or subcutaneous]
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: • Administer only in severe hypoglycemia and if IV dextrose is not available or in absence of IV access. Consider IO access to administer dextrose depending on the clinical situation, as glucagon takes at least 10-15 min to act.
• Can be given IV, but if the IV route is available, dextrose should be given instead.
• Hypoglycemia in pediatrics is usually ketogenic with lowered hepatic glucose stores; glucagon will be of little effectiveness in this clinical context. 
• Effective in the treatment of insulin-dependent hypoglycemia.
      particularity: • A 2nd dose can be repeated after 15 minutes PRN.
• May cause nausea and vomiting, especially with repeated doses

## Indicación: Anaphylaxis in patient using beta-blocker
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 5 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Reconstitution :
Presentation: Vial of Glucagon 1 mg
1) Add 1 mL of sterile water
2) Concentration after reconstitution = 1 mg/mL

• More rapid administration may induce vomiting.
• Due to the short half-life of glucagon (approximately 6.6 minutes), an infusion is often necessary following the bolus.
      particularity: • Should only be used for epinephrine refractory anaphylaxis in patients on beta-blockers.
• The use of glucagon as a bolus or continuous infusion is recommended by national anaphylaxis experts of different countries, although the literature is limited on this subject.

  - STEP: Infusion (Pump)
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Reconstitution :
Presentation: Vial of Glucagon 1 mg
1) Add 1 mL of sterile water
2) Concentration after reconstitution = 1 mg/mL

• Start the infusion at 5 mcg/min; titrate according to hemodynamic response.
• Do NOT remove volume during preparation
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration
• Stability in NaCl 0.9% is controversial.
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W. Lack of compatibility data with NaCl 0.9%, epinephrine and other medications; ideally administer via another route.
• In poisoning, doses up to 2.5 mcg/Kg/min (0.15 mg/Kg/h) or 166.6 mcg/min (10 mg/h) have been used.
• Should only be used for epinephrine refractory anaphylaxis in patients on beta-blockers.
• The use of glucagon as a bolus or continuous infusion is recommended by national anaphylaxis experts of different countries, although the literature is limited on this subject.
