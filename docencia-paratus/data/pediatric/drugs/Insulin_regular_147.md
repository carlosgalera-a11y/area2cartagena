# Insulin regular (Toronto® ou Humulin R®)
- Drug ID: 147
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Hyperkalemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 1 min]  Administer IV DILUTED
      concentration: 1 unités/mL
      concentration note: 1 unit/mL premixed bag
      administration: If premixed bag of insulin 1 unit/mL not available: 

Presentation: Vial Insulin REGULAR 100 units/mL
1) Use a 250 mL bag of NaCl 0.9%
2) Add 250 units of insulin (2.5 mL of Insulin 100 units/mL) to the bag, WITHOUT removing any volume.
3) Final concentration = 250 units in 252.5 mL ≈ 1 unit/mL.
4) To mix the solution and saturate the adsorption of insulin to the PVC of the bag, vigorously invert the bag 10 times.
5) For a dose of 0.1 units/Kg, administer 0.1 mL/Kg of the solution diluted at 1 unit/mL over 1 min.

In order to reduce calculation and compounding errors, the final insulin concentration used is 1 unit/mL rather than 0.99 unit/mL. (Expert opinion) 

Additional information:
• In doubt, control venous K+ level and look for anomalies on the 12-lead EKG.
• Administer with D10% or D25% to avoid hypoglycemia. (See D10% or D25% Adjuvant to IV insulin in hyperkalemia). 
• It is possible to mix regular insulin and D10% before administration in paediatrics
• Ratio of 0.1 unit of Insulin for 0.5 g of Dextrose to avoid hypoglycemia
• Close blood glucose monitoring; post-insulin hypoglycaemia may occur up to 4-6 hours after the bolus of insulin, particularly in patients with renal impairment.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5%, NaCl, LR and KCl.
Bag or vial stable for 25 days at room temperature when removed from the refrigerator.

## Indicación: CCB and BB intoxication
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 1 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 100 unités/mL
      administration: • Always start a drip after the bolus
• Administer with dextrose if blood glucose levels below 22 mmol/L (see D25% if central access available or D10% if peripheral access only)
• In the hemodynamically unstable patient, low blood sugar should be treated by increasing the dextrose intake and not by decreasing the insulin dose.
• Monitor potassium levels
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5%, NaCl, LR and KCl.

Suggested titration:
1) After the bolus of 1 unit/Kg, start the drip at 1 unit/Kg/h
2) Increase by 0.5units/kg/h every 15 minutes depending on clinical response. 
3) Titrate to maintain a systolic BP and a pulse above lower age-set target.

Additional information:

• When the dose exceeds 2 units/Kg/h or before if clinical signs of fluid overload, it is recommended to transition to the higher concentration preparation of insulin (16unitsmL).
• Monitor potassium levels

## Indicación: Diabetic ketoacidosis
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Infusion (Pump)
      concentration: 100 unités/mL
      administration: In pediatric diabetic ketoacidosis, the 0.05 units/kg/h insulin dose has been shown to be equivalent to the 0.1 units/kg/h dose over the duration of the acidosis and causes less hypokalemia. Reserve the 0.1 units/kg/h insulin dose for refractory cases or special situations in collaboration with pediatric endocrinology or pediatric intensive care.
 
Additional information:
• Do NOT remove volume during preparation
• Make 2 purges of tubing to saturate the PVC of the tubing with the insulin before connecting to the patient and starting the administration.
• Do NOT prepare in a 50 mL syringe for syringe pump; excessive adsorption of insulin to PVC.
      particularity: Treatment of acidosis related to ketone bodies: consider IV dextrose in case of hypoglycemia instead of decreasing insulin infusion.

Y-site compatible with D5%, NaCl, LR. 
Keep refrigerated.
