# Sodium Phenylacetate/Benzoate (Ammonul®)
- Drug ID: 245
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Urea cycle disorder with hyperammonemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 90 min]  Administer IV DILUTED
      concentration: 9.09 mg/mL
      administration: • Administer preferably via proximal peripheral venous access if central access not available (hyperosmolar solution).
• Collect venous blood ammonia on ice to avoid falsely elevated values ​​due to haemolysis.

DILUTED 9.09 mg/mL solution

Further dilution for administration via pump:
Presentation: Vial of Phenylacetate/Benzoate of Na 100 mg/mL (10%) (5000 mg in 50 mL).
1) Use a 500 mL bag of D5W (bolus) or D10W (drip)
2) Add 50 mL (1 vial) of Phenylacetate/Benzoate of Na 100 mg/mL to the bag, WITHOUT removing volume, using the 0.22 micron filter provided with the vial.
3) Final concentration = 5000 mg in 550 mL = 9.09 mg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the solution before starting the administration
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR, Plasmalyte, KCl.
• Final sodium concentration of 133 mmol/L.
• Drug available through Health Canada's Special Access Program (Not marketed in Canada)
• In hyperammonaemia secondary to hepatic insufficiency of origin other than urea cycle disorder, use Sodium Benzoate without Phenylacetate and Arginine. In both situations, for severe cases, Hemodialysis or CRRT should be use to clear ammonia.
• Medications to avoid or use with caution in patients with urea cycle disorder (non-exhaustive list, to be discussed with geneticist if necessary): corticosteroids, valproic acid and haloperidol.

Further dilution for administration via pump:
1) Prepare a DILUTED 9.09 mg/mL solution
2) Do NOT remove volume during preparation
3) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting the administration
4) After the bolus, start the continuous infusion at 250 mg/Kg/day

• Can be diluted in D5W, but recommendations for the management of hyperglycaemia in decompensated genetic disease are usually in favor of treatment with insulin.
