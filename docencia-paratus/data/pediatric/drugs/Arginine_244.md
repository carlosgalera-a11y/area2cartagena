# Arginine (L-arginine)
- Drug ID: 244
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Stroke-like episode in MELAS patient
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 60 min]  Administer IV DILUTED
      concentration: 26.8 mg/mL
      administration: In the majority of episodes in known patients with MELAS (Mitochondrial encephalomyopathy, lactic acidosis and stroke-like episodes), no imaging is urgently required and should not delay the administration of the loading dose of arginine.

DILUTED 26.8 mg/mL solution

Further dilution for administration via pump:
Presentation: Vial of Arginine 250 mg/mL (7500 mg in 30 mL).
1) Use a 500 mL bag of D5W
2) Add 60 mL (2 vials) of Arginine 250 mg/mL to the bag, WITHOUT removing volume
3) Final concentration = 15000 mg in 560 mL = 26.8 mg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting the administration
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR, Plasmalyte, KCl.
• Drugs to avoid or use with caution in patients with MELAS (non-exhaustive list, to be discussed with geneticist if necessary);
triptans, phenobarbital, benzodiazepines, topiramate, propofol, neuromuscular blockers, aminoglycosides, salbutamol, metformin, statins, beta-blockers

## Indicación: Urea cycle disorder with hyperammonemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 90 min]  Administer IV undiluted with syringe pump
      concentration: 250 mg/mL
      administration: • Administer preferably via proximal peripheral venous access if central access not available (hyperosmolar solution).
• If a syringe pump not available, administer on pump at the same rate directly from the vial.
• Collect venous blood ammonia on ice to avoid falsely elevated values ​​due to haemolysis.
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR, Plasmalyte, KCl.
• Medications to avoid or use with caution in patients with urea cycle disorder (non-exhaustive list, to be discussed with geneticist if necessary): corticosteroids, valproic acid and haloperidol.
