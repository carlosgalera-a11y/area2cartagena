# Tenecteplase (TNKase®)
- Drug ID: 201
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Acute ischemic stroke
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV push]  Administer IV undiluted after reconstitution
      concentration: 5 mg/mL
      administration: • Not indicated in children < 10 years old or if onset of symptoms not defined or > 4.5 hours.
• Validate the absence of contraindications with the neurologist before stroke thrombolysis.
• Pediatric doses of tenecteplase and superiority over alteplase are based on extrapolation from adult studies and consensus of local experts including pediatric vascular neurologists and pediatric intensivists. If both thrombolytic agents are available, confirm the choice of agent with the neurologist; otherwise, administer the thrombolytic agent available as soon as possible.
• Pediatric stroke usually requires radiological confirmation, either by CT or CT-angiography (normal parenchyma or with minimal ischemic change AND partial or complete occlusion of the corresponding artery) OR by MRI (acute stroke on diffusion sequences) combined with angio-MRI (partial or complete occlusion of the corresponding artery)

Reconstitution:

Presentation: vial of Tenecteplase 50 mg
Refer to the monograph for detailed reconstitution
1) Using a 10 mL syringe, take 10 mL of sterile water for injection. 
2) Inject gently the sterile water into the tenecteplase vial.
3) Mix the solution with a gentle swirl to make sure the product is completely dissolved, DO NOT shake
4) Concentration after reconstitution = 5 mg/mL
      particularity: Y-site incompatible with D5%, LR or any other solutions. Only compatible with NaCl 0.9%.

Contraindications (Non-exhaustive list, see monograph)

Patient-related:
- Weight < 40 Kg
- Systolic BP >15% of the 99th percentile or > 185 mmHg despite treatment
- Current use of anticoagulants (heparin (< 48 hrs with abnormal aPTT), enoxaparin (< 24 hrs), dalteparin (< 24 hrs), warfarin, dabigatran, rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban)
- INR > 1.7, platelets <100 x 10^9/L, aPTT > 40 sec, PT > 15 sec, fibrinogen < 1 g/L despite treatment
- Active coagulation disorder (e.g. Von Willebrand disease)
- Glycemia < 4 mmol/L or > 22 mmol/L despite treatment
- Neoplasia (< 1 month after completion of treatment)
- Anaphylaxis due to tenecteplase 

Presentation related:
- Onset of symptoms not defined or > 4.5 hours
- Presentation suggestive of subarachnoid hemorrhage (even if CT-scan without bleeding)
- Stroke related to infectious endocarditis, meningitis, moyamoya, sickle cell anemia, fat or gas embolism
- Presentation suggestive of myocardial infarction or post-infarction pericarditis
- Active internal bleeding

Related to imagery
- Hypodensity > 1/3 cerebral hemisphere on CT-scan
- Intracranial bleeding
- Intracranial dissection (defined as involvement at or distal to the ophthalmic artery)
- Arterial or venous intracerebral malformation, aneurysm or cerebral neoplasm

History-related:
- History of vasculitis or vasculitis of the primary or secondary central nervous system
- In the last 7 days: arterial puncture on a non-compressible site
- In the last 14 days: childbirth, major surgery or biopsy of parenchyma
- In the last 21 days: digestive or urinary bleeding
- In the last 3 months: intracranial or intraspinal surgery, severe head trauma, intracranial hemorrhage, ischemic stroke
