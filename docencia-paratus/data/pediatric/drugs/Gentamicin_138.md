# Gentamicin (Garamycin®)
- Drug ID: 138
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Sepsis
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Less than 1 month  [IV over 3-5 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      concentration note: Different concentrations available
      administration: Caution:
• Before the administration of the 2nd dose, validate the dose and dosage interval according to gestational age and postnatal age with a specialist in pediatrics or neonatology.
• Usual dose for infants (< 1 month): 3 mg/Kg/dose IV q12-18h (0-7 days: q18h, 8-28 days: q12h), excluding patients < 40 weeks (See Neonatal environnement) and patients with reduced renal function.

Additional information:
• IV administration over 3-5 minutes is safe according to the literature.
• It is possible to administer the drug over a longer period according to the hospital protocols in place.
• Secondary dilution not necessary.
• Can be administered IM.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR. 

Because of the risk of nephrotoxicity, consider another class of antibiotics in patients with CrCl less than 30 mL/min or in acute renal failure.
Dosage adjustment in renal failure.

The first dose of an anti-infective medication (mg) should not be influenced by the presence or absence of renal insufficiency in order to achieve an effective concentration quickly. If adjustments are necessary, these should be applied at subsequent doses.

## Indicación: Urinary tract infection
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: More than 1 month
      concentration: 40 mg/mL
      administration: Additional information:
• Once-daily administration is only indicated for the population > 1 month old WITH non-complicated and isolated urinary tract infection (exclude systemic, neurological involvement, dehydration) 
• Usual dosing: 5-7,5 mg/Kg IV q24h up to a maximum of 350 mg IV q24h excluding premature babies and patients with reduced renal function. 
• For patients > 5 years of age, the dose of 5 mg/Kg is usually sufficient to achieve urinary concentrations of 5-10 x the MIC
• Administration over 30 min to decrease ototoxicity and nephrotoxicity

If 25 mL bag not available
1) Remove 25 mL from a 50 mL NaCl 0.9% bag
2) Add the indicated dose
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR. 

Because of the risk of nephrotoxicity, consider another class of antibiotics in patients with CrCl less than 30mL/min or in acute renal failure.
Dosage adjustment in renal failure.

The first dose of an anti-infective medication (mg) should not be influenced by the presence or absence of renal insufficiency in order to achieve an effective concentration quickly. If adjustments are necessary, these should be applied at subsequent doses.
