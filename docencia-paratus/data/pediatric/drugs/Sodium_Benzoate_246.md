# Sodium Benzoate ({"fr":null})
- Drug ID: 246
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Hyperammonemia in non-genetic liver failure
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 90 min]  Administer IV DILUTED
      concentration: 9.09 mg/mL
      administration: • For severe cases of hyperammonemia, hemodialysis or CRRT should be used to remove ammonia. Sodium benzoate can be used as a temporary treatment until the initiation of extrarenal replacement in a specialized pediatric center.
• Drug not marketed in Canada in its IV formulation; the stock solution of sodium benzoate 100 mg/mL must be prepared in advance by the pharmacy from a powder. (preparation with filtration too long to allow treatment of hyperammonaemia in hepatic insufficiency in a timely manner.)
• If sodium benzoate is not available, but phenylacetate/benzoate of sodium is, administer the bolus in the form of phenylacetate/benzoate of sodium pending transfer to a referral centre. (See entry Urea cycle disorder with hyperammonemia)
• Administer preferably via proximal peripheral venous access if central access not available (hyperosmolar solution).
• Collect venous blood ammonia on ice to avoid falsely elevated values ​​due to haemolysis.

DILUTED 9.09 mg/mL solution

Further dilution for administration via pump:
Presentation: Vial of Benzoate of Na 100 mg/mL (10%) (5000 mg in 50 mL).
1) Use a 500 mL bag of D5W (bolus) or D10W (drip)
2) Add 50 mL (1 vial) of Benzoate of Na 100 mg/mL to the bag, WITHOUT removing volume, using the 0.22 micron filter.
3) Final concentration = 5000 mg in 550 mL = 9.09 mg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the solution before starting the administration
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR, Plasmalyte, KCl.
• Final sodium concentration of 70 mmol/L.
