# Propofol (Diprivan®)
- Drug ID: 193
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Post-intubation sedation
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: mcg/kg/min  Administer IV undiluted directly from the vial
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: Caution:
• Avoid if metabolic disease suspected
• Continous sedation with propofol is still a contraindication in children under 16 years old according to Health Canada, but the benefits can outweight the risks when used for less than 48-72 hours to avoid accumulation of other sedative agents such as midazolam or morphine. (neurologic assessment)

Additional information:
• Lipid emulsion: do NOT dilute
• Use the bottle directly with a ventilated tubing
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration

1) Start the infusion at a rate ranging from 30-50 mcg/kg/min
2) Titrate by increments of 5-10 mcg/kg/min q5-10 min until the desired level of sedation is achieved. (Expert opinion)

N.B. Increase starting dose and titrate faster depending on clinical picture
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR.
• Propofol as a continuous infusion is not a first-line agent in pediatrics because of the risk of Propofol Infusion Syndrome (PRIS). If benefits > risks compared to therapeutic alternatives, make sure to measure lactates, CK, triglycerides and blood gas at least daily, as well as to reduce the duration of exposure to a minimum. The change in urine color, typically green, is not related to the risk of PRIS and is considered benign.
• Use minimal required dose for adequate sedation. The use of a standardized tool for the evaluation of sedation depth in intubated patients is strongly recommended (ex: RASS).
• Ensure adequate analgesia.
• Monitor for hypotension.

## Indicación: Refractory Status Epilepticus
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: mcg/kg/min  Administer IV undiluted directly from the vial
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: Caution:
• Avoid if metabolic disease suspected
• Consider another agent, such as high-dose midazolam and/or ketamine infusion, for patients < 16 years of age due to risk of propofol infusion syndrome (PRIS); risks are increased with doses > 5 mg/Kg/h (> 83 mcg/Kg/min) given for > 48 hours

Additional information:
• Lipid emulsion: do NOT dilute
• Use the bottle directly with a ventilated tubing
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration
      particularity: Under EEG monitoring.
Have equipment and personnel ready for airway management.

Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR.

Propofol as a continuous infusion is not a first-line agent in pediatrics because of the risk of Propofol Infusion Syndrome (PRIS). If benefits > risks compared to therapeutic alternatives, make sure to measure lactates, CK, triglycerides and blood gas at least daily, as well as to reduce the duration of exposure to a minimum. The change in urine color, typically green, is not related to the risk of PRIS and is considered benign.
