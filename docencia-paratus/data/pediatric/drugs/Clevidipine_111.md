# Clevidipine (Cleviprex®)
- Drug ID: 111
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Targeted BP control
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer IV undiluted directly from the vial
      concentration: 0.5 mg/mL
      administration: Caution:
• Avoid if suspected congenital abnormality of lipid metabolism or history of anaphylaxis to eggs or soy.
• Use with caution if patient with pancreatitis or severe acute hepatic failure.

Suggested titration:
1) Start infusion at 0.25-0.5 mcg/Kg/min
2) Titrate 0.25-0.5 mcg/Kg/min q5min until target BP is reached (onset of action 2-4 min; rapid titration q5min or more gradual q10min)
3) Maximum dose of 10 mcg/Kg/min or 350 mcg/min, i.e. 21 mg/h.

Additional information:
• 20% Lipid emulsion: do NOT dilute
• Use the bottle directly with a ventilated tubing
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration
• Administer through a peripheral or central IV access. 
• Very limited IV compatibility, preferably administered alone in one line.
• The majority of patients achieve blood pressure control with doses < 5 mcg/Kg/min.
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR.
• Discard the vial and infusion set or replace them after 12 hours when connected to the patient.
• Protect from light during storage; not necessary when administered to patient.
• Store in the refrigerator; stable for 2 months when placed at room temperature
• Metabolized by plasma esterases; no adjustment for renal insufficiency.
• The monograph mentions the potential negative inotropic effects of clevidipine without providing clinical data; adult and pediatric clinical studies, particularly post-op of cardiac surgeries, do not demonstrate these effects and the specific dihydropyridine mechanism of action, like nicardipine, does not suggest negative inotropy with clevidipine at therapeutic doses.
• A dose of 1 mcg/Kg/min provides approximately 5.75 Kcal/Kg/day in the form of 20% lipids. The maximum adult dose of 21 mg/h provides approximately 2000 Kcal/day. Monitor triglycerides if high doses for > 24 hours, especially if administered concurrently with propofol or parenteral nutrition. (Expert opinion)
