# Nitrite (sodium) (NaNO₂) (Nithiodote®)
- Drug ID: 304
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Cyanide intoxication
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 5 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 30 mg/mL
      administration: • Administer in combination with sodium thiosulfate as a replacement for hydroxocobalamin in cyanide poisoning. Sodium nitrite and sodium thiosulfate can be administered in the same IV line. 
• May induce hypotension. Consider administering over > 5 min if the patient is hemodynamically unstable. 
• Measure methemoglobin levels 30–60 minutes post-administration, targeting a level between 20% and 30%. A half-dose of sodium nitrite may be repeated after 30 minutes if methemoglobin levels remain below the target range, after discussion with your poison control center. If methemoglobin >30%, contact the poison control center to validate the use of methylene blue. 
• The dose of 6 mg/kg, not adjusted for hemoglobin, is considered safe for patients with hemoglobin >70 g/L. Do not delay administration for hemoglobin results, but contact your poison control center if Hb <70 g/L post-administration.
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with sodium thiosulfate. Incompatible with hydroxocobalamin, NaCl, D5%, and LR (no data with NaCl, D5%, and LR).
• The antidote of choice for treating cyanide poisoning is hydroxocobalamin.
• If combined carbon monoxide poisoning is suspected, contact your poison control center to discuss the risks and benefits.
