# Botulism Immunoglobulin (BIG-IV) (BabyBIG®)
- Drug ID: 303
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Botulism (< 1 year)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer IV diluted after reconstitution with a 18 micron filter
      concentration: 50 mg/mL
      administration: Additional information: 
• If syringe pump not available, administer with low volume tubing or piggyback the entire dose + NaCl 0.9% into a preexisting line containing NaCl 0.9%.
 
• Treatment of infantile botulism (< 1 year) caused by toxin type A or B
• Medication only available through Health Canada's Special Access Program through the California Department of Health Services' Infant Botulism Treatment and Prevention Program (510-231-7600). In Canada, immunoglobulins must be managed by the Blood Bank; contact your local Blood Bank for authorization and ordering of the product.
• It is recommended to take samples for toxin detection before giving treatment, but do not wait for confirmation of toxins to begin treatment.  

Reconstitution:
Presentation: Vial of Botulism immunoglobulin 100 mg
1) Add 2 mL of sterile water
2) Mix gently, roll between hands until completely dissolved to avoid bubble formation. DO NOT shake the bottle. 
3) Let dissolve for around 30 min. 
4) Concentration after reconstitution = 50 mg/mL
5) Further dilute with NaCl 0.9%
6) Start administration within 2 hours after the reconstitution
7) Rinse tubing with NaCl 0.9% after administration at same rate
      particularity: • Keep refrigerated. Stable 4 hours after reconstitution
• Y-site compatible with NaCl. No compatibility data with other solutions or other medications.
• Contains 1% of human albumin
• Risk of allergic or infusion reaction during administration, renal failure (urea/creatinine pre-administration) and aseptic meningitis (like all immunoglobulins, neurological clinical monitoring).
