# Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)
- Drug ID: 189
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Massive hemorrhage
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Rate varies according to indication
      administration: Additional information:
• Repeat according to clinical response
• In early massive hemorrhage, favor transfusing blood products at a ratio of 1:1:1 (Plasma: Platelets: RBCs)
• If INR > 1.8 AND absence of bleeding, opinion of pediatric hematology or pediatric intensive care recommended
• 1 unit of FFP = approximately 250 mL
• May be administered over 3 hours if patient is stable

## Indicación: Angioedema, bradykinin-mediated
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administration rate varies depending on clinical situation
      administration: • Administer according to local protocols and the urgency of the situation.
• 1 unit of FFP = approximately 250 mL
      particularity: • The dose can be repeated 2-4 hours later if symptoms persist.
• Therapies for angioedema should never delay securing the airway.
• For hereditary angioedema, use FFP only as 2nd line if first line therapies are not available (C1 esterase inhibitor, icatibant, ecallantide).
• Controversial in ACE inhibitor induced angioedema. Data limited to case reports.

## Indicación: Vitamin K antagonist (VKA) reversal in patients with acute major bleeding
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administration rate varies depending on clinical situation
      administration: Use only if prothrombin complex concentrates (4-factor) are not available or contraindicated since fresh frozen plasma takes longer to administer, may cause transfusion reactions and has been shown to be inferior in the literature.

 Administer according to local protocols and the urgency of the situation. Can be administered flush with pressure bag in case of major hemorrhage. 
 
Opinion of pediatric hematology recommended.

1 unit of FFP = approximately 250 mL
      particularity: • Administer 20 mL/Kg ( up to 4 units) empirically, or 10 mL/Kg (up to 2 units) and repeat dose if INR remains above 1.5.
• Measure INR 15 minutes after the end of plasma administration.
• Complete reversal of warfarin with FFP typically requires 30 mL/Kg.
• Must be administered with IV Vitamin K (5 or 10 mg depending on weight) in case of major bleeding.
• Consider furosemide before, half way or immediately after plasma administration in patients at risk for volume overload.
• Consider calcium administration or close monitoring with repletion of calcium as need.
