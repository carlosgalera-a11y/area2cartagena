# Sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO3)
- Drug ID: 96
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Sodium channel blocker toxicity
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 1-2 min]
      concentration: 0.5 mmol/mL
      administration: Administer preferably through a proximal IV access (Hyperosmolar solution)

If Sodium Bicarbonate 4.2% not available:
Presentation : Sodium Bicarbonate 8.4% 1mmol/ml
1) Dilute 10mmol (10ml) with 10ml of sterile water or D5%
2) Final concentration = 4.2%
3) Draw the required dose
4) Administer IV push

Use sodium Bicarbonate solution at 1 mmol/mL (8.4%) for patients of 2 years old or more
      particularity: Rinse with NaCl 0.9% before and after administration (incompatible with calcium and epinephrine).
May be administered diluted IV over 30 minutes if patient is stable.

## Indicación: Urinary alkalization
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 1 mmol/mL
      administration: • Final concentration: 130 mmol/L
• Do NOT remove volume during preparation
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting the administration
• Titrate the infusion to obtain a urinary pH greater than 7.5
• The dose of 0.26-0.40 mmol/Kg/h represents a significant amount of volume of 2-3 mL/Kg/h and can partially or totally replace the maintenance fluids.
      particularity: Keep the serum potassium over 4 mmol/L
Close monitoring of blood pH in addition to urine pH.

## Indicación: Hyperkalemia with acidosis
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Hemodynamic instability  [IV over 2-5 min]  Administer IV undiluted
      concentration: 0.5 mmol/mL
      administration: IV push in cardiac arrest

If the patient is hemodynamically stable, administer diluted over 30 minutes to reduce the risk of cerebral hemorrhage and better efficacy. (Hyperosmolar undiluted bicarbonate causes K+ to move extracellularly secondary to the hyperosmolar effect, thus negating the effect on K+ due to pH)

Administer preferably via proximal peripheral venous access if central access not available (hyperosmolar solution).

Neonates often tolerate blood levels of K+ higher than children and adults. In doubt, control the K+ venous level and look for anomalies on the 12-lead EKG.

If Sodium Bicarbonate 4.2% (0.5mmol/mL) is unavailable:
Presentation : Vial or syringe of Sodium Bicarbonate 8.4% (1 mmol/mL)
1) Withdraw 10 mL of Bicarbonate 1 mmol/mL with a 20 mL syringe
2) Add to 10 mL of Sterile water for injection or D5W to the syringe
3) Final concentration = 10 mmol in 20 mL = 0.5 mmol/mL = 4.2%
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with NaCl 0.9%, D5%, KCl. Incompatible with Lactate Ringer.
• Multiple drug incompatibilities. Sodium Bicarbonate inactivates vasopressors and catecholaminergic type inotropes (Ex.: Epinephrine, Norepinephrine)
• Incompatible in Y with Calcium or Epinephrine. Rinse well with NaCl 0.9% between administrations
• No benefit reported in the literature of undiluted hypertonic bicarbonates compared to isotonic bicarbonates in the treatment of hyperkalemia.
• Potentially effective therapy only in the presence of metabolic acidosis. Does not replace other therapies known to be effective in hyperkalemia (insulin, B2-agonists, calcium, etc.).
• If intraosseous administration, interference with the blood gas if drawn in the same access.

## Indicación: Non-anion-gap metabolic acidosis (NAGMA) or 2nd to AKI
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Maintenance fluids
      concentration note: NaHCO3 DILUTED at 130 mmol/L
      administration: • Isotonic bicarbonate solution at 130 mmol/L at this initial rate provides fluid intake according to pediatric maintenance needs (4-2-1 Rule) up to a maximum of 80-100 mL/h.
• Adjust the rate according to pH, volume status and the clinical situation.
• Addition of another Y-solute is not recommended, as the patient will receive 200% of their baseline fluid intake.

Further dilution for final concentration at 130 mmol/L:
Presentation: Sodium Bicarbonate Syringe or vial 8.4% (1 mmol/mL)
1) Add 150 mL of Sodium bicarbonate to a 1L bag of D5W, WITHOUT removing volume.
2) Final concentration = 150 mmol in 1150 mL = 130 mmol/L
3) Administer the indicated dose via pump (volume/time) or withdraw the indicated dose and administer via syringe pump.
4) Rinse the tubing with the solution before starting the administration

• Can be diluted in sterile water for injection if patient is hyperglycemic.
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, KCl. Incompatible with LR.
• Multiple drug incompatibilities. Sodium Bicarbonate inactivates vasopressors and catecholaminergic type inotropes (Ex.: Epinephrine, Norepinephrine)
• Indicated only in non-anion-gap metabolic acidosis (NAGMA) or secondary to acute renal failure. Not indicated in lactic acidosis, diabetic ketoacidosis, etc.
• Although the BICAR-ICU study (Jaber 2018) demonstrated a potential benefit with 4.2% sodium bicarbonate (0.5 mmol/mL), this solution is not available in Canada in bigger format than 10 mL. Given the complexity and the risk of error when preparing such a solution, it seems more appropriate to continue to use an isotonic bicarbonate solution as described above when clinically indicated.
• The BICAR-ICU study did not include patients < 18 years old. (Expert opinion)
• May cause hypokalemia and hypocalcemia.
• If intraosseous administration, interference with the blood gas analysis if drawn in the same access.
