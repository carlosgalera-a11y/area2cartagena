# Hypertonic saline 3% (NaCl 3%)
- Drug ID: 198
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Intracranial Hypertension
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer IV undiluted
      concentration note: Premixed commercial bag (250 mL)
      administration: Additionnal information:
• Administer preferably via proximal peripheral venous access if central access not available (hyperosmolar solution)
• Rinse the tubing with the solution before administration
• If osmotherapy is indicated to treat intracranial hypertension, favor the use of NaCl 3% as first line. Repeat the dose after 20 minutes as needed until a natremia of 155 mmol/L is reached. Increased risk of haematological adverse effects with serum sodium > 160 mmol/L for 72 hours or more.
• Administration by continuous infusion is no longer recommended in the latest guidelines for the management of pediatric severe traumatic brain injury

## Indicación: Severe hyponatremia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer IV undiluted on volume/time pump
      concentration note: Premixed commercial bag
      administration: Administer preferably via proximal peripheral venous access if central access not available (hyperosmolar solution)
Rinse the tubing with the solution before administration
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR. (Expert advice)

Administer in case of severe hyponatraemia with symptoms (seizures, altered level of consciousness, confusion, vomiting, tremors, movement disorders, etc.)

If there is no clinical improvement 5-10 minutes after administration, dose can be repeated. The biochemical objective is to increase serum sodium by 4-6 mmol/L in the first hours of treatment.

Monitor serum sodium closely thereafter, according to usual guidelines and with specialist consultation if necessary. Serum sodium should not increase by more than 6-8 mmol/L per 24 hour period. Risk factors for osmotic demyelination syndrome: serum Na <105 mmol/L at presentation, chronic/subacute presentation, hypokalemia, malnutrition, advanced liver failure or alcoholism (or history of).

If possible, before administration of 3% saline, measure serum and urine electrolytes/osmolarity. 
 
To avoid iatrogenic hyponatremia secondary to maintenance fluids in pediatrics, it is recommended to avoid hypotonic solutions such as NaCl 0.45% and to use isotonic solutions such as NaCl 0.9% or, preferably, Lactate Ringer.
