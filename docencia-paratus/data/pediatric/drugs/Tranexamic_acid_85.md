# Tranexamic acid (Cyklokapron®)
- Drug ID: 85
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Hemorrhage (miscellaneous/non traumatic)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 5 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 10 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Do not administer via the same IV access as blood products.
      particularity: • Consider for patients with post-thrombolytic hemorrhage, perioperative bleeding, dental treatments in a patients with coagulopathy, hemoptysis or uterine bleeding, but also for patients with traumatic bleeding (see separate entries for trauma).
• Consider topical administration when possible (e.g. epistaxis, dental treatments).
• Consider nebulization in patients with hemoptysis (see separate entry).
• Tranexamic acid is not recommended for clinically significant upper gastrointestinal bleeding.

## Indicación: Angioedema, bradykinin-mediated (treatment)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 5 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 10 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: Administration in 5 minutes is safe and keeps IV access available in case of emergency.
      particularity: • Therapies for angioedema should never delay securing the airway.
• The efficacy of tranexamic acid remains controversial in angioedema because of the lack of definitive studies. However, tranexamic acid is safe, widely available and easy to administer which makes it a therapy to consider for bradykinin-mediated angioedema.
• For hereditary angioedema cases, administer 1st line agents when possible (e.g. C1-esterase inhibitor, icatibant).

## Indicación: Nebulization for hemoptysis/post-tonsillectomy hemorrhage
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer by nebulizer undiluted
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: • Do not dilute for faster administration.
• Consider using a mouth piece rather than a mask to better direct the nebulization in non intubated patients.
• Can be instilled directly into the endotracheal tube followed by 1-2 mL (< 10 Kg) or 2-5 mL (≥ 10 Kg) of NaCl 0.9% in emergency situations in already intubated patients. 
• In intubated infants, aspiration of clots post-hemoptysis may be difficult and require additional manipulation secondary to the small size of the endotracheal tube. Consider consulting a pediatric airway specialist promptly.
      particularity: Can be repeated every 6-8 hours as needed.

## Indicación: Hemorrhage associated with trauma (single bolus without drip)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Single bolus < 12 y/o  [IV over 5 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 10 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      administration: • Do not administer via the same IV access as blood products. 
• Administration rate faster than 100 mg/min is commonly used in practice in trauma and other indications. (Expert opinion)
• According to some studies and pediatric protocols from hospital centers specialized in traumatology, a bolus of 30 mg/Kg or 2 g alone (without infusion) could be equivalent to the standard method of administration (bolus + infusion).
      particularity: • Should be given within three hours of trauma, otherwise less effective and possibly associated with adverse effects.
• Benefits not demonstrated in isolated severe head trauma
