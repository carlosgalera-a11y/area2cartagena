# Phenylephrine (NeoSynephrine®)
- Drug ID: 185
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: TET spells in Tetralogy of Fallot
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 100 mcg/mL  [IV push q3min PRN]  Administer IV DILUTED
      concentration: 100 mcg/mL
      administration: Caution:
Pediatric Critical Care or Pediatric Cardiology opinion recommended

Presentation: Vial of Phenylephrine 10 mg/mL (10 mg in 1 mL)
1) Withdraw 1 mL (1 vial) of Phenylephrine 10 mg/mL with a 1 or 3 mL syringe
2) Add to a 100 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% WITHOUT removing the bulk
2) Final concentration = 10 mg in 101 mL ≈ 0.1 mg/mL = 100 mcg/mL
3) Draw the required dose using a syringe
4) Clearly identify syringes with the correct concentration
5) Administer IV push q3min PRN to a target O2 saturation

Additional information:
• Use after the fluid bolus in the patient with hypoxic Tetralogy of Fallot.
• Repeat the dose q3min PRN until target O2 saturations are reached or total dose of 20 mcg/Kg. (Consider IV infusion of phenylephrine if partial or temporary bolus response.)
• Systemic vasoconstriction reduces the right to left shunt and improves pulmonary blood flow in Tetralogy of Fallot.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR. 

Although the preferred route of administration of vasopressors is via central venous access, multiple studies demonstrate that administration via a good quality peripheral access is safe when used for a short period of time with frequent verifications of the access site.

## Indicación: Shock
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Infusion (Pump)
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: Caution:
• Opinion of Pediatric Intensive Care recommended
• Not recommended as a 1st line agent in all types of pediatric shock

Preferred concentration in children under 5 Kg = 20 mcg/mL (2 mg/100 mL) and 5 Kg or more = 100 mcg/mL (10 mg/100 mL). Use the lowest concentration if a peripheral venous access is used.

Suggested titration:
1) Start infusion at 0.1 mcg/kg/min.
2) Titrate by increments of 0.1 mcg/kg/min q5min until target BP is reached or a maximum dose of 2 mcg/Kg/min ou 200 mcg/min. (Expert Opinion)

Additional information:
• Do NOT remove volume during preparation.
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration.
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR. 
• Maximal reported dose up to 5 mcg/kg/min
• Although the preferred route of administration of vasopressors is via central venous access, multiple studies demonstrate that administration via a good quality peripheral access is safe if used for a short period of time and with frequent verifications of the access site.
