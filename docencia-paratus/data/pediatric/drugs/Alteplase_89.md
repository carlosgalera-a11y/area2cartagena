# Alteplase (Activase® rt-PA)
- Drug ID: 89
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Acute ischemic stroke
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Step 1  [IV over 1 min]  Administer IV undiluted directly from the vial after reconstitution
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      concentration note: Administer 10% of the total dose IV push
      administration: Not indicated in children < 2 years old or if onset of symptoms not defined or > 4.5 hours.
Validate the absence of contraindications with the neurologist before stroke thrombolysis.

Pediatric stroke requires radiological confirmation, either by CT or CT-angiography (normal parenchyma or with minimal ischemic change AND partial or complete occlusion of the corresponding artery) OR by MRI (acute stroke on diffusion sequences) combined with angio-MRI (partial or complete occlusion of the corresponding artery)

Reconstitution:
Presentation: vial of Alteplase 100 mg, 100 mL vial of sterile water for injection  and transfer device
Refer to the monograph for detailed reconstitution
1) Mix the alteplase vial with the diluent with the help of the transfer device provided. DO NOT shake the bottle.
2) Mix the solution with a gentle swirl to make sure the product is completely dissolved.
3) Visually inspect the solution for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration.
4) Concentration after reconstitution = 1 mg/mL

Presentation: vial of Alteplase 50 mg
Refer to the monograph for detailed reconstitution
1) Using a 50 mL syringe and the large needle (18G), take 50 mL of sterile water for injection. 
2) Inject gently the sterile water into the alteplase vial.
3) Mix the solution with a gentle swirl to make sure the product is completely dissolved.
4) Visually inspect the solution for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration.
5) Concentration after reconstitution = 1 mg/mL
      particularity: Incompatible in Y with D5W, LR or any other solutions. Only compatible with NaCl 0.9%.

Contraindications (Non-exhaustive list, see monograph or article by Fugate et al.)

Patient-related:
- Age < 2 years
- Systolic BP >15% of the 99th percentile or > 185 mmHg despite treatment
- Current use of anticoagulants (heparin (< 48 hrs with abnormal aPTT), enoxaparin (< 24 hrs), dalteparin (< 24 hrs), warfarin, dabigatran, rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban)
- INR > 1.7, platelets <100 x 10^9/L, aPTT > 40 sec, PT > 15 sec, fibrinogen < 1 g/L despite treatment
- Active coagulation disorder (e.g. Von Willebrand disease)
- Glycemia < 4 mmol/L or > 22 mmol/L despite treatment
- Neoplasia (< 1 month after completion of treatment)
- Alteplase anaphylaxis

Presentation related:
- Onset of symptoms not defined or > 4.5 hours
- Presentation suggestive of subarachnoid hemorrhage (even if CT-scan without bleeding)
- Stroke related to infectious endocarditis, meningitis, moyamoya, sickle cell anemia, fat or gas embolism
- Presentation suggestive of myocardial infarction or post-infarction pericarditis
- Active internal bleeding

Related to imagery
- Hypodensity > 1/3 cerebral hemisphere on CT-scan
- Intracranial bleeding
- Intracranial dissection (defined as involvement at or distal to the ophthalmic artery)
- Arterial or venous intracerebral malformation, aneurysm or cerebral neoplasia

History-related:
- History of vasculitis or vasculitis of the primary or secondary central nervous system
- In the last 7 days: arterial puncture on a non-compressible site
- In the last 14 days: childbirth, major surgery or biopsy of parenchyma
- In the last 21 days: digestive or urinary bleeding
- In the last 3 months: intracranial or intraspinal surgery, severe head trauma, intracranial hemorrhage, ischemic stroke

## Indicación: Massive Pulmonary Embolism
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer IV undiluted directly from the vial after reconstitution
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: • There is no consensus on the dose of alteplase in pediatric massive pulmonary embolism. Doses vary from 0.1-0.6 mg/Kg/h over 6 hours for systemic thrombolysis with alteplase. The selected dose of 1.2 mg/Kg over 2 hours is the result of a consensus of local experts including intensivists and pediatric hematologists. Consider starting at a reduced dose in infants. For patients who remain symptomatic and hemodynamically unstable after 2 hrs of treatment, consider repeating another 2-hour course.
• Consider administering a bolus of 0,12-0,25 mg/Kg, max 10 mg (10-20% of the total dose) IV push if the patient is very unstable. The rest of the dose can then be administered IV over 2 hours.
• Withhold heparin before beginning alteplase administration. Heparin can be restarted when the aPTT < 40 sec (1.5 times the normal value)

- Within last 3 months: intracranial or intra-spinal surgery, severe head trauma, intracranial hemorrhage, prior ischemic stroke
- Arterial or venous intracerebral malformation, aneurysm or cerebral neoplasia
- Active coagulation disorder (e.g. Von Willebrand disease)
- Suspected aortic dissection
- Alteplase anaphylaxis

- Traumatic CPR
- Internal bleed within 4 weeks prior
- Systolic BP >99th percentile or > 180 mmHg despite treatment
- Major surgery within 3 weeks prior
- Ischemic stroke > 3 months old

## Indicación: Cardiac arrest with high suspicion of pulmonary embolism
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 2 min]  Administer IV undiluted after reconstitution
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      concentration note: If in-hospital cardiac arrest in an ECMO center, validate with Pediatric Intensive Care before administration
      administration: A second dose of 0.6 mg/Kg, max 50 mg can be repeated as needed (about 10-15 minutes later) to complete the total dose at 1.2 mg/Kg, max 100 mg. There is no scientific consensus on the dosage of alteplase in cardiac arrest, particularly in pediatrics. The suggested dose is an editorial decision based on all the available evidence.
