# Naloxone (Narcan®)
- Drug ID: 173
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Opioid Poisoning
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Infusion (< 0.1 mg/h or < 15 Kg)
      concentration: 0.4 mg/mL
      administration: Caution:
The administration of this infusion can represent a considerable volume in the newborn. Consider a concentrated naloxone drip when the administration rate exceeds 50% of maintenance fluids (Expert opinion)

Calculate the infusion dose based on the cumulative bolus dose
1) Calculate the dose corresponding to 2/3 of the cumulative effective bolus dose
2) Start the infusion to administer this calculated dose in 1 hour
3) Stop the infusion according to the clinical response and the half-life of the narcotic

Example:
The patient wakes up after 2 boluses of naloxone: 1st bolus 0.5 mg, 2nd bolus 1 mg
1) Effective cumulative bolus dose = 0.5 +1 = 1.5 mg
2) 2/3 of this dose = 2/3 x 1.5 = 1 mg
3) Start the infusion at 1 mg/h

Additional information:
• Do NOT remove volume during preparation. 
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting the administration
      particularity: • Infusion can also be started empirically at 0.2-0.4 mg/h.
• If respiratory depression reappears, a new loading dose of naloxone should be given and this dose repeated as needed, or continue the infusion.
• The duration of the naloxone infusion should be dictated by the clinical examination, but also by the type of opioid involved, its dose and formulation (progressive release or not).

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV push]
      concentration: 0.04 mg/mL
      administration: DILUTED solution at 0.04 mg/mL
Presentation: Vial of Naloxone 0.4 mg/mL
1) Withdraw 1 mL (1 vial) of Naloxone 0.4 mg/mL with a 10 mL syringe
2) Add to 9 mL of NaCl 0.9% to the syringe
3) Final concentration = 0.4 mg in 10 mL = 0.04 mg/mL
      particularity: Repeat every 2 to 3 min PRN until correction of respiratory depression.
If partial reversal sought or in the case of an opioid-dependent patient, use lower dose initially.
