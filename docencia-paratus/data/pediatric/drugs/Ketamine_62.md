# Ketamine (Ketalar®)
- Drug ID: 62
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Procedural sedation
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: IV  [IV over 1-2 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: Additional information:
• Usual dose for patient > 3 months of life: 1-1.5 mg/Kg
• Repeat dose of 0.5mg/kg q5 minutes as needed.
• Ketamine is not recommended for emergency procedural sedation < 3 months of life due to increased risk of laryngospasm, apnea and airway obstruction. Consider maximizing non-pharmacological measures and local therapies and if the need for IV procedural sedation persists, consider ketamine 0.5 mg/Kg under closer monitoring.

  - STEP: Intranasal  [Intranasal over 1-2 seconds]
      concentration: 50 mg/mL
      administration: • Use 50 mg/mL concentration
• Not recommended for use in patients less than 6 months
• For doses > 100 mg, administer in 2 sequences spaced 1-2 minutes apart.
• Dose of 5-6 mg/Kg for use as monotherapy. Reduce dose if drug combination.
• The combination with a benzodiazepine would not reduce the psychomimetic and emergence effects of ketamine.

Material :
- MAD-300 device
- 1 or 3 mL Luer-Lock syringe

2) Draw the desired dose and add 0.1 mL to fill the dead space
3) Connect the MAD-300 device to the syringe
4) Divide the dose evenly between the 2 nostrils (possible volume of 0.2-1 mL per nostril)
5) Administer in 1-2 seconds
      particularity: Wait 5 minutes for optimal sedative effect.

- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IM in < 10 seconds]  Administer IM undiluted in the thigh

• Reserved for patients who are dangerous to themselves or those around them
• For 30 Kg and more, do not exceed 5 mL per injection site
• For less than 30 Kg, do not exceed 3 mL per injection site

      particularity: • Close monitoring of hemodynamic and respiratory status, especially if administration of other molecules before ketamine.
• Consider using IV or IM benzodiazepines as a first choice, depending on the clinical situation.

- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 1 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: • Preferentially use of the 10 mg/mL concentration
• Ketamine is usually not a first choice for post-intubation sedation and analgesia, but is an alternative in hemodynamically unstable or refractory patients.
• If the patient is hemodynamically unstable or has severe heart failure, administer the bolus over at least 5 minutes.
• According to recent literature, ketamine is not contraindicated in intracranial hypertension and reduces ICP
• Can increase bispectral index (BIS)

## Indicación: Refractory Acute Severe Asthma

- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 1-2 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: Asthma in patients < 9 months of life is very rare.

Suggested titration:
1) Bolus of 0.5-2 mg/Kg
2) Start the infusion at 0.5 mg/Kg/h
3) Additional bolus of 0.5-2 mg/Kg as needed, between each increase in the infusion
4) Increase the infusion by 0.5 mg/Kg/h q15-30min depending on respiratory and neurological status. (Expert opinion)

Additional information:
• Close monitoring of the level of consciousness and respiratory drive if the patient is not intubated.

## Indicación: Refractory Status Epilepticus
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV over 1 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 10 mg/mL
      administration: • Combine the ketamine infusion with another infusion agent. (ex .: midazolam)
• Ketamine is not used in neonatal refractory status epilepticus; consider phenobarbital coma on the recommendation of neonatology and pediatric neurology if patient < 4 weeks of life.

Suggested titration:
1) Bolus of 1-3 mg/Kg
2) Start the infusion at 1 mg/Kg/h
3) Additional bolus of 1-3 mg/Kg between each increase in the infusion
4) Increase the infusion by 1 mg/Kg/h q15-30min until the convulsions stop. (Expert opinion)
      particularity: • Under EEG monitoring
• Have equipment and personnel ready for airway management
• Incompatible with LR. Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl
• The early use in Refractory Status Epilepticus of a lower dose ketamine infusion (0.1-1 mg/Kg/h) could also be effective, but doses between 1-10 mg/Kg/h are more documented. (Expert opinion)
