# Phentolamine (Rogitine®)
- Drug ID: 184
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Extravasation of vasoconstrictor agents
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [Subcutaneous]
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      concentration note: Maximum total dose that may be used
      administration: Presentation : Vial of phentolamine 5 mg/mL
1) Withdraw 2 mL of phentolamine 5 mg/mL with a 10 mL syringe
2) Add to 8 mL of NaCl 0.9% to the syringe
3) Final concentration = 10 mg in 10 mL= 1 mg/mL
4) Remove the infiltrated catheter.
5) Inject 0.5-1 mL at a time into the infiltrated area with a 25 or 27G needle.
6) Do not exceed the maximum total dose.
      particularity: Must be given within 12 hours of extravasation.
Keep refrigerated.

## Indicación: Targeted BP control
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Loading dose  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0.9%
      concentration: 5 mg/mL
      administration: Suggested titration:
1) Repeat the loading dose q5-10min PRN until the target BP is reached or a total cumulative dose of 0.1 mg/Kg or 20 mg.
2) Maximum effect reached in 2 minutes, duration of action 15-30 minutes.
3) Always follow the loading dose with the infusion. (Expert opinion)

Additional information:
Reversible alpha receptor blockade with a half-life of 19 min.
      particularity: Consider the use of this agent for targeted blood pressure control in cases of pheochromocytoma or intoxication with a sympathomimetic (eg: cocaine). In the other indications, it is a 2nd or 3rd line treatment.

Y-site compatible with NaCl, LR. No compatibility data with D5W.
Keep refrigerated.

  - STEP: Infusion (Pump)
      concentration: 5 mg/mL
      administration: Suggested titration:
1) Start infusion at 0.5-1 mcg/Kg/min
2) Increase by 0.5-1 mcg/Kg/min q15min PRN until target BP is reached.
3) Maximum dose 6 mcg/Kg/min or 666 mcg/min (40 mg/h). (Expert opinion)

Additional information:
• Reversible alpha receptor blockade with a half-life of 19 min.
• Do NOT remove volume during preparation.
• Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration.
• There are no studies on the stability of the chosen dilution, but other dilutions (10 mg in 500 mL) result in excessive high flow rates and fluid intakes. Dilution recommended based on reported cases and historical use at CHU Sainte-Justine and SickKids Hospital in Toronto. Use immediately after preparation and discard after 24 hours.
