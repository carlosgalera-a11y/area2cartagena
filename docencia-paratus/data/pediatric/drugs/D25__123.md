## Indicación: Adjuvant to high dose IV insulin in toxicology
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  Administer IV DILUTED on volume/time pump
      concentration: 0.25 g/mL
      administration: Contact your poison control center

D25% should only be used via central access, particularly for the infusion. In the absence of central venous access, use D10%. Considering the significant flow rate of D10%, D25% via central access should be used as quickly as possible (smaller flow rate).

Further dilution for final concentration of D25% (prediluted D25% not available commercially):
Presentation : Vial or syringe of D50%
1) Add 50 mL of D50% to a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% (do NOT remove volume)
2) Final concentration = D25%-NaCl 0.45%
3) Draw the required dose and administer IV over 5 min

• Futher dilution with a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% is used to simplify and reduce delays of preparation. If a D25% solution without NaCl is indicated (clinically unlikely), a dilution with sterile water for injection should be used instead of NaCl 0.9%.
• Start only if blood sugar levels below 22 mmol/L and aim for blood sugar levels above 5.5 mmol/L.
• In the hemodynamically unstable patient, low blood sugar should be treated by increasing the dextrose intake and not by decreasing the insulin dose.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR and KCl.

3) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting the administration

• Futher dilution with a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% is used to simplify and reduce delays of preparation. If a D25% solution without NaCl is indicated (clinically unlikely), a dilution with sterile water for injection should be used instead of NaCl 0.9%.
• Because of high flow rates, average patients will need a diluted bag of D25% approximately every 45-60 minutes. It is therefore preferable to have D25% prepared by pharmacy. If this is not possible, another option is to prepare a 250 or 500 mL bag of D25% with the above dilution.
• For older children with high flow rates, consider having a more concentrated D50% solution prepared by pharmacy. If this is not possible, another option is to inject several vials of D50% into an empty bag and infuse at the same rate. (See EZResus Adult)

## Indicación: Adjuvant to IV insulin in hyperkalemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer IV DILUTED on volume/time pump
      concentration: 0.25 g/mL
      administration: Further dilution for final concentration of D25% (prediluted D25% not available commercially):
Presentation : Vial or syringe of D50%
1) Add 50 mL of D50% to a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% (do NOT remove volume)
2) Final concentration = D25%-NaCl 0.45%
3) Draw the required dose and administer IV over 5 min

• Futher dilution with a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% is used to simplify and reduce delays of preparation. If a D25% solution without NaCl is indicated (clinically unlikely), a dilution with sterile water for injection should be used instead of NaCl 0.9%.
• It is possible to mix regular insulin and D25% before administration in paediatrics
• Ratio of 0.1 unit of regular insulin to 0.5 g of dextrose to avoid hypoglycemia
