# Epoprostenol (Flolan® or Caripul®)
- Drug ID: 301
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Pulmonary hypertension (nebulization)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [Nebulization every 4 hours as needed]  Administer by nebulizer diluted up to 2-5 mL of NaCl 0.9% (Caripul®) or SPECIAL diluent for Flolan®
      concentration: 50 mcg/mL
      administration: • Study demonstrates ineffectiveness if administered with standard (jet) nebulizer vs mesh nebulizer.
• In a pulmonary hypertension crisis in patients intubated before cardiac arrest, milrinone sould be use over epoprostenol in those situation because of its effect on the right ventricule. Milrinone can be administered directly into the endotracheal tube followed by a flush. (Expert opinion) 
• Administrer at a minimal O2 flow rate of 1 L/min
• Deposition with repeated use secondary to the glycine contained in the product; check and change the filter more frequently.
• The pediatric dose of 1 mcg/Kg q4h is the dose used in practice, despite the paucity of data in the literature, because administration by continuous nebulization causes too much glycine deposits and risks obstructing the filter or the endotracheal tube. This dose, on the other hand, corresponds to a low to moderate dose in continuous nebulization and administration q4h is demonstrated in adults. (Expert opinion)

Reconstitution:
Presentation: Vial of Epoprostenol 0.5 mg
1) Add 10 mL of special diluent for Flolan® or 10 mL of sterile water or NaCl 0.9% for Caripul® 
2) Concentration after reconstitution = 50 mcg/mL

Presentation: Vial of Epoprostenol 1.5 mg 
1) Add 10 mL of special diluent for Flolan® or 10 mL of sterile water or NaCl 0.9% for Caripul® 
2) Concentration after reconstitution = 150 mcg/mL
      particularity: • NO remains the treatment of choice for pulmonary hypertension in pediatrics; prioritize this treatment if available.
• Avoid if pulmonary bleed
• Despite significant systemic absorption, inhaled epoprostenol causes less vasodilator effect than continuous IV infusion, according to studies combining pharmacokinetics and cardiac ultrasound measurements.
• Inhaled epoprostenol may be combined with inhaled milrinone treatment; alternating administration every 2 hours is recommended in this situation.
