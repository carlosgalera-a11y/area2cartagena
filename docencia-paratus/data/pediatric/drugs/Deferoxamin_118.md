# Deferoxamin (Desferal®)
- Drug ID: 118
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Iron intoxication
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: 
      concentration: 94 mg/mL
      administration: DILUTED solution at 19,8 mg/mL:

Reconstitution:
Presentation: Vial of Deferoxamine 500 mg
1) Add 5 mL of sterile water
2) Concentration after reconstitution = 94 mg/mL

Presentation: Vial of Deferoxamine 2000 mg
1) Add 20 mL of sterile water
2) Concentration after reconstitution = 95 mg/mL

Further dilution for administration via pump:
1) Draw 2000 mg of DILUTED Deferoxamine (4 vials of 500mg or 1 vial of 2000mg)
2) Withdraw 20 mL from a 100 mL bag of NaCl 0.9%
3) Add the DILUTED Deferoxamine to the 100 mL bag of NaCl 0.9%
4) Final concentration after further dilution = 19,8 mg/mL

Suggested titration:
• Start the infusion at 15 mg/kg/h.
• In the event of severe poisoning and in discussion with your poison control centre, the flow rate can be increased by 15-20 mg/kg/h every 15 minutes, depending on haemodynamic tolerance. Maximum dose of 50 mg/kg/h.
• Usual maximum dose of 80 mg/kg per 24 hours recommended by the manufacturer. However, it is possible that a higher dose than this will be administered following consultation with your poison control centre.

Additional informations:
• If the IV route is not available, the IM route can be used: 90 mg/kg initially, then 45 mg/kg per dose (max 1 g/dose) every 8h. For IM administration, reconstitute the 500 mg and 2 g vials with 2 and 8 mL of sterile water, respectively, to obtain a final concentration of 213 mg/mL.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with NaCl, D5W, LR.

Indications:
- Ingestion of 40 mg/kg or more of elemental iron with symptoms of toxicity.
- Serum concentration greater than 90 mcmol/L (500 mcg/dL) in acute intoxication.
- Serum concentration greater than 63 mcmol/L (350 mcg/dL) and cardiovascular symptoms or lactic acidosis.

If the appearance of adverse effects, such as hypotension, limits the titration of the infusion, consider the administration of vasopressors, especially for cases of poisoning requiring a maximum dose (50 mg/kg/h) of deferoxamine.
