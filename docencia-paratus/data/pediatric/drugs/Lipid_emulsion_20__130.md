# Lipid emulsion 20% (Intralipid®)
- Drug ID: 130
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Local anesthetic systemic toxicity
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   Administer IV undiluted
      administration: If cardiovascular collapse (bradycardia, asystole, ventricular arrhythmias) secondary to the administration of a local anesthetic (bupivacaine, lidocaine, ropivacaine), when refractory to first-line treatments.

The loading dose can be repeated every 5 minutes (max 3 total doses) if there is no clinical response.
Administer centrally or peripherally
Filter not necessary during administration

Consult your poison control center if:
- No response to boluses
- Altered state of consciousness with or without convulsions secondary to the administration of a local anesthetic
- Cardiac arrest or shock refractory to other therapeutic modalities secondary to intoxication with a sodium channel blocker or a fat-soluble drug. (To be considered only if all usual therapeutic modalities have failed.)

The administration of a lipid emulsion infusion is not supported by the literature.
      particularity: Even if contraindicated by the monograph if allergy to eggs, soy or peanuts, the IV lipid emulsion does not cause cross-allergy or anaphylaxis according to the literature.

The majority of studies on lipid emulsion in intoxication have been done with Intralipid. The different lipid emulsions are not interchangeable in toxicology.

Administration of lipid emulsion may interfere with laboratory tests for several hours and cause occlusion of the ECMO or hemodialysis circuit.

Absolute maximum dose of 10-12 mL/Kg/24h
