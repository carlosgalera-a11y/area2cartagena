# Octreotide (Sandostatin®)
- Drug ID: 178
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Sulfonylurea intoxication
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: IV  [IV over 3 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 50 mcg/mL
      administration: • Favor the subcutaneous route.
• Consider administering IV if severe intoxication or peripheral hypoperfusion. 
• IV administration over 3 min may cause bradycardia; consider administering over 15 min in 50 mL of NaCl 0.9% or bradycardia supportive therapy for patients at risk. (Consider that the use of a bag of 50 mL can represent a significant amount of volume depending on the clinical situation.)
• Do NOT confuse with the long-acting or depot formulation (LAR)
      particularity: • Administer in combination with dextrose.
• Contact your poison control center 
• Repeat the dose q4h if necessary (expert opinion: shorten interval to q4h when the IV route is used).
• Keep refrigerated and protect from light. Stable for 14 days at room temperature.

## Indicación: Esophageal variceal bleed
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  Administer IV DILUTED
      concentration: 4.95 mcg/mL
      administration: Additional information:
The DILUTED solution between 4.5-5 mcg/mL allows the administration of boluses and continuous infusion via the same solution. The volume provided by multiple boluses is adequate even for fluid-restricted patients.

Caution: multiple concentrations available 
Further dilution for administration via pump:

DILUTED 4.95 mcg/mL solution: 
Presentation: Vial of Octreotide 500 mcg/mL (500 mcg in 1 mL)
1) Use a 100 mL bag of NaCl 0.9%
2) Add 1 mL (500 mcg) of Octreotide 500 mcg/mL to the bag, WITHOUT removing volume
3) Final concentration = 500 mcg in 101 mL = 4.95 mccg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting the administration
5) For a dose of 1-2 mcg/Kg, administer 0.2-0.4 mL/Kg (max 10.1 mL) of the solution diluted to 4.95 mcg/mL over 3 min

OR

 DILUTED 4.88 mcg/mL solution: 
Presentation: Vial of Octreotide 200 mcg/mL (200 mcg in 1 mL)
1) Use a 100 mL bag of NaCl 0.9%
2) Add 2.5 mL (500 mcg) of Octreotide 500 mcg/mL to the bag, WITHOUT removing volume
3) Final concentration = 500 mcg in 102.5 mL = 4.88 mcg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting the administration
5) For a dose of 1-2 mcg/Kg, administer 0.2-0.4 mL/Kg (max 10.2 mL) of the solution diluted to 4.88 mcg/mL over 3 min

OR

DILUTED 4.76 mcg/mL solution: 
Presentation: Vial of Octreotide 100 mcg/mL (100 mcg in 1 mL)
1) Use a 100 mL bag of NaCl 0.9%
2) Add 5 mL (500 mcg) of Octreotide 500 mcg/mL to the bag, WITHOUT removing volume

5) For a dose of 1-2 mcg/Kg, administer 0.21-0.42 mL/Kg (max 10.5 mL) of the solution diluted to 4.76 mg/mL over 3 min 

OR

 DILUTED 4.55 mcg/mL solution: 
Presentation: Vial of Octreotide 50 mcg/mL (50 mcg in 1 mL)
1) Use a 100 mL bag of NaCl 0.9%
2) Add 10 mL (500 mcg) of Octreotide 500 mcg/mL to the bag, WITHOUT removing volume
3) Final concentration = 500 mcg in 110 mL = 4.55 mcg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting the administration
5) For a dose of 1-2 mcg/Kg, administer 0.22-0.44 mL/Kg (max 11 mL) of the solution diluted to 4.55 mcg/mL over 3 min
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR. Incompatible with IV pantoprazole (no data)
• Keep refrigerated
• Monitor blood sugar and adjust sugar intake as needed, especially in infants.
