# Potassium chloride (KCl)
- Drug ID: 281
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Severe hypokalemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Peripheral or central line  Administer IV slowly over 1 hour
      concentration: 0.1 mmol/mL
      administration: • Use local protocols for repletion with concentrated KCl if available.
• Premixed commercial bag at 10 mmol/100 mL (0.1 mmol/mL)
• Administer under cardiac monitoring if dose > 0.5 mmol/Kg (max 20 mmol) or speed of administration > 0.5 mmol/Kg/h (max 20 mmol/h)
• Reduce rate or administer in the same IV access than maintenance in case of irritation at the infusion site. 
• Check serum potassium 1-2 hours after the bolus.
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5%, NaCl, LR and MgSO4.
• 1 mmol KCl = 1 mEq KCl 
• Severe hypokalemia is usually defined as a serum potassium < 3 mmol/L or < 3.5 mmol/L with symptoms or ECG changes
• Precaution in renal failure; consider the lower dose of 0.5 mmol/Kg (max 10 mmol).
• In severe hypokalemia, intracellular potassium deficit can be up to 40-80 mmol/Kg.
• Consider concomitant PO administration as higher doses can be given by the PO route. 
• Consider serum magnesium measurement and/or magnesium repletion.
