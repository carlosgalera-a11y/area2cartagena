# Calcium (Chloride) (CaCl₂)
- Drug ID: 99
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Hyperkalemia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 2-5 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      concentration note: Dose expressed in mg of Calcium Chloride
      administration: Additional information:
• IV push in cardiac arrest
• Administer via central venous access (risk of skin necrosis with extravasation)
• Incompatible with Bicarbonate. Rinse the IV line thoroughly between administrations to avoid precipitation and loss of the venous access
• The use of calcium gluconate is preferred in a patient without central venous access
• Administer over 15-30 minutes in a stable patient
• In doubt, control the venous K+ level and look for anomalies on the 12-lead EKG
      particularity: In elemental calcium equivalents, 1 g of calcium chloride = 3 g calcium gluconate

## Indicación: Hemorrhagic shock
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 2-5 min]  Administer IV undiluted or diluted up to 5 mL of NaCl 0,9%
      concentration: 100 mg/mL
      concentration note: Dose expressed in mg of Calcium Chloride
      administration: Additional information:
• IV push in cardiac arrest
• Administer via central venous access (risk of skin necrosis with extravasation)
• Do not administer via the same IV access as blood products
• Consider administering a 1st bolus of calcium after the first transfusion of red blood cells or fresh frozen plasma and then after every 2-4 transfusions.
• Administer over 15-30 minutes in a stable patient

## Indicación: Hypocalcemia, severe
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 5 min]  Administer IV DILUTED
      concentration: 20 mg/mL
      concentration note: Dose expressed in mg of Calcium Chloride
      administration: • Warning: in the absence of a central or IO access, use Calcium Gluconate

DILUTED solution at 20 mg/mL

Further dilution for administration via syringe pump:
Presentation: Pre-filled syringe of CaCl2 10% (100 mg/mL) (1000 mg in 10 mL)
1) Withdraw 10 mL of CaCl2 100 mg/mL and transfer to a 50 mL syringe
2) Add 40 mL of NaCl 0.9% to the syringe
3) Final concentration = 1000 mg in 50 mL = 20 mg/mL
4) Withdraw the dose from the DILUTED 20 mg/mL solution and administer over 5 min.

• Consider treatment when ionized/measured serum calcium < 0.9 mmol/L with clinical signs of hypocalcemia such as Chvostek, Trousseau or Erb, signs of prolonged depolarization on the ECG (prolonged QT or AV block), impairment of cardiac function due to lack of contractility, convulsions, papilloedema or psychiatric symptoms. 
• Total blood calcium is a poor marker of hypocalcemia. (If ionized/measured serum calcium is not available, a total calcium < 1.65 mmol/L is closer to 0.9 mmol/L ionized/measured than a total calcium < 2.1 mmol/L.)
• IV push if cardiac arrest secondary to hypocalcemia, hypermagnesemia or hyperkalemia; systematic use in cardiac arrest is no longer recommended as it diminishes the incidence of return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC).
• Administer over 30-60 minutes in a stable patient
      particularity: • Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl. Incompatible with LR or Ceftriaxone
• Increased risk of hypocalcemia in patients undergoing extrarenal purification with Na citrate (intermittent or continuous hemodialysis, plasmapheresis), transfusions of packed red blood cells or fresh plasma, chronic renal failure, post-op thyroidectomy, premature or newborn without vitamin D supplementation, biphosphonate treatment or suspicion of DiGeorge (22q11, often accompanied by congenital heart disease)
• In case of concomitant hypocalcemia and hyperphosphatemia, giving calcium may cause precipitation of calcium phosphate (calciphylaxis). The ideal treatment in this situation is treatment of the hyperphosphatemia.
• In elemental calcium equivalents, 1 g of calcium chloride = 3 g calcium gluconate.
