# Salbutamol (Ventolin®)
- Drug ID: 197
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Acute severe asthma
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Inhalation  [q20min x 3 doses]
      concentration: 100 mcg/inhalation
      administration: • Asthma in patients < 9 months of life is very rare
• Ineffective in bronchiolitis

Additional information:
• Metered-dose inhaler (MDI) with an Aerochamber® is the preferred device for salbutamol administration because it is more efficient than a nebulizer for bronchodilator delivery.
• Use Aerochamber® for optimal administration
• If the patient is on CPAP or BiPAP, favor nebulized administration to allow the patient to retain the benefits of PEEP
• Administer q30-60min if clinical improvement after 3 treatments q20min

## Indicación: Acute severe asthma (continuous nebulization)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: With 2 mg/mL (< 20 Kg)
      concentration: 2 mg/mL
      administration: Caution:
• Use only if patient has good air entry during intermittent treatments, but desaturates between treatments q20-30min. Continuous nebulization is not superior to intermittent q20min treatments. In case of deterioration without improvement with q20min treatments, switch to continuous IV salbutamol.
• Fixed rate at 10 mL/h for optimal nebulization with all types of nebulizers.
• By using the salbutamol solution for nebulization at 2 mg/ml, with a fixed flow rate of 10 ml/h, the dose administered is slightly higher than the doses usually seen in practice/recommended by the CSP (10 mg/h instead of 7.5 mg/h). This dose nevertheless corresponds to 0.5-1 mg/kg/h reported in the literature and ensures the feasibility of a simple dilution with the equipment usually available (Expert opinion) 
• Administrer at a minimal O2 flow rate of 1 L/min, adjust the oxygen flow preventing the nebulizer from emptying or overflowing. 

Further dilution for administration via pump if syringe pump not available:
1) Remove 25 mL from a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% = 25 mL
2) Add 25 mL (50 mg) of Salbutamol 2 mg/mL to the bag
3) Final concentration = 50 mg in 50 mL = 1 mg/mL
4) Infuse directly into the nebulizer at 10 mL/h = 10 mg/h
5) Administer a 2 mL bolus into the nebulizer before connecting oxygen. 

OR

For administration via pump if 2 mg/mL solution not available :
1) Remove 50 mL from a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% = 0 mL or use an empty IV bag
2) Add 50 mL (50 mg) of Salbutamol 1 mg/mL to the bag
3) Final concentration = 50 mg in 50 mL = 1 mg/mL
4) Infuse directly into the nebulizer at 10 mL/h = 10 mg/h
5) Administer a 2 mL bolus into the nebulizer before connecting oxygen.

## Indicación: Acute severe asthma (IV infusion)
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Infusion (Pump)
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Asthma in patients < 9 months of life is very rare.

Rince tubing with the infusion before administration 

If 25 mL bag not available:
1) Remove 25 mL from a 50 mL bag
2) Add the indicated dose 

Additional information:
• Maximum documented adult dose = 80 mcg/min; with the help of a pediatric resuscitation expert, consider ketamine IV, epinephrine IV infusion, or supra-therapeutic doses of salbutamol if patient still symptomatic with 80 mcg/min.
• Concentrated infusions > 10 mcg/mL are from Australian asthma guidelines and are commonly used in pediatrics.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR.

When using IV salbutamol, an increase in lactates without acidosis (hyperlactatemia type B) is not associated with signs of hypoperfusion. An increase in troponins may also be observed, but is not associated with cardiac dysfunction. Instead, favor monitoring via ECG if necessary in the presence of significant tachycardia.
