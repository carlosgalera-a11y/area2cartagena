# Vitamin K (Phytonadione)
- Drug ID: 205
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Life-threatening bleeding with use of vitamin K antagonist
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Pump
      concentration: 2 mg/mL
      administration: • Administration of IV vitamin K with the current injectable formulation (without PEO-CO or cremophor) is safe and doesn't cause life-threatening reactions.
• The use of subcutaneous vitamin K is not recommended for bleeding secondary to vitamin K antagonists because its absorption is erratic and unpredictable. 
• Maximum rate of 1 mg/min to decrease the risk of non-IgE mediated anaphylactic reaction. 

If 25 mL bag not available:
1) Remove 25 mL from a 50 mL bag
2) Add the indicated dose

Consider that the use of a bag of 25-50 mL can represent a significant amount of volume depending on the clinical situation.
      particularity: • Onset of action (IV): 6 hours
• Use in combination with Prothrombin complex concentrate (4-factor) or fresh frozen plasma (if 4F-PCC not available)
• The dose of 5 or 10 mg IV is preferred to the dose of 0.03 mg/Kg for life-threatening bleeding, because the dose of vitamin K antagonist to be neutralized is adjusted according to the INR rather than the weight. (Expert opinions)
