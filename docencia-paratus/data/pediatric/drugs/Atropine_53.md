# Atropine ({"fr":null})
- Drug ID: 53
- Type: drug
- Entorno: pediatric

## Indicación: Bradycardia
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV push]  Administer IV undiluted
      concentration: 0.1 mg/mL
      concentration note: Different concentrations available
      administration: Slow injection may cause paradoxical bradycardia.
The minimum dose of 0.1 mg is no longer recommended.

PALS 2020 recommends atropine for the treatment of bradycardia with hemodynamic instability secondary to vagal stimulation, primary AV block excluding high-grade AV block, or intoxication. There is no evidence that atropine treats bradycardia from other causes.
      particularity: If high risk of bradycardia secondary to catecholamine depletion (ex.: patient in shock), treat with vasopressor agents (Epinephrine as a push dose pressor or continuous drip) rather than with atropine.

If patient requiring repeated neurological examination (ex.: head trauma patient), treat with vasopressor agents (epinephrine as a rapid bolus vasopressor or continuous infusion) rather than with atropine. Atropine causes loss of pupillary assessment (mydriasis) lasting 30-45 minutes.

- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV push]  Administer IV undiluted
      concentration: 0.1 mg/mL
      concentration note: Different concentrations available

The minimum dose of 0.1 mg is no longer recommended.

## Indicación: Organophosphate poisoning
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Bolus  [IV push]  Administer IV undiluted
      concentration: 0.1 mg/mL
      concentration note: Different concentrations available
      administration: • Double the dose every 5 minutes until muscarinic signs resolve (bronchorrhea, bradycardia, hypotension). In the presence of muscarinic symptoms, administer atropine even if the patient is tachycardic.
• Consider starting an atropine infusion. 
• Slow injection may cause paradoxical bradycardia.
• The minimum dose of 0.1 mg is no longer recommended.
      particularity: There is no maximum cumulative dose of atropine in the treatment of organophosphate toxicity.
Tachycardia is not a contraindication to administration.

  - STEP: Drip (Pump)
      concentration: 0.1 mg/mL
      administration: Calculate the infusion dose based on the cumulative bolus dose
1) Calculate the dose corresponding to 10 to 20% of the effective cumulative bolus dose (max initial dose 3 mg/h)
2)  Start the infusion to administer this calculated dose over 1 hour
3) Adjust perfusion based on signs of under- or over-atropinization
4) Atropinization is defined as: resolution of bronchorrhea + bradycardia + hypotension + dry axillae
 
Example:
The patient is atropinized after 3 boluses: 1st bolus 0.5 mg, 2nd bolus 1 mg, 3rd bolus 2 mg
1) Cumulative effective bolus dose = 1+2+3 = 6 mg
2) 10 to 20% of this dose = 0.6-1.2 mg
3) Start the infusion at 0.6-1.2 mg/h
 
Caution: multiple concentrations available.
Dilute dose in NaCl 0.9% to achieve a final concentration of 0.1 mg/mL, administer using a volumetric pump. 

DILUTED 0.1 mg/mL solution:

Presentation: Syringe of Atropine 0.1 mg/mL (5 mL or 10 mL).
1) Use an empty 50 mL or 100 mL bag.
2) Add 50 mL (5 mg) of Atropine 0.1 mg/mL to the bag.
3) Final concentration = 0.1 mg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting administration
 
Presentation: Syringe of Atropine 0.2 mg/mL (1 mg in 5 mL).
1) Withdraw 25 mL from a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% = 25 mL
2) Add 25 mL (5 mg) of Atropine 0.2 mg/mL to the bag
3) Final concentration = 5 mg in 50 mL = 0.1 mg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting administration

Presentation: Vial of Atropine 0.4 mg/mL (0.4 mg in 1 mL).
1) Withdraw 12.5 mL from a 50 mL bag of NaCl 0.9% = 37.5 mL
2) Add 12.5 mL (5 mg) of Atropine 0.4 mg/mL to the bag
3) Final concentration = 5 mg in 50 mL = 0.1 mg/mL
4) Rinse the tubing with the infusion before starting administration
