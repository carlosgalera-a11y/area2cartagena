# Sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO3)
- Drug ID: 96
- Type: drug
- Entorno: neonatal

## Indicación: Hyperkalemia with acidosis
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Hemodynamic instability  [IV over 2-5 min]  Administer IV undiluted
      concentration: 0.5 mmol/mL
      administration: IV push in cardiac arrest

If the patient is hemodynamically stable, administer diluted over 30 minutes to reduce the risk of cerebral hemorrhage.

Administer preferably via proximal peripheral venous access if central access not available (hyperosmolar solution).

Hyperkaliemia in neonatology is defined as K+> 6.5-7.5 mmol/L. Neonates often tolerate blood levels of K+ higher than children and adults. In doubt, control the K+ level and look for anomalies on the 12-lead EKG.

If Sodium Bicarbonate 4.2% (0.5mmol/mL) is unavailable:
Presentation : Vial or syringe of Sodium Bicarbonate 8.4% (1 mmol/mL)
1) Withdraw 5 mL of Bicarbonate 1 mmol/mL with a 10 mL syringe
2) Add to 5 mL of Sterile water for injection or D5W to the syringe
3) Final concentration = 5 mmol in 10 mL = 0.5 mmol/mL = 4.2%
      particularity: Incompatible in Y with Calcium or Epinephrine. Rinse well with NaCl 0.9% between administrations

## Indicación: Pulmonary hypertension with acidosis
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP:   [IV over 2-5 min]  Administer IV undiluted
      concentration: 0.5 mmol/mL
      administration: Caution: 
• IV push in cardiac arrest or profound hypoxia due to pulmonary hypertension
• Administer preferably via a proximal peripheral venous access if central access not available (hyperosmolar solution).

If Sodium Bicarbonate 4.2% (0.5mmol/mL) is unavailable:
Presentation : Vial or syringe of Sodium Bicarbonate 8.4% (1 mmol/mL)
1) Withdraw 5 mL of Bicarbonate 1 mmol/mL with a 10 mL syringe
2) Add to 5 mL of Sterile water for injection or D5W to the syringe
3) Final concentration = 5 mmol in 10 mL = 0.5 mmol/mL = 4.2%

Additional information:
Promote the adaptation of ventilatory parameters to adjust the pH in a PAH situation; in hemodynamic instability or if ventilation and oxygenation are maximized, balance the risks of cerebral hemorrhage from bicarbonates with the risks of hypoxia from PAH.
