## Indicación: Shock
- Categoría: Uncategorized
- Weight type: 1

  - STEP: Infusion (Pump)
      concentration: 1 mg/mL
      administration: Preferred concentration in children under 2 Kg = 10 mcg/mL, between 2 and 4 Kg = 20 mcg/mL or over 4 Kg or in fluid restriction = 40 mcg/mL. Use the lowest concentration if a peripheral venous access is used.

In cardiogenic shock, the reported doses are 0.02-0.1 mcg/Kg/min. In distributive septic shock, the reported doses are 0.05-1 mcg/Kg/min. In practice, the addition of the vasoconstrictor effect (alpha) to the inotropic effect (beta) is not precise and absolute.

Suggested titration:
1) Start infusion at 0.05 mcg/kg/min.
2) Titrate by increments of 0.01-0.02 mcg/kg/min q5min depending on clinical response. (Expert Opinion)

Do NOT remove volume during preparation.
Rinse the tubing with the infusion before administration.
Epinephrine can also be diluted in NaCl 0.9% or D10W.
      particularity: Y-site compatible with D5W, NaCl, LR. 
Avoid 1:1000 nomenclature as it is associated with more medication errors.

Although the preferred route of administration of vasopressors is via central venous access, multiple studies demonstrate that administration via a good quality peripheral access is safe if used for a short period of time and with frequent verifications of the access site.
