# Neonatal ventilation device check — Ayre's T-Piece Breathing Circuit

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: neonatal
- Indication ID: 3107


## Tab: Preparation
## Equipment preparation
    
    Recommended device for neonatal resuscitation (pressure stability and lung protection) Allows for: Requires a source of compressed gas Ensure the availability of a self-inflating bag in case of failure of the compressed gas source

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Ensure that all components are securely attached (manometer, tubing, mask) Connect the oxygen tubing to the balloon to a compressed gas source (oxygen flowmeter or air/oxygen mixer) Set the gas flow rate to 10 L/min
## 2
    Obstruct the gas outlet on the mask with the palm of the hand without compressing the balloon
## 3
    Verify that the balloon inflates properly Adjust the flow control valve as needed to prevent overinflation
## 4
    Maintain the mask occluded Adjust the flow control valve so that the pressure observed on the manometer is 5 cmH₂O, without compressing the balloon
## 5
    Maintain mask occlusion and firmly compress the balloon at a rate of 40 to 60 times per minute Ensure that the balloon is adequately inflated between compressions and that the pressure on the manometer reaches 30 to 40 cmH₂O
## 6
    Ensure that the pressure relief valve is active (not blocked), which will limit the ventilation pressure to 40 cmH₂O
