# Intraosseous access — Proximal tibia

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: neonatal
- Indication ID: 2972


## Tab: Preparation
## Indication
    Intravenous access is difficult, impossible or insufficient in the context of a critical condition The manufacturer recommends EZ-IO® use in patients over 3 kg, but successful cases have been reported in newborns as small as 1040 g
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Fracture of the targeted bone Extremity with signs of neurovascular compromise Osteogenesis imperfecta
  ### Relative
    Infection at area of insertion site Prior attempt in the same bone during the current resuscitation (Extravasation risk)
## Anatomy
    Palpable tibial tuberosity
    Tibial tuberosity NOT palpable
    
## Equipment preparation
    Recommended needle
    EZ-IO® Drill
    EZ-Stabilizer® Dressing
    EZ-Connect® Extension Set
    Normal Saline Syringe 10 mL
    Antiseptic Swabstick
    Syringe 10 mL
    Non-sterile gloves

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    Clean, no touch technique with antiseptic solution according to local protocols
## 2
    Palpable tibial tuberosity
    Tibial tuberosity NOT palpable
    
## 3
    
    Flush the EZ-Connect® extension set with 1 mL of sterile saline
## 4
    
    Stabilize the insertion site with your left hand resting on the patient Grasp both borders of the flat aspect of the tibia with your thumb and index
## 5
    
    Insert the needle at a 90 degree angle to bone and apply downpressure until you feel bone without activating the drill Check if you are using the appropriate needle length:
## 6
    Press the driver trigger and apply gentle pressure Release the trigger as soon as you feel a "pop" or a loss of resistance. Avoid recoil and do not pull back on the driver when releasing the trigger
## 7
    Stabilize the needle with your thumb and index already sitting on the patient A stable needle within the bone is your first confirmation of placement
## 8
    
    Disconnect the driver and remove the stylet by twisting counterclockwise
## 9
    
    Cut your dressing for a better fit on smaller limbs Never place circumferentially Apply the EZ-Stabilizer® Dressing
## 10
    
    Connect the primed EZ-Connect® Extension Set to the hub, secure by twisting clockwise Pull the tab off the dressing and apply the adhesive to the skin
    Confirm placement by aspirating bone marrow if not already done
## 11
    Flush 2-5 mL of NaCl 0.9% and look for any evidence of extravasation
## 12
    Administer using a syringe and 3-way stopcock to ensure gentle, controlled pressure Continuously monitor for resistance, swelling, or signs of extravasation

## Tab: Aftercare
## Confirmation of placement
    1) Needle stands on its own within the bone 2) Bone marrow is obtained The incapacity to draw bone marrow doesn't necessarily mean that the IO is not at the right place. Consider aspirating after the flush 3) Flushing of the needle occurs without any evidence of extravasation Resistance to flushing is expected
## Maintenance
    Frequently check for signs of extravasation Remove the IO after 24 hours of use
## Removal
    Do not rock or bend the catheter during removal 1) Remove the dressing and the extension set 2) Attach a luer lock syringe to the hub of the needle 3) Maintain axial alignment 4) Twist the syringe clockwise, while pulling straight out 5) Apply sterile dressing
## Complications
    Extravasation or infiltration of fluids Compartment syndrome Osteomyelitis/Sepsis Fracture of the targeted bone
## Theoretical complications without evidence
    Injury to the growth plate in pediatric patients No growth plate damage has been documented in known medical literature Fat embolism There are no reports of clinically significant fat embolism in the literature
