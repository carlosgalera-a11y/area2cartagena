## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Face parallel to the ceiling or slightly posterior (< 15 degrees), avoid excessive chin lift Consider adding a small roll under the shoulder
## 2
    
    Grasp the handle of the laryngoscope with the left hand Gently open the mouth with the thumb or index finger of the right hand Insert the blade into the center of the mouth
## 3
    Advance along the tongue in small increments until visualization of the epiglottis. Insert the blade tip between the base of the tongue and the epiglottis (vallecula)
## 4
    
    Engage the hyoepiglottic ligament by moving forward and upward Avoid the backward tipping movement
## 5
    Attempt external laryngeal manipulation to improve visualization (see tips) Consider lifting the epiglottis directly with the tip of the blade if the view is unsatisfactory.
## 6
    
    Insert tube on the right side of the mouth and then posteriorly Advance the tube and insert it between the vocal cords Gently remove the stylet
## 7
    In direct visualization, advance the tube to the suggested depth below Remove the laryngoscope
## 8
    
    Connect the ventilation device and the neonatal capnograph to the endotracheal tube Initiate ventilation
## 9
    Confirm proper positioning using a neonatology-appropriate capnograph (EtCO₂) Bilateral lung auscultation Observe chest rise Other indicators
## 10
    Use tape to secure the device to the patient's face Order a chest x-ray
## 11
    Gastric distension can impair ventilation effectiveness Consider placing an orogastric tube after intubation
