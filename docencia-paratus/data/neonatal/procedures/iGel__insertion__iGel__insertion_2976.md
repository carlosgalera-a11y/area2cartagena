# iGel® insertion — iGel® insertion

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Airway & Ventilation
- Entorno: neonatal
- Indication ID: 2976


## Tab: Preparation
## Indication
    Failure of mask ventilation and intubation
## Contraindication
  ### Absolute
    Complete upper airway obstruction
  ### Relative
    Limited mouth opening
## Anatomy
    
## Equipment preparation
    Micropore Paper Tape

## Tab: Execution
## 1
## 2
    Remove the iGel® from its casing Apply a water-based lubricant to the posterior aspect of the tube Do NOT put lubricant on the ventilation port
## 3
    Position the patient's head in a neutral or "sniffing" position if possible
## 4
    Hold iGel® with your dominant hand like a pencil
## 5
    The chin should be gently pressed down before proceeding Introduce the leading soft tip into the mouth of the patient in a direction towards the
    hard palate
## 6
    Insert the device gently, following the natural curvature of the palate, pushing it downward and backward until clear resistance is felt
## 7
    Connect the ventilation device and the neonatal capnograph to the iGel® Initiate ventilation
## 8
    A rising heart rate is a strong physiological marker of adequate ventilation Confirm proper positioning using a neonatal capnograph Bilateral lung auscultation Observe chest rise
## 9
    
    Use tape to secure the device to the patient's face

## Tab: Aftercare
## Maintenance
    Monitor patient's ventilation & oxygenation Continuously monitor the patient's oxygen saturation (SpO₂) and capnography
## Removal
    Remove when the baby starts to breathe effectively spontaneously or to insert an endotracheal tube Suction secretions from the mouth before removal Gently remove the iGel® from the patient's mouth
## Complications
    Device malposition can lead to inadequate ventilation Tissue trauma Laryngospasm Aspiration (rare)
