# Emergency umbilical venous catheter insertion — Emergency umbilical venous catheter insertion

- Tipo: procedure
- Categoría: Circulation & Access
- Entorno: neonatal
- Indication ID: 3046


## Tab: Preparation
## Indications
    Rapid venous access in neonatal resuscitation
## Contraindications
  ### Absolute
    Omphalitis Abdominal wall anomalies (omphalocele, gastroschisis) Peritonitis
  ### Relative
    Necrotizing enterocolitis

## Equipment
    Antiseptic Swabstick
    Sterile gauze 4x4
    Sterile drapes
    Straight forceps
    Iris scissor
    Three-way stop-cock
    Umbilical tape
    3-0 suture
    10mL syringe with normal saline (x2)
    Scalpel

## Tab: Execution
## 1
    
    Put on sterile gloves and prepare the equipment Attempt the most sterile technique possible, taking into account the urgency and the need for rapid vascular access
## 2
    Attach the 3-way stopcock to the catheter Attach the normal saline syringes to the 3-way stopcock Flush the catheter and the stopcock with normal saline Close the stopcock
## 3
    Disinfect the umbilical cord and the peri-umbilical region with an antiseptic solution
## 4
    
    This knot can be tightened to limit bleeding after cutting the cord
## 5
    
    Stabilize the umbilical cord with the forceps below the umbilical clamp Cut the cord at a 90-degree angle with the scalpel 1-2 cm above the skin
## 6
    
    Umbilical vein (1) Umbilical arteries (2)
## 7
    
    Grasp the umbilical vein with forceps and gently lift it upward using your non-dominant hand Insert the catheter 2–4 cm into the umbilical vein with your dominant hand (or using a second pair of forceps if needed) During insertion, an assistant helps confirm proper placement:
## 8
    Keep the non-dominant hand on the catheter to ensure it remains in the correct position. Ask an assistant to administer the medications
